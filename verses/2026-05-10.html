Bhagavad Gita Chapter 5, Verse 7

सanskrit

योगयुक्तो विश‍ुद्धात्मा विजितात्मा जितेन्द्रिय: । सर्वभूतात्मभूतात्मा कुर्वन्नपि न लिप्यते ॥ ७ ॥

Transliteration

yoga-yukto viśuddhātmā vijitātmā jitendriyaḥ sarva-bhūtātma-bhūtātmā kurvann api na lipyate

Translation

One who works in devotion, who is a pure soul, and who controls his mind and senses is dear to everyone, and everyone is dear to him. Though always working, such a man is never entangled.

Purport

One who works in devotion (yoga-yuktaḥ), with a purified heart (viśuddhātmā), who has mastery over the self (vijitātmā) and senses (jitendriyaḥ), and who sees the same Divine Self dwelling in all beings (sarva-bhūtātma-bhūtātmā), remains unstained (na lipyate) by the reactions of work. Just as a lotus leaf remains untouched by water, or a crystal mirror reflects without absorbing the images before it, the karma yogi engages in action while remaining interiorly untouched. Their work is an offering—like pouring oil into a flame, the fuel is consumed but the wick stays pure. By fixing the mind on the Divine and performing duties as service, the false sense of “I am the doer” dissolves. Actions flow, but no karmic residue sticks, because the true actor is the Supreme, acting through the purified instrument of the devotee. Hence, though constantly engaged, they are free from bondage.

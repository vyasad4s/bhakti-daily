🏹 BG 7.1 | 2026-06-02

 

श्रीभगवानुवाच

मय्यासक्तमना: पार्थ योगं युञ्जन्मदाश्रय: ।

असंशयं समग्रं मां यथा ज्ञास्यसि तच्छृणु ॥ १ ॥

 

śrī-bhagavān uvāca
mayy āsakta-manāḥ pārtha yogaṁ yuñjan mad-āśrayaḥ
asaṁśayaṁ samagraṁ māṁ yathā jñāsyasi tac chṛṇu

 

The Supreme Personality of Godhead said: Now hear, O son of Pṛthā, how by practicing yoga in full consciousness of Me, with mind attached to Me, you can know Me in full, free from doubt.

 

A new chapter opens. Not with a question from Arjuna, but with a declaration from Kṛṣṇa. After six chapters of the soul's inward journey — the nature of the self (Ch. 2), the discipline of action (Ch. 3), the transmission of knowledge (Ch. 4), the architecture of renunciation (Ch. 5), the technology of meditation (Ch. 6) — the seventh chapter turns the lens outward. The question shifts from "who am I?" to "who is the One I am attached to?" The method is given in a single verse: mayy āsakta-manāḥ — attach your mind to Me. Yogaṁ yuñjan mad-āśrayaḥ — practice yoga taking shelter in Me. Asaṁśayaṁ samagraṁ mām — you will know Me completely, without doubt. Three movements: attachment, shelter, knowledge. Not three separate paths — one path with three stages that reinforce each other.

 

The word āsakta is precise. It does not mean vague interest, casual curiosity, or intellectual appreciation. It means attached — the mind gripping its object the way a climber grips the rock face. This is not the detachment taught in Chapter 5 — this is the right attachment, the one attachment that does not bind but liberates. Every other attachment (to body, to outcome, to identity, to experience) chains consciousness to prakṛti. This one attachment — mayi, "in Me" — is the rope that pulls you out of the well. The distinction is absolute: attachment to the Supreme is not bondage but the termination of all bondage. It is the one link that, when established, dissolves every other link.

 

Mad-āśrayaḥ — taking shelter of Me. The prefix āśraya means that upon which something rests, its foundation, its ground. Kṛṣṇa is not a goal you achieve through your own effort. He is the ground upon which effort itself stands. This is the radical inversion of spiritual ambition: you do not climb toward the Supreme — you take shelter in the Supreme, and from that position, everything becomes visible. Samagraṁ — in full. Not partial knowledge, not one attribute, not a single dimension of the Absolute. The totality. And asaṁśayaṁ — without doubt. The doubt that paralyzed Arjuna on the battlefield, the doubt that makes spiritual life feel like speculation rather than certainty — Kṛṣṇa promises its termination. The method? Tac chṛṇu — just hear. The simplest instruction in the entire Gītā: listen.

 

🔥 Yoga Sūtra Bridge: YS I.23

 

ईश्वरप्रणिधानाद्वा

īśvara-praṇidhānād vā

Or, [samādhi is attained] from devotion to the Lord.

 

Patañjali's sūtra I.23 introduces a single word that changes everything: vā — "or." After a sequence of methods for attaining samādhi — practice, detachment, inquiry, breath control — he presents devotion as a self-sufficient path. Vyāsa's bhāṣya explains: bhakti-viśeṣa — a particular quality of devotion, the yogi's deep longing, draws Īśvara's grace. The path of devotion does not require the preliminary practices. It stands on its own.

 

Kṛṣṇa's instruction in 7.1 fills in what Patañjali left open. Patañjali says "īśvara" — the Lord, undefined. Kṛṣṇa says "mām" — Me. Patañjali says "praṇidhāna" — devotion, surrender. Kṛṣṇa says āsakta-manāḥ and mad-āśrayaḥ — attach your mind, take shelter. The structure is identical: the method is devotion, the object is the Supreme, the result is the highest attainment. The Yoga Sūtras give the architecture; the Gītā gives the address. One tradition draws the frame, the other fills in the portrait. Both point to the same door: the door that opens when consciousness takes shelter in its source.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

jñāna — Knowledge, specifically transcendental knowledge of the Absolute. Not information — direct cognition of reality as it is. The difference between reading the source code and compiling it in your own consciousness.

āsakta-manāḥ — Mind attached to Me (Kṛṣṇa). Not passive attachment — the deliberate anchoring of consciousness to its source. The mind that has found its proper object and refuses to drift.

mad-āśrayaḥ — In full consciousness of Me; taking shelter of Me. The prefix āśraya means refuge, shelter, that upon which something depends. Kṛṣṇa is not a destination you travel to — He is the ground you stand on.

asaṁśayam — Without doubt, free from uncertainty. The state where no further verification is needed — not because you stopped questioning, but because the question has been answered at the root. The compiler has no remaining syntax errors.

samagram — Completely, in full, as a whole. Not partial knowledge, not one aspect of the Absolute — the totality. The entire codebase, not just one module.

īśvara-praṇidhāna — Devotion, surrender, and offering of all actions to the Lord. Patañjali's term for the bhakti path within yoga. Not belief — the active redirection of every impulse toward its divine source.

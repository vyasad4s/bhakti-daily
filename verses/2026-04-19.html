🏹 BG 4.10 | April 19, 2026

 

वीतरागभयक्रोधा मन्मया मामुपाश्रिताः ।

बहवो ज्ञानतपसा पूता मद्भावमागताः ॥ १० ॥

 

vīta-rāga-bhaya-krodhā
man-mayā mām upāśritāḥ
bahavo jñāna-tapasā
pūtā mad-bhāvam āgatāḥ

 

Being freed from attachment, fear and anger, being fully absorbed in Me, taking refuge in Me, many, many persons in the past became purified by knowledge of Me — and thus they all attained transcendental love for Me.

 

The verse marks the transition from knowledge to love. Jñāna Yoga is not the terminus but the gateway. The purified by knowledge (jñāna-tapasā) do not remain in abstraction; they arrive at mad-bhāvam — the Lord's own state of being, which is prema-bhakti.

 

Vīta-rāga-bhaya-krodhā — freed from the three gates of hell. Rāga (attachment) binds through pleasure. Bhaya (fear) binds through anxiety. Krodha (anger) binds through destruction. These three are the kleśas operationalized: rāga is grasping after pleasant experience, bhaya is aversion to unpleasant, krodha is the defensive reaction. The jñāna-tapasā burns these roots.

 

Man-mayā mām upāśritāḥ — absorbed in Me, taking refuge in Me. This is not intellectual acknowledgment but existential saturation. The mind (manas) becoming mayā — made of Me. The upāśritāḥ — those who have taken shelter — have crossed from jñāna to bhakti. Knowledge becomes love when it reaches the Person.

 

Jñāna-tapasā pūtāḥ — purified by the austerity of knowledge. Tapas here is not outer mortification but inner fire: the heat of discrimination that burns away misidentification. The same heat that destroys rāga-bhaya-krodha purifies the instrument of perception.

 

Today's cosmic portal: Akṣaya Tritiya — the "imperishable third" — where actions bear eternal fruit. Paraśurāma Jayanti — the sixth avatāra who wielded the axe to destroy corrupt kṣatriya dynasties twenty-one times. Today commemorates the removal of obstacles: rāga, bhaya, krodha — attachment, fear, anger. The axe of knowledge severs the knot of material entanglement.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

vīta — freed, released from; past passive participle indicating completed liberation

rāga — attachment, passion, desire for pleasure; one of the three gates of hell (BG 16.21)

bhaya — fear, anxiety, dread of unpleasant; binding through avoidance

krodha — anger, wrath, defensive reaction; destructive emotional response

man-mayā — absorbed in Me, made of Me; mind saturated with Kṛṣṇa-consciousness

upāśritāḥ — having taken refuge, surrendered; those who have sought shelter

jñāna-tapasā — by the austerity/heat of knowledge; purification through discriminative understanding

mad-bhāvam — My state of being, transcendental love; the Lord's own prema-bhāva

🏹 BG 9.3 | 2026-07-09

 

अश्रद्दधाना: पुरुषा धर्मस्यास्य परन्तप । अप्राप्य मां निवर्तन्ते मृत्युसंसारवर्त्मनि ॥ ३ ॥

 

aśraddadhānāḥ puruṣā dharmasyāsya paran-tapa aprāpya māṁ nivartante mṛtyu-saṁsāra-vartmani

 

Those who are not faithful in this devotional service cannot attain Me, O conqueror of enemies. Therefore they return to the path of birth and death in this material world.

 

This verse strikes at the heart of spiritual progress: faith (śraddhā) is the indispensable foundation for advancing in Kṛṣṇa consciousness. Without faith in the process of devotional service, one cannot attain the Supreme Lord, regardless of intellectual understanding or external rituals. The faithless remain trapped in the cycle of birth and death (mṛtyu-saṁsāra), continually missing the opportunity for liberation. Śrīla Prabhupāda explains that faith is not blind belief but a firm conviction developed through hearing about the Lord from pure devotees and seeing the transformative power of bhakti in their lives. It is the complete confidence that by serving Kṛṣṇa, one can achieve all perfection. This faith is cultivated in association with devotees; without such association, even extensive study of scriptures leaves one hesitant and unable to fix the mind on devotion. The verse also reveals a compassionate warning: the faithless are not condemned eternally, but they simply continue their material journey until they develop the necessary sincerity. For the sincere practitioner, this verse underscores the importance of nurturing faith through regular hearing (śravaṇa), chanting (kīrtana), and association with sādhus. Faith acts as the root that nourishes the tree of devotional service; without it, branches (practices) may appear, but they lack vitality and cannot bear the fruit of love for God. From a systems perspective, faith is the initialization parameter that enables the “devotional OS” to boot. Without setting this flag (śraddhā=1), the system remains in a loop of material processes (saṁsāra), unable to access the higher routines of bhakti. Engaging with devotees is like loading the correct firmware—it sets the faith flag and allows the soul to progress toward Kṛṣṇa.

 

🔥 YS I.20 – śraddhā-vīrya-smṛti-samādhi-prajña-pūrvaka itareṣām – “For others, [samādhi] is preceded by faith, energy, mindfulness, and wisdom.” This sutra directly echoes BG 9.3: faith (śraddhā) is listed as the first prerequisite for attaining samādhi (the state of complete absorption in the divine). Just as the Gītā declares that the faithless cannot attain Kṛṣṇa, the Yoga Sūtras teach that without śraddhā, the higher states of yoga remain inaccessible. Faith is the ignition switch that enables the practitioner to marshal energy (vīrya), retain mindfulness (smṛti), and cultivate discernment (prajña) leading to samādhi. YS I.20 complements the Gītā’s emphasis on association with devotees: faith grows in the company of those who embody it. When one hears about the Lord’s glories from pure devotees, śraddhā arises; this then fuels the sustained effort (abhyāsa) and devotion (bhakti) praised in BG 9.3. Conversely, the absence of such association leaves the mind undeveloped in faith, resulting in the return to saṁsāra described in the verse. Furthermore, YS II.1 (tapas svādhyāyeśvara-praṇidhānāni kriyā-yogaḥ) – “Discipline, self‑study, and surrender to God constitute the yoga of action” – shows how faith expresses itself in practice: tapas (discipline) to maintain regular sadhana, svādhyāya (self‑study) to deepen understanding of the Lord, and īśvara‑praṇidhāna (surrender) as the culmination of faith. Together, these elements form the practical path that transforms the faithless into the faithful. Thus, the Yoga Sutras provide the methodological framework for achieving the faith praised in BG 9.3: through śraddhā, the practitioner unlocks the potential to progress toward samādhi and, ultimately, to attain the Supreme Lord through exclusive devotion.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

śraddhā (n.) – faith, firm conviction; like the boot flag that loads the devotional OS

dharma (n.) – here, devotional service; the prescribed duty of the soul

mṛtyu-saṁsāra (n.) – cycle of birth and death; the endless loop of material existence

parantapa (n.) – conqueror of enemies; epithet of Arjuna, one who defeats internal and external opposition

nivartante (v.) – they return, turn back; the soul’s regression to material cycles

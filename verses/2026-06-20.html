🏹 BG 8.15 | 2026-06-20

 

आब्रह्मभुवनाल्लोकाः punar āvartino ’rjuna । मामुपेत्य पुनर्जन्म दु:खालयमशाश्वतम् । नाप्‍नुवन्ति महात्मान: संसिद्धिं परमां गता: ॥ १५ ॥

 

ā-brahma-bhuvanāl lokāḥ
punar āvartino ’rjuna
mām upetya punar janma
duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam
nāpnuvanti mahātmānaḥ
saṁsiddhiṁ paramāṁ gatāḥ

 

From the highest planet in the material world down to the lowest, all are places of misery wherein repeated birth and death take place. But one who attains to My abode, O son of Kuntī, never takes birth again.

 

From the Sankhya perspective, the material world (prakṛti) is a temporary, miserable place of repeated birth and death (saṃsāra). This verse contrasts the temporary nature of all material planetary systems (even the highest, Brahmaloka) with the eternal abode of the Supreme Personality of Godhead. One who attains Kṛṣṇa’s abode never returns to the cycle of birth and death because they have achieved the highest perfection (saṁsiddhi). The Yoga Sutra affirms that through dispassion (vairāgya) and practice (abhyāsa), the mind becomes fixed on the puruṣa, leading to liberation (YS I.12‑I.16). Thus the verse serves as both a diagnosis of material existence’s insufficiency and a prescription for attaining the eternal goal through bhakti-yoga.

 

The verse invites us to recognize that all material planetary systems (ā-brahma-bhuvanāl lokāḥ) are temporary places of misery (duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam) where repeated birth and death occur. But one who attains the Supreme’s abode (mām upetya) achieves the highest perfection (saṁsiddhiṁ) and never returns to rebirth (punar janma na vidyate). In the language of the energy centers, this is the realization that even the highest material achievements are temporary, while the supreme goal is eternal liberation through devotion to Kṛṣṇa.

🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 3.3 | February 26, 2026

 

श्रीभगवानुवाच

लोकेऽस्मिन् द्विविधा निष्ठा पुरा प्रोक्ता मयानघ ।

ज्ञानयोगेन साङ्ख्यानां कर्मयोगेन योगिनाम् ॥ ३.३ ॥

 

śrī-bhagavān uvāca
loke 'smin dvi-vidhā niṣṭhā
purā proktā mayānagha
jñāna-yogena sāṅkhyānāṁ
karma-yogena yoginām

 

"The Supreme Personality of Godhead said: O sinless one, the two paths leading to liberation were previously explained by Me—the path of knowledge of the empiric philosophers, and the path of work for those devoted to action."

 

Here is the answer to Arjuna's demand for ONE path. Kṛṣṇa reveals: liberation has TWO gates, but ONE destination. Not contradiction—complementarity. The paths are not exclusive; they converge.

 

Jñāna-yoga (the Sāṅkhya path) — for those oriented toward analysis, contemplation, discrimination. Through knowledge of puruṣa and prakṛti, the embodied soul realizes its true nature.

 

Karma-yoga (the path of action) — for those oriented toward engagement, service, devotion. Through action surrendered to the Supreme, the soul transcends reaction.

 

Today's Ārdrā energy: the howling storm. Punarvasu follows—the return to goodness, Aditi's blessing. The two nakṣatras mirror the two paths: storm (intense effort) gives way to shelter (divine grace). Both are necessary.

 

The saptamī tithi: the 7th day, the number of forces at play. Seven chakras, seven notes, seven days of creation. The multiplicity that reveals the One.

 

The answer is not WHICH path but THAT you walk. The inactive cannot claim jñāna; the undiscerning cannot claim karma. Know your nature, pick your gate, walk through. The destination is the same.

 

🔥 YS 1.23 — īśvara-praṇidhānād vā — "Or [this state is attainable] from devotion to the Lord."

 

Both paths, when infused with devotion to Īśvara, become ONE path. Devotion is the secret fire that transforms knowledge into realization, and action into liberation.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

Dvi-vidhā (compound, nom. sg. f.) — two-fold, of two kinds; dvi (two) + vidhā (manner/kind)

Niṣṭhā (feminine noun) — firm standing, devotion, faith, fixed concentration; from ni-sthā (to stand firmly)

Jñāna-yoga (masculine compound) — the yoga of knowledge; jñāna (knowledge) + yoga (union/discipline)

Sāṅkhyānām (genitive plural) — of the Sāṅkhyas, the empiric philosophers; followers of Sāṅkhya

Karma-yoga (masculine compound) — the yoga of action; karma (action) + yoga (discipline)

Yoginām (genitive plural) — of the yogīs, of those devoted to yoga practice

🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 3.3 | February 26, 2026

 

श्रीभगवानुवाच

लोकेऽस्मिन् द्विविधा निष्ठा पुरा प्रोक्ता मयानघ ।

ज्ञानयोगेन साङ्ख्यानां कर्मयोगेन योगिनाम् ॥ ३.३ ॥

 

śrī-bhagavān uvāca
loke 'smin dvi-vidhā niṣṭhā
purā proktā mayānagha
jñāna-yogena sāṅkhyānāṁ
karma-yogena yoginām

 

"The Supreme Personality of Godhead said: O sinless Arjuna, I have already explained that there are two classes of men who try to realize the self. Some are inclined to understand it by empirical, philosophical speculation, and others by devotional service."

 

Here is the answer to Arjuna's demand for ONE path. Kṛṣṇa reveals TWO classes of men—not contradictory paths, but complementary approaches to the same truth. Both lead to Kṛṣṇa consciousness.

 

Sāṅkhya-yoga — the analytical study of spirit and matter. For those inclined to speculation, to understand by empirical knowledge and philosophy. The indirect process. Philosophers honestly seeking the Absolute Truth will eventually come to Kṛṣṇa consciousness.

 

Karma-yoga / Buddhi-yoga — working in Kṛṣṇa consciousness. The direct process of devotional service. Religion without philosophy is sentiment or fanaticism; philosophy without religion is mental speculation. Both must converge on Kṛṣṇa.

 

Prabhupāda's crucial point: "The path of Kṛṣṇa consciousness is better because it does not depend on purifying the senses by a philosophical process. Kṛṣṇa consciousness is itself the purifying process, and by the direct method of devotional service it is simultaneously easy and sublime."

 

Today's Ārdrā → Punarvasu: the storm of philosophical struggle transforms into the shelter of direct devotion. Both men eventually reach the same destination—but the bhakta arrives directly, while the jñāni takes the winding road.

 

🔥 YS 1.23 — īśvara-praṇidhānād vā — "Or [this state is attainable] from devotion to the Lord."

 

Both paths, when infused with devotion to Īśvara, become ONE path. Devotion is the secret fire that transforms knowledge into realization, and action into liberation.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

Dvi-vidhā (compound, nom. sg. f.) — two-fold, of two kinds; dvi (two) + vidhā (manner/kind)

Niṣṭhā (feminine noun) — firm standing, devotion, faith, fixed concentration; from ni-sthā (to stand firmly)

Jñāna-yoga (masculine compound) — the yoga of knowledge; jñāna (knowledge) + yoga (union/discipline)

Sāṅkhyānām (genitive plural) — of the Sāṅkhyas, the empiric philosophers; followers of Sāṅkhya

Karma-yoga (masculine compound) — the yoga of action; karma (action) + yoga (discipline)

Yoginām (genitive plural) — of the yogīs, of those devoted to yoga practice

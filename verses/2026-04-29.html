🏹 BG 4.3 | April 29, 2026

 

स एवायं मया तेऽद्य योगः प्रोक्तः पुरातनः ।

भक्तोऽसि मे सखा चेति रहस्यं ह्येतदुत्तमम् ॥ ३ ॥

 

sa evāyaṁ mayā te 'dya
yogaḥ proktaḥ purātanaḥ
bhakto 'si me sakhā ceti
rahasyaṁ hy etad uttamam

 

That very ancient science of the relationship with the Supreme is today told by Me to you because you are My devotee as well as My friend and can therefore understand the transcendental mystery of this science.

 

Kṛṣṇa reveals why this knowledge is transmitted. The secret is not withheld from qualified recipients. Arjuna receives because of two conditions: bhaktaḥ asi (you are My devotee) and sakhā ca (and My friend). Both are required — devotion without intimacy remains formal; intimacy without devotion becomes familiar.

 

Saḥ eva ayam — that very same (ancient) science. Not new, not different. The same knowledge spoken millions of years ago to Vivasvān. Kṛṣṇa is telling the truth — this is not theoretical religion, this is recovery of the original system.

 

Mayā te adya proktaḥ — proclaimed by Me to you today. The Lord does not repeat this to everyone. The transmission requires qualification. Today, Narasimha Jayanti, we remember how the Lord appeared specifically for His devotee Prahlāda — just as now He speaks for Arjuna.

 

Rahasyam uttamam — the supreme secret. This is veda-rahasya, the hidden core of the Vedas. Not for those who treat spiritual knowledge as information, but for those who treat the Lord as beloved and friend.

 

🔥 SĀṄKHYA-FIRST PRINCIPLES ANALYSIS

The verse presents the mechanism of transmission at the level of metaphysical architecture:

 

Puruṣa ↔ Buddhi Interface: Sāṅkhya describes buddhi (intellect) as the interface between puruṣa (conscious soul) and prakṛti (material nature). For puruṣa to receive knowledge of itself, the buddhi must become transparent — this is bhakti. Arjuna's buddhi is prepared through devotion (the dissolution of ahaṅkāra, the "I am this body" delusion) AND friendship (the establishment of sāmya, equality of vision). The Lord speaks directly to buddhi when buddhi is cleared of prakṛti's obstructions.

 

The Rahasya as Para-Prakṛti: The "secret" is not hidden information but direct experience of para-prakṛti (transcendental nature) as opposed to apara-prakṛti (material nature). Material wisdom is received through the senses → manas → buddhi. Transcendental wisdom is received through devotion → buddhi → direct puruṣa-realization. The interface is different, the source is different, the result is different.

 

Paramparā = Resonance: Knowledge transmitted from guru to disciple is not information transfer but frequency alignment — like two violins of identical pitch. When the guru's buddhi vibrates with realized truth, the disciple's buddhi resonates. But the resonance requires: (1) proper pitch (qualification through devotion), and (2) proximity (sakhya, the intimate relationship). Lack either and the transmission fails.

 

🔥 YS I.7 — pratyakṣa-anumāna-āgamāḥ pramāṇāni

"Direct perception, inference, and testimony are the sources of valid knowledge."

 

Kṛṣṇa here exemplifies āgama — testimony, the word of the authoritative teacher. But this is not blind acceptance. Arjuna qualifies to receive because his devotion AND friendship create the vessel. Testimony becomes pramāṇa (valid knowledge) only when the recipient is qualified. Five-year-old Prahlāda didn't need śāstra — his testimony was direct: "Lord Narasimha is everywhere." His buddhi was transparent because his devotion was pure.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

saḥ eva — "that very same" — the ancient science. Indicates identity across time: knowledge is not invented but recovered.

pura-tanaḥ — "ancient"; from purātana — from the earliest times. The paramparā stretches to the horizon.

proktaḥ — "proclaimed, spoken" — from pra-vac; implies formal transmission, not casual speech.

bhaktaḥ — devotee; one who is devoted (from bhaj, to serve). The active condition: engagement.

sakhā — friend; intimate companion (from sac, to accompany). The relational condition: closeness.

rahasya — mystery, secret; the hidden thing. In Sāṅkhya: that which requires buddhi-sūkṣma (subtle intellect) to perceive.

uttamam — supreme, highest, best; from ud (up) — that which transcends level.

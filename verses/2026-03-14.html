🏹 BG 9.22 | March 14, 2026

 

अनन्याश्चिन्तयन्तो मां ये जनाः पर्युपासते ।

तेषां नित्याभियुक्तानां योगक्षेमं वहाम्यहम् ॥ २२ ॥

 

ananyāś cintayanto māṁ ye janāḥ paryupāsate
teṣāṁ nityābhiyuktānāṁ yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmy aham

 

But those who always worship Me with exclusive devotion, meditating on My transcendental form—to them I carry what they lack, and I preserve what they have.

 

This is the verse that defines Ekādaśī. Not the fasting, not the vigil, not the external observance—this is what those observances point toward: ananyāḥ, no other. When Kṛṣṇa says "those who think of Me without deviation," He is describing a consciousness so exclusively focused that it permits no rivals.

 

Ananyāś cintayantaḥ: this is not occasional remembrance. This is cintayantaḥ—continuous, unbroken thought-flow toward the Supreme. Like a river that has found its ocean, the devotee's consciousness flows ceaselessly toward Kṛṣṇa. The fasting of Ekādaśī creates the conditions for this; it removes obstacles, clears noise, makes space for the holy name to resound.

 

What does Kṛṣṇa promise? Yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmy aham. The most intimate verse on divine providence. Yoga: I provide what you lack. Kṣema: I protect what you have. Like a mother who feeds her hungry child and keeps safe what is given, Kṛṣṇa takes total responsibility. The Papamochani Ekādaśī energy works this way: the vow creates the vessel, Kṛṣṇa's promise fills it.

 

🔥 YS I.23 — ईश्वरप्रणिधानाद्वा
"Or [samādhi is attained] by surrender to Īśvara." Patañjali offers surrender as alternative path. But Kṛṣṇa reveals deeper dimension: the surrendered devotee finds the Lord Himself carrying yoga (acquisition) and kṣema (preservation). This is Bhakti Yoga's unique gift.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

ananyāḥ (nom. pl.) — having no other; exclusive devotion without rival; the absolute singularity of bhakti

cintayantaḥ (pres. act. part.) — thinking of, meditating upon; continuous thought-flow; unbroken remembrance

paryupāsate (3pl. mid.) — worship properly from all sides; complete, adequate worship surrounding the Lord

nitya-abhiyuktānām (gen. pl.) — of those always fixed in devotion; perpetual application

yoga (m.) — acquisition of what is lacking; what is needed for spiritual advancement

kṣema (m.) — preservation of what is possessed; protection, safety; what keeps one from falling

vahāmi (1sg. pres.) — I carry, I bear; the Lord's active taking of responsibility

🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 9.29 | March 03, 2026

 

समोऽहं सर्वभूतेषु न मे द्वेष्योऽस्ति न प्रियः ।

ये भजन्ति तु मां भक्त्या मयि ते तेषु चाप्यहम् ॥ २९ ॥

 

samo 'haṁ sarva-bhūteṣu
na me dveṣyo 'sti na priyaḥ
ye bhajanti tu māṁ bhaktyā
mayi te teṣu cāpy aham

 

"I envy no one, nor am I partial to anyone. I am equal to all. But whoever renders service unto Me in devotion is a friend, is in Me, and I am also a friend to him."

 

The Supreme Lord declares His absolute impartiality toward all beings while simultaneously revealing the transcendent principle of devotion. This is not contradiction—this is the operating system of bhakti. Krishna rains His mercy upon all, like a cloud that showers water without discrimination upon rock, soil, or sea. Yet the reservoir that opens itself to receive becomes filled.

 

This verse cuts through the delusion that the Absolute is either an indifferent void or a capricious favor-giver like the petty gods of worldly pantheons. The Lord is present in all beings equally—this is the truth of samaḥ (equally disposed), without differentiation or preference. But the reciprocation of love is not automatic; it requires the opening of the heart. Devotion (bhakti) is the API call that establishes two-way communication. The devotee enters into Krishna (mayi te), and Krishna enters into the devotee (teṣu cāpy aham). This is not metaphorical—this is the lived reality of those who engage in bhajanti, loving service.

 

On this day of Pūrṇimā and Chaitanya Mahāprabhu Jayanti, we remember the Lord who descended to teach that divine love knows no boundaries. The moon appears the same to all, yet only the clear lake reflects it perfectly. The heart cleansed by devotion becomes that mirror. This is the unitive principle: complete equality establishing the foundation for the most intimate particularity.

 

🔥 YS 1.23 — īśvara-praṇidhānāt vā — "Or [samādhi is attained] by devotion to the Supreme." Patañjali presents īśvara-praṇidhāna as one of the central avenues to meditative absorption. Here we see the elegant meeting of Bhakti and Raja Yoga. Where the sūtra states devotion to the Supreme as a means, Krishna reveals it as the fulfillment. Practicing praṇidhāna (surrender, devotion) is the method; achieving mayi te (they are in Me) is the result. The sūtra is the code; the verse is the compiled output.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

samaḥ (adj.) — equally disposed, same; without differentiation or preference

sarva-bhūteṣu (loc. pl.) — in all living entities; sarva = all, bhūta = living being

dveṣyaḥ (adj.) — hateful, odious; one who is to be hated

bhajanti (vb., 3rd pl. pres.) — they worship, serve; related to bhakti, the verb of loving devotion

bhaktyā (f. instr. sg.) — by devotion; the instrument of spiritual connection

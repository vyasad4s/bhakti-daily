🏹 BG 8.15 | 2026-06-22

 

मामुपेत्य पुनर्जन्म दु:खालयमशाश्वतम् । नाप्नुवन्ति महात्मान: संसिद्धिं परमां गता: ॥ १५ ॥

mām upetya punar janma
duḥkhālayam aśāśvatam
nāpnuvanti mahātmānaḥ
saṁsiddhiṁ paramāṁ gatāḥ

 

After attaining Me, the great souls, who are yogīs in devotion, never return to this temporary world, which is full of miseries, because they have attained the highest perfection.

 

BG 8.15 points to the irreversibility of true bhakti-yoga: once the soul attains Krishna’s abode, the material world loses its appeal permanently. This is not a temporary suppression of desire but a fundamental shift in consciousness—like a process that has achieved root access and no longer needs to interact with the user-space illusions of Maya. The 'great souls' (mahātmānaḥ) are those who have executed the highest perfection (saṁsiddhiṁ) through unwavering devotion, making the cycle of birth and death (punar janma) irrelevant. In Sankhya terms, this is the puruṣa disengaging from prakṛti’s modifications, realizing its true nature as conscious agent distinct from the material machinery. The devotee’s association with Krishna becomes the only desirable 'process' to run, rendering all other 'services' obsolete.

 

🔥 YS 1.23: īśvara-praṇidhānāt va

Connection: BG 8.15 emphasizes that after attaining Krishna, the great souls never return to the temporary world full of miseries. Yoga Sutra 1.23 states that samādhi (perfect absorption) can be attained through special devotion to the Lord (īśvara-praṇidhāna). Both point to the same essential practice: exclusive focus on the Divine leads to liberation from the cycle of birth and death. The verse’s “after attaining Me” (mām upetya) is the practical result of īśvara-praṇidhāna, where the mind’s ceaseless flow is directed toward Krishna, resulting in the attainment of the highest perfection (saṁsiddhiṁ paramāṁ gatāḥ). Thus the sutra provides the methodological foundation for the verse’s promise of permanent liberation.

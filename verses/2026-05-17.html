🏹 BG 5.1 | 2026-05-17

 

अर्जुन उवाच

सन्न्यासं कर्मणां कृष्ण पुनर्योगं च शंससि ।

यच्छ्रेय एतयोरेकं तन्मे ब्रूहि सुनिश्चितम् ॥ १ ॥

 

arjuna uvāca
sannyāsaṁ karmaṇāṁ kṛṣṇa
punar yogaṁ ca śaṁsasi
yac chreya etayor ekaṁ
tan me brūhi su-niścitam

 

Arjuna said: O Kṛṣṇa, first of all You ask me to renounce work, and then again You recommend work with devotion. Now will You kindly tell me definitely which of the two is more beneficial?

 

In this Fifth Chapter of the Bhagavad-gītā, the Lord clarifies the relationship between renunciation (sannyāsa) and the yoga of action (karma-yoga). Arjuna is perplexed: he sees renunciation as the cessation of activity and karma-yoga as the engagement in it. He seeks the "shreya"—the ultimate benefit.

 

From a Sāṅkhya perspective, the confusion lies in the identification of the self with the material body (prakṛti). The a-void of action is not found in the absence of movement, but in the absence of the "doer" mentality. When action is performed as a service to the Divine, it ceases to be "karma" (binding action) and becomes a tool for purification. It is the "debugging" of the ego-drive from the execution of duty.

 

🔥 YS 2.1: kriyāyogaḥ — Austerity, self-study, and surrender to God constitute the yoga of action. This sutra directly complements BG 5.1 by defining karma-yoga as a threefold practice: tapas (disciplined effort), svādhyāya (self-reflection through scripture), and īśvarapraṇidhānā (surrender to the Divine). True action is a conscious, offered performance that purifies the practitioner, transforming daily routine into a spiritual exploit.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

Sannyāsa (noun) - Renunciation; the act of giving up material desires and duties to focus on the Absolute.

Karma-Yoga (noun) - The yoga of action; performing one's duty without attachment to the results.

Shreya (noun) - The ultimate good or benefit; the path leading to liberation (mukti) rather than temporary pleasure (preya).

 

TODAY'S PRACTICE:

The Karma-Yoga Offering:

1. Morning Offering: Upon waking, dedicate today's actions to the Divine. Say: "May all my work be an offering to You, O Kṛṣṇa."
2. Detach from Results: Before beginning any task, consciously release attachment to success or failure. Focus on the duty itself, not the outcome.
3. Evening Review: At sunset, reflect: "Did I act with sincerity? Did I offer the fruits?"

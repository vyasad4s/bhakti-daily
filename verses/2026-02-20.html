🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 2.69 | February 20, 2026

 

या निशा सर्वभूतानां

तस्यां जागर्ति संयमी ।

यस्यां जाग्रति भूतानि

सा निशा पश्यतो मुनेः ॥

 

yā niśā sarva-bhūtānāṁ
tasyāṁ jāgarti saṁyamī
yasyāṁ jāgrati bhūtāni
sā niśā paśyato muneḥ

 

"What is night for all beings is the time of awakening for the self-controlled; and the time of awakening for all beings is night for the introspective sage."

 

Two realities exist simultaneously. For those absorbed in material pursuits, spiritual discrimination is darkness — they literally cannot see it. The self-controlled yogi lives in that "night" fully awake, perceiving what the masses miss entirely. Complete polarity.

 

The "wakefulness" of material culture — its achievements, ambitions, sensory pleasures — is actually sleep in disguise. The sage sees what others are too busy to look at. This is viveka operating at full capacity: one who has completely inverted the default setting of consciousness.

 

The progression continues: BG 2.66 established prerequisites, 2.67 showed sense capture, 2.68 demonstrated restraint → established intelligence. Now 2.69: the ultimate result — saṁyamī, self-control, creates complete perceptual inversion. The yogi is awake when others sleep.

 

🔥 YS II.54 — svārṣānubhūtiviṣayāsaṁprayoge cittasya svarūpānukāra ivendriyāṇāṁ pratyāhāraḥ — "The withdrawal of the senses from their objects that follows the mastery of the mind is pratyāhāra."

 

BG 2.69 describes the result of pratyāhāra practiced to perfection. When senses withdraw, the yogi no longer perceives sense objects as "real" — they become like dreams to the waking mind. Patañjali defines pratyāhāra as the mind's return to its own form; BG 2.69 shows this mastery complete.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

Niśā (feminine noun) — night, darkness, state of unknowing; the condition where consciousness is obscured

Saṁyamī (masculine noun, from saṁyama) — one who practices self-control; literally "one who holds together" — holds the mind from its natural tendency to fragment

Jāgarti (verb, 3rd person singular) — is awake, is alert; from jāgṛ to wake, to be vigilant

Bhūtāni (neuter plural noun) — living entities, beings; literally "that which has come into existence" — all embodied souls

Paśyataḥ (present active participle, genitive singular) — of one who sees, of the seer; from paś to see — the sage whose perception is purified

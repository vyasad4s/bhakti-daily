🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 3.19 | April 4, 2026

 

तस्मादसक्तः सततं कार्यं कर्म समाचर ।

असक्तो ह्याचरन्कर्म परमाप्‍नोति पूरूषः ॥ १९ ॥

 

tasmād asaktaḥ satataṃ kāryaṃ karma samācara | asakto hy ācaran karma param āpnoti pūruṣaḥ ||

 

Therefore, without being attached to the fruits of activities, one should act as a matter of duty, for by working without attachment one attains the Supreme.

 

Arjuna asks why he should act if he's spiritually advanced. Krishna answers with devastating clarity: attachment to inaction is itself a bondage. The path is not renunciation of work—it's renunciation of the fruits of work.

 

This is the heart of Karma Yoga. Most "spiritual" people use practice to escape responsibility. They meditate to avoid hard conversations. They chant to bypass difficult decisions. This is not liberation—it's spiritual bypassing, and Krishna calls it out.

 

The Sanskrit is precise: asaktaḥ (without attachment), satatam (constantly), kāryam karma (duty as work), samācara (perform completely). Not half-heartedly. Not when convenient. Not when inspired. Constantly. As duty. Without attachment to results.

 

The promise—param āpnoti—is that working this way attains the Supreme. Not despite the work. Through the work. The action itself becomes yoga when attachment is burned away.

 

🔥 Yoga Sūtra 2.1: tapāḥ-svādhyāya-īśvara-praṇidhānāni kriyā-yogaḥ — "Discipline, self-study, and devotion to God constitute Kriyā Yoga."

 

Patañjali's Kriyā Yoga maps directly to BG 3.19. Tapas burns attachment. Svādhyāya examines motive—am I acting from duty or desire? Īśvara-praṇidhāna surrenders all fruits to the Supreme. Karma Yoga is not separate from classical yoga—it IS the path of action refined into spiritual practice.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

asaktaḥ (adj.) — without attachment, detached. Literally "not stuck to." The action complete in itself, not grasping at fruits.

kāryam (n.) — duty, what must be done. Not optional. The non-negotiables of your dharma.

satatam (adv.) — constantly. Not when inspired. Without interruption.

samācara (verb) — perform completely. Execute fully.

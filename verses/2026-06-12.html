🏹 BG 7.21 | 2026-06-12

 

यो यो यां यां तनुं भक्त: श्रद्धयार्चितुमिच्छति

तस्य तस्याचलां श्रद्धां तामेव विदधायहम् ॥ २१ ॥

 

yo yo yāṁ yāṁ tanuṁ bhaktaḥ śraddhayārcitum icchati tasya tasyācalāṁ śraddhāṁ tām eva vidadhāmy aham

 

I am in everyone’s heart as the Supersoul. As soon as one desires to worship some demigod, I make his faith steady so that he can devote himself to that particular deity.

 

This verse reveals the intimate presence of the Lord as the Supersoul residing within every heart. Even when a person turns toward demigod worship motivated by material desire, Krishna does not reject them; instead, He steadies their faith so that their devotion, however misdirected, remains sincere. This steady faith is the gift of the Supersoul, ensuring that the living entity’s inclination toward the divine is not lost, even amidst material aspirations. Today’s pañcāṅga highlights Pradosh Vrat, the auspicious time for Shiva worship, occurring during the Trayodashi tithi (from 10:06 AM). The Nakṣatra Bharani (ruled by Yama, the lord of restraint) holds until 18:35 IST, emphasizing discipline and the capacity to endure hardship. The transition to Krittika (ruled by Agni, fire) after 18:35 IST brings the energy of transformation and purification. The Yoga Atiganda (obstacle-producing) until 11:55 IST reminds us that material desires create obstacles, while the succeeding Sukarman (good deed) yoga supports virtuous action. The Sun in Taurus provides endurance, and the Moon in Aries then Taurus offers initiative followed by stability.

 

🔥 ईश्वरप्रणिधानात वा īśvara-praṇidhānād vā Or, this previously mentioned state is attainable from devotion to the lord. BG 7.21 and YS I.23 both highlight the role of devotion to the Supreme Lord. In the Gītā, Krishna affirms that He steadies the faith of those who worship demigods, ensuring their devotion remains sincere. In the Yoga Sūtras, devotion to īśvara (the Lord) is presented as a direct means to attain the state of samādhi. The connection lies in the understanding that steady faith (śraddhā) in the Lord, whether expressed through direct worship or through the medium of demigods, is the power that lifts the mind beyond distraction. When we offer our faith to the Lord, He reciprocates by making our devotion unwavering, just as He steadies the faith of demigod worshippers. Thus, the practice of īśvara-praṇidhāna is the inner steadying of faith that BG 7.21 describes from the Lord’s side.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

bhakta — Devotee, one who engages in devotional service. Like a dedicated process that runs continuously in service to the kernel.

śraddhā — Faith, trust, conviction. Like the root certificate authority that establishes trust in a secure system.

acala — Steady, unmoving, firm. Like a stable load balancer that never drops connections.

īśvara — Supreme Lord, controller. Like the hypervisor that manages all virtual machines.

tasmāt — From that, therefore. Like a logical implication that follows inevitably from a premise.

 

Practice for Pradosh Vrat and Steady Faith

Morning (Brahma Muhūrta 03:53–04:41 AM): Chant BG 7.21 slowly. Focus on the word śraddhā — faith. Where in your life do you place your trust? Is it in material outcomes, relationships, or the Divine? Let the chant awaken the recognition that even your faith in demigod worship is steadied by the Supersoul. Then, as the day begins, offer your intention: “May my faith be steady and true to You, O Lord.”

Evening (Amṛt Kāl 14:15–15:42 IST / 02:15–03:42 PM EDT): After the Pradosh Vrat period has begun, sit in quiet reflection. The Moon in Taurus provides steadiness, and the Sun in Taurus offers endurance. In this transition, meditate on the steady faith that the Lord gives: not the external form of worship, but the internal trust that remains unshaken. Let YS I.23’s insight sink in: devotion to the Lord is the direct path to inner stability. Use this period for extended japa or study of the Bhāgavatam, focusing on the pastimes of the Lord that exemplify steady faith—such as Prahlāda’s unwavering devotion amidst tribulation.

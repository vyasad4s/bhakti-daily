Bhagavad Gita Chapter 9, Verse 30

अपि चेत्सुदुराचारो भजते मामनन्यभाक् । जनिमान्तोमुखास्ते ’हं भवामि तस्य दर्शनः ॥ ३० ॥

Yoga Sūtra Connection

YS I.33: maitrī-karuṇā-muditopekṣāṇāṁ viṣayāṁ cittaprasādanam

The mind becomes purified by cultivating feelings of friendliness, compassion, joy, and equanimity.

This sutra provides the practical "how-to" for the purification (pavitratā) promised in BG 9.30. Krishna says that the devotee becomes saintly; Patanjali explains the internal chemistry of that saintliness. Purity is not the absence of a past; it is the presence of a new attitude. By replacing envy with friendliness (maitrī), hatred with compassion (karuṇā), jealousy with joy (muditā), and attachment with equanimity (upekṣā), the practitioner systematically scrubs the "karmic rust" from the mirror of the heart.

The "abominable actions" mentioned in the Gita are the result of a mind clouded by the opposite of these four attitudes: anger, greed, and hatred. Therefore, the path from "sudurācāra" (worst behavior) to "pavitra" (pure) is exactly the path described in YS I.33. The "hero in knowledge" (jñāna-vīra) is simply one who has the discipline to maintain these four attitudes even in the face of material adversity. When the citta is thus "prasāda" (clear/tranquil), the divine grace mentioned in BG 9.30 can flow unobstructed, completing the transformation from sinner to saint.

Practice for Today

Karmic Audit: Take 15 minutes today to identify one recurring negative pattern (a "karmic loop") in your life. Instead of feeling guilt, simply observe it as a "legacy code" error and consciously offer that pattern to Krishna, asking for the strength to rewrite it through service.

Cultivating Purity (YS I.33): Throughout the day, whenever you feel a spark of judgment or envy toward another, immediately replace it with a wish for their well-being (maitrī). Notice how this shift in energy lightens your own internal load.

The "Root Access" Mantra: Use the Hare Krishna mahā-mantra today as a tool for "system reset." Whenever you feel overwhelmed by material stress, stop for one minute and chant with the intention of shifting your identity from the "doer" to the "servant."

Shatabhisha Healing: Since today is the day of the "Hundred Physicians," treat your sadhana (chanting, reading, service) as the primary medicine for your soul. Be consistent and disciplined in your practice, as if your spiritual life depended on it—because it does.

Surrender the Result: Practice BG 9.30 by trusting that your sincere efforts in bhakti are more powerful than your past mistakes. Let go of the fear of "not being good enough" and simply act with love.

🏹 BG 7.9 | 2026-06-11

 

पुण्यो गन्ध: पृथिव्यां च तेजश्चास्मि विभावसौ ।

जीवनं सर्वभूतेषु तपश्चास्मि तपस्विषु ॥ ९ ॥

puṇyo gandhaḥ pṛthivyāṁ ca tejaś cāsmi vibhāvasau jīvanaṁ sarva-bhūteṣu tapaś cāsmi tapasviṣu

I am the original fragrance of the earth, and I am the heat in fire. I am the life of all that lives, and I am the penances of all ascetics.

Commentary

This verse reveals Kṛṣṇa as the essence behind all that sustains life and spiritual practice. The original fragrance of earth (puṇya gandha) points to the uncontaminated, pure source that permeates everything — just as the true self is the unchanging background of all experience. The heat in fire (tejaś cāsmi vibhāvasau) is the energy that transforms, cooks, and sustains — analogous to the inner fire of discipline that burns away impurity. Life in all living beings (jīvanaṁ sarva-bhūteṣu) shows that consciousness itself is a spark of the Divine. Finally, and most relevant to today’s Ekādaśī, Kṛṣṇa declares Himself to be the penances of all ascetics (tapaś cāsmi tapasviṣu). Tapas is not mere austerity for its own sake; it is the focused heat of devotion that purifies the mind and senses. On Ekādaśī, we engage in tapas through fasting and withdrawal from sense gratification, not to torture the body but to redirect the energy normally consumed by digestion toward higher awareness. In this way, the fasting becomes a living offering: we recognize that even our capacity for discipline originates from the Lord who is the very essence of tapas.

Today’s pañcāṅga highlights Parama Ekādaśī, the supreme day of fasting, with the Moon in fiery Aries and the Sun in steady Taurus — a combination that supports both the initiative to begin the fast and the endurance to sustain it. The Nakṣatra Ashwini (until 08:58 PM) brings the energy of the divine physicians, the Ashwini Kumāras, who renew and heal. This aligns with the verse’s promise: the original fragrance and healing heat are none other than Kṛṣṇa Himself. As the day progresses into Bharani Nakṣatra (from 08:58 PM), the energy of restraint and yama deepens, supporting the continuation of the fast. The Yoga of Sobhana (auspicious fortune) until 15:29 indicates that the observance is favored, while the later Atiganda Yoga reminds us that obstacles may arise — yet the strength to overcome them comes from recognizing the Lord as the source of our own tapas.

Yoga Sūtra Connection: YS II.1

तपः स्वाध्यायेश्वरप्रणिधानानि kriyāyogaḥ

tapaḥ svādhyāyeśvarapraṇidhānāni kriyāyogaḥ

Discipline, self-study, and surrender to God constitute the yoga of action.

BG 7.9 and YS II.1 both place tapas (discipline) at the forefront of spiritual practice. In the Gītā, Kṛṣṇa reveals Himself as the very essence of the ascetic’s penances — the power that fuels true austerity. In the Yoga Sūtras, tapas is the first component of Kriya Yoga, joined by svādhyāya (self-study) and īśvara-praṇidhāna (surrender to the Lord). Together, they form a balanced path: discipline burns away impurity, self-study cultivates discernment, and surrender ensures that the effort is directed toward the Divine rather than egoic achievement. On Ekādaśī, we practice a form of Kriya Yoga: the fasting is tapas, the study of scripture and chanting is svādhyāya, and the offering of the fast to Śrī Kṛṣṇa is īśvara-praṇidhāna. Thus the day becomes a complete unit of yoga in action, where the discipline is not an end in itself but a means to reconnect with the source of all strength.

TERM DEFINITIONS:

puṇyaḥ — Original, uncontaminated, pure. Like the root filesystem image that remains untouched by runtime modifications — the clean source from which all clean instances are spawned.

gandhaḥ — Fragrance, scent. Like the cryptographic hash of a file — an invisible signature that permeates and verifies integrity without being consumed.

tejaḥ — Heat, radiance, prowess. Like the CPU’s thermal design power (TDP) — the measured capacity to perform work, not the work itself, yet enabling all computation.

vibhāvasu — Fire, specifically the sacred fire of ritual. Like the power supply unit that converts raw energy into stable voltages — unseen but essential for all components to function.

jīvanam — Life, vital principle. Like the kernel’s scheduler — the invisible process that allocates time to all tasks, making multitasking possible.

tapaḥ — Discipline, austerity, inner heat. Like the CPU’s boost clock — a temporary increase in performance driven by controlled effort, not sustainable indefinitely but critical for peak performance.

tapasviṣu — Those who practice tapas, ascetics. Like the system administrators who enforce security policies — they undergo discomfort to maintain the integrity of the whole.

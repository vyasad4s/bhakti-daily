March 02, 2026 BG 3.7

यस्त्विन्द्रियाणि मनसा नियम्यारभतेऽर्जुन ।

कर्मेन्द्रियैः कर्मयोगमसक्तः स विशिष्यते ॥ ३.७ ॥

yas tv indriyāṇi manasā

niyamyārabhate 'rjuna

karmendriyaiḥ karma-yogam

asaktaḥ sa viśiṣyate

"On the other hand, if a sincere person tries to control the active senses by the mind and begins karma-yoga [in Kṛṣṇa consciousness] without attachment, he is by far superior."

The contrast is complete. Yesterday exposed the pretender — one who restrains the body while the mind clings to sense objects. Today reveals the superior path — karma-yoga with asakti (non-attachment). Kṛṣṇa declares this person viśiṣyate — not just better, but by far superior.

The sequence is deliberate: verse 3.6 described the hypocrite (mithyācāraḥ); verse 3.7 reveals the authentic practitioner. The difference is not external restraint but internal alignment. The true yogī uses manasā niyamya — regulating through the mind, which has been submitted to Kṛṣṇa.

Prabhupāda emphasizes: better a sincere sweeper than a charlatan meditator. Every ordinary action becomes worship when offered without attachment. The householder who chants Hare Kṛṣṇa while earning livelihood surpasses the pseudo-renunciant.

The key lies in asaktaḥ — without attachment. Not aversion; not suppression. The person who controls senses through buddhi and dedicates every action to the Supreme — this is the superior yogī.

🧠 Sāṅkhya Concepts

The guṇas of prakṛti still operate. The true yogī transforms action through consciousness (cit). The puruṣa remains unattached witness while buddhi directs all action toward the Supreme.

When buddhi is fixed on Kṛṣṇa, action flows without binding. This is naiṣkarmya — actionlessness in action.

🔥 Yoga Sutra Connection

YS 1.12 — abhyāsa-vairāgyābhyāṁ tat-nirodhaḥ — "The fluctuations of the mind are restrained by practice AND detachment."

Karma-yoga combines both. Practice without detachment creates rigid habit; detachment without practice becomes mere philosophy.

📚 Sanskrit Terms

  • Tu — but; contrasting particle
  • Indriyāṇi — the five senses of perception
  • Manasā — by the mind
  • Niyamya — regulating, controlling
  • Ārabhate — begins, undertakes
  • Karmendriyaiḥ — by the organs of action
  • Karma-yogam — the path of duty without attachment
  • Asaktaḥ — unattached, free from clinging
  • Sa — that person
  • Viśiṣyate — is by far superior; distinguished

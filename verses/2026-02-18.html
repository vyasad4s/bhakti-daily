🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 2.67 | February 18, 2026

 

इन्द्रियाणां हि चरतां

यन्मनोऽनुविधीयते ।

तदस्य हरति प्रज्ञां

वायुर्नावमिवाम्भसि ॥

 

indriyāṇām hi caratāṃ
yan mano 'nuvidhīyate
tad asya harati prajñāṃ
vāyur nāvam ivāmbhasi

 

"As a strong wind sweeps away a boat on the water, even one of the roving senses on which the mind focuses can carry away a man's intelligence."

 

Yesterday established that without yukti (connection), the prerequisites for peace are destroyed. Today we see how this destruction happens — the mechanics of bondage in real-time.

 

The caratām — the roving, wandering, uncontrolled senses — are not the problem. The problem is yan mano 'nuvidhīyate — the mind that follows them. This is the discipline failure: not sense-withdrawal but sense-chasing. The wind of sensory input doesn't capsize the boat; the anchorless mind drifting with the current does.

 

The image is precise: vāyur nāvam ivāmbhasi — a boat on water (ambhasi) caught by wind. The senses are the wind (vāyu). The mind is the boat. The intelligence (prajñā) is the cargo that gets swept away. In Sāṅkhya terms: the puruṣa misidentifies as the drifting vessel instead of the witnessing shore.

 

This is Dvitīya energy — the second day, the day of sequence and continuation. What began in the darkness of Amāvāsyā continues into the waxing light. The transition from Śatabhiṣā (healing) to Pūrva Bhādrapadā (fire/penance) mirrors the verse: healing requires stability, but the mind that follows distraction turns healing into burning.

 

🔥 YS 2.54 — saṁprajñāta-pratyāhāraḥ — "Withdrawal of the senses from their objects is called pratyāhāra."

 

Patañjali's pratyāhāra is the technical solution to BG 2.67's problem. Where the verse describes the boat carried by wind, the Yoga Sūtra prescribes the harbor. Pratyāhāra is not suppression but withdrawal — the mind ceases to anuvidhīyate (follow) the caratām indriyānām (roving senses). The same wind blows, but the boat is anchored.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

Caratām (participle, genitive plural) — of those that roam/wander; from car (to move). The senses in their natural uncontrolled state — not evil, just mobile.

Anuvidhīyate (passive verb, 3rd person singular) — is followed/obeyed; from anu-vidh (to follow rules). The mind's voluntary submission to sensory movement.

Prajñā (feminine noun) — wisdom, intelligence; from pra + jñā (to know). The capacity for discrimination — the CPU of consciousness.

Nāva (feminine noun) — boat, vessel; the mind as vehicle. Requires direction or drifts.

Ambhasi (locative singular) — in the water; the sense-field, the ocean of viṣaya.

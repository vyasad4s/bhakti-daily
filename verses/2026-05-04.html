🏹 BG 5.29 | May 4, 2026

 

भोक्तारं यज्ञतपसां सर्वलोकमहेश्वरम् ।

सुहृदं सर्वभूतानां ज्ञात्वा मां शान्तिमृच्छति ॥ २९ ॥

 

bhoktāraṁ yajña-tapasāṁ
sarva-loka-maheśvaram
suhṛdaṁ sarva-bhūtānāṁ
jñātvā māṁ śāntim ṛcchati

 

The sages, knowing that the Supreme Lord is the ultimate enjoyer of all sacrifices and austerities, the master of all planets, and the well-wisher of all beings, attain peace.

 

Refuge in the Read-Only Observer: BG 5.29 offers the exploit to mental noise. This is not passive escapism; it's a system logout. The verse maps directly to Sāṅkhya metaphysics — puruṣa (consciousness) is the read-only observer, while prakṛti (nature) handles the writes. When you recognize the Supreme as the bhoktā (enjoyer), you stop clinging to outcomes. This is the root access to śānti — the null state where mental noise ceases.

 

Sāṅkhya-First Principles: - Puruṣa Reflection: a-bhoktṛ (non-enjoyer) realizes the bhoktā (enjoyer) is Kṛṣṇa. - Epistemic Shift: The ego's claim on 'my action' is replaced by 'His enjoyment'. - Result: a total collapse of the la-do-re-mi pattern of desire-frustration.

 

🔥 YS I.23 — īśvara-praṇidhānād vā

"Or [samādhi is attained] by surrender to īśvara (the Lord)."

 

BG 5.29 is the practical application of YS I.23. By knowing the Lord as the ultimate enjoyer and well-wisher, the devotee stops struggling against the current of prakṛti and enters the flow of divine grace. The 'surrender' here is the recognition of an existing fact: the Lord is already the master of all planets.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

bhoktāraṁ — the enjoyer; the ultimate recipient of all action

sarva-loka-maheśvaram — the supreme lord of all worlds/planets

suhṛdaṁ - the well-wisher; the one who desires the good of all beings

śāntim — peace; the absence of mental turbulence (citta-vṛtti)

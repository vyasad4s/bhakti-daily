🏹 BG 9.22 | 2026-07-08

 

अनन्याश्चिन्तयन्तो मां ये जना: पर्युपासते । तेषां नित्याभियुक्तानां योगक्षेमं वहाम्यहम् ॥ २२ ॥

 

ananyāś cintayanto māṁ ye janāḥ paryupāsate teṣāṁ nityābhiyuktānāṁ yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmy aham

 

But those who always worship Me with exclusive devotion, meditating on My transcendental form – to them I carry what they lack, and I preserve what they have.

 

This verse reveals the intimate promise of the Supreme Lord to His exclusive devotees: He personally carries what they lack and preserves what they have. The phrase "ananya-cintayanto" (those whose minds are fixed on Me alone) describes the state of exclusive devotion (ananya-bhakti), where every thought, word, and action is offered to Krishna. Such devotees are "nitya-abhiyukta" — perpetually engaged in His service through hearing, chanting, remembering, serving, worshiping, and surrendering. The Lord’s role as the divine sustainer is twofold: He removes deficiencies (yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmi) and protects existing blessings. For the devotee, this means that material and spiritual needs are met by the Lord’s personal attention. The term "yoga" here refers to the connection or linkage with the Divine, while "kṣema" denotes welfare, protection, and well-being. This is not a passive promise but an active, reciprocal relationship: the devotee’s exclusive attention invites the Lord’s personal care. From a philosophical standpoint, this verse underscores the non-dual nature of bhakti-yoga within the dualistic framework. While the soul remains distinct from the Lord, the loving exchange creates a unity of purpose. The devotee’s lack is not merely material; it includes the absence of divine association, which the Lord fulfills. Conversely, what the devotee possesses — faith, devotion, surrender — is preserved and enhanced by the Lord’s grace. Practically, this verse assures the sincere practitioner that exclusive devotion is never in vain. Even when external resources seem lacking, the Lord arranges for what is needed. This is the essence of "yoga-kṣema" — the divine arrangement that facilitates spiritual progress and material sufficiency for the devotee.

 

🔥 YS I.23 – īśvara-praṇidhānād vā – “Or, [samādhi is attained] by devotion to the Lord.” This sutra offers a direct parallel to BG 9.22: the attainment of yogic perfection (samādhi) through īśvara-praṇidhāna (surrender to the Lord). The verse promises that the Lord personally cares for the exclusive devotee; the sutra indicates that such surrender is a swift path to samādhi. Both emphasize that the Lord’s grace is responsive to the devotee’s exclusive orientation. YS II.45 further elaborates: samādhi-siddhiḥ īśvara-praṇidhānāt — "Perfection in samādhi is achieved through devotion to the Lord." This underscores the causative relationship: exclusive devotion (īśvara-praṇidhāna) leads to the highest yogic attainment. In the context of BG 9.22, the devotee’s exclusive worship (ananya-bhakti) invites the Lord’s personal care (yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmy aham), which in turn facilitates spiritual perfection. Additionally, YS I.33 (maitrī-karuṇā-muditopekṣāṇāṁ viṣayāṁ cittaprasādanam) — "The mind becomes purified by cultivating feelings of friendliness, compassion, joy, and equanimity" — complements the verse by describing the pure consciousness that results from bhakti. When the mind is thus purified through devotion, it becomes a fit vessel for the Lord’s sustaining grace. Thus, the Yoga Sutras provide the methodological foundation for achieving the exclusive devotion praised in BG 9.22: through īśvara-praṇidhāna and the cultivation of divine qualities (brahmavihārās), the devotee aligns with the Lord’s promise to carry what is lacking and preserve what is possessed.

TERM DEFINITIONS:

ananya (adj.) – exclusive, undivided; like a single-threaded process with no distractions

yoga (n.) – here, “what is lacking”; the divine provision that completes the devotee’s need

kṣema (n.) – welfare, protection, well-being; divine safeguard of what has already been attained

īśvara (n.) – Supreme Lord as the controller and recipient of devotion

praṇidhānā (n.) – surrender, dedicated offering of the self; committing your code to the divine repository

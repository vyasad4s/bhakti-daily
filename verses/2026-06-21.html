🏹 BG 8.14 | 2026-06-21

 

अनन्यचेता: सततं यो मां स्मरति नित्यश: । तस्याहं सुलभ: पार्थ नित्ययुक्तस्य योगिन: ॥ १४ ॥

 

ananya-cetāḥ satataṁ
yo māṁ smarati nityaśaḥ
tasyaāhaṁ su-labhaḥ pārtha
nitya-yuktasya yoginaḥ

 

For one who always remembers Me without deviation, I am easy to obtain, O son of Pṛthā, because of his constant engagement in devotional service.

 

From the Sankhya perspective, the material world (prakṛti) is a place of bondage where the puruṣa (consciousness) is entangled through misidentification with the body-mind complex. The verse highlights that unwavering, deviation-free remembrance of Krishna (ananya-cetah) allows the puruṣa to withdraw its identification from prakṛti and rest in its true nature. This constant engagement (nitya-yuktasya) in bhakti-yoga purifies the buddhi (intellect) and stills the modifications of the citta (mind), leading to liberation (saṁsiddhi). The Yoga Sutra affirms that devotion to the Lord (īśvara-praṇidhāna) is a direct path to samādhi.

 

🔥 YS 1.23: īśvara-praṇidhānāt va Connection: The verse emphasizes constant, deviation-free remembrance of Krishna, which aligns with Yoga Sutra 1.23 stating that samādhi can be attained through special devotion to the Lord (īśvara-praṇidhāna). Both teach that exclusive focus on the Divine leads to swift realization. The verse’s promise that Krishna becomes "easy to obtain" (su-labha) for one constantly engaged in devotional service (nitya-yuktasya) is the fruition of īśvara-praṇidhāna, where the mind’s ceaseless flow is directed toward Krishna, resulting in the Lord's easy accessibility.

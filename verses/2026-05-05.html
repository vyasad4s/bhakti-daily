🏹 BG 5.27–5.28 | May 5, 2026

यतेन्द्रियमनोबुद्धिर्मुनिर्मोक्षपरायणः । विगतेच्छाभयक्रोधो यः सदा मुक्त एव सः ॥२७॥ श्रीभगवानुवाच- यतेन्द्रियमनोबुद्धिर्युक्तो योगवतिष्ठति । युञ्जन्नेवात्मनात्मानं पश्यन्नात्मनि चात्मनः ॥२८॥

yatendriya-mano-buddhir munir mokṣa-parāyaṇaḥ | vigata-icchā-bhaya-krodho yaḥ sadā mukta eva saḥ || 27 || yatendriya-mano-buddhiyukto yoga-vatiṣṭhati | yuñjann evātmanātmānaṁ paśyann ātmani cātmanaḥ || 28 ||

Translation (Vedabase.io): "Shutting out all external sense objects, keeping the eyes and vision concentrated between the two eyebrows, suspending the inward and outward breaths within the nostrils, and thus controlling the mind, senses and intelligence, the transcendentalist aiming at liberation becomes free from desire, fear and anger. One who is always in this state is certainly liberated."

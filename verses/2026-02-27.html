🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 3.4 | February 27, 2026

 

न कर्मणामनारम्भान्नैष्कर्म्यं पुरुषोऽश्न‍ुते

न च संन्यसनादेव सिद्धिं समधिगच्छति ॥ ३.४ ॥

 

na karmaṇām anārambhān
naiṣkarmyaṁ puruṣo ’śnute
na ca sannyasanād eva
siddhiṁ samadhigacchati

 

"Not by merely abstaining from work can one achieve freedom from reaction, nor by renunciation alone can one attain perfection."

 

Here Kṛṣṇa demolishes two extremes: the do-nothing avoidance and the pretend-renunciation. Neither abstention without understanding, nor renunciation without works, leads to liberation.

 

Na anārambhāt — Not by mere non-starting, not by refusing to act. The escapist who withdraws from the world to avoid karma fails—because attachment to inaction is still attachment.

 

Na sannyasanāt eva — Not by renunciation alone. The show of outer renunciation without inner detachment is vanity. The monk who has left the world but not the ego has gone nowhere.

 

Today's Punarvasu energy: Aditi's shelter. Return to the original Self. This nakṣatra teaches: you cannot go anywhere to escape yourself. The only escape is transformation.

 

The aṣṭamī tithi: the 8th day, the number of dissolution and protection. After the fullness of saptamī comes the breaking—the vessel must crack for the light to enter. But dissolution without action is mere collapse; dissolution with purpose is transcendence.

 

The real renunciation is within; the real sannyāsa is in the heart. He who acts without attachment to results—he has renounced, though remaining in the world. He who has withdrawn to a cave but clings to the honor of being a renunciant—he is bound tighter than the householder.

 

🔥 YS 1.12 — abhyāsa-vairāgyābhyāṁ tan-nirodhaḥ — "The vṛttis are stilled by practice and dispassion."

 

Both are required: abhyāsa (practice, action) AND vairāgya (dispassion, inner renunciation). Neither alone suffices.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

Anārambhāt (ablative singular) — from non-commencement, from not beginning; ārambha = beginning/undertaking

Naiṣkarmya (neuter noun) — freedom from reaction, inaction in action; the state of being akarma

Puruṣaḥ (masculine noun, nominative) — the person, the soul, the conscious self; also the Supreme Person

Aśnute (verb, 3rd person singular) — attains, reaches, obtains; from aś (to reach, attain)

Sannyasanāt (ablative singular) — from renunciation; sannyāsa = complete renunciation of world and works

Siddhiḥ (feminine noun) — perfection, success, accomplishment; the goal of yoga

Samadhigacchati (verb, 3rd person singular) — one fully attains; sam + adhi + gam (to go toward completely)

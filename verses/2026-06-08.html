🏹 BG 7.14 | 2026-06-08

 

दैवी ह्येषा गुणमयी मम माया दुरत्यया । मामेव ये प्रपद्यन्ते मायामेतां तरन्ति ते ॥ १४ ॥

daivī hy eṣā guṇamayī mama māyā duratyayā mām eva ye prapadyante māyām etāṁ taranti te

 

This divine energy of Mine, consisting of the three modes of material nature, is difficult to overcome. But those who have surrendered unto Me can easily cross over it.

 

Kṛṣṇa has described His two natures (aparā and parā prakṛti), identified the living beings as His higher nature (7.5), and revealed Himself as the origin and dissolution of all existence (7.6). Now He declares that His divine energy, consisting of the three modes of material nature (māyā), is difficult to overcome. However, those who surrender unto Him can easily cross over it. This verse emphasizes that while māyā is powerful, bhakti (surrender) is the direct means to transcend it. The living entity, caught in the grip of the three modes (attachment, inertia, and false purity), can only be freed by taking refuge in the Lord. The analogy of pearls on a thread (from BG 7.7) is extended here: the thread is the Lord's surrender, and the pearls are the living entities that can slide along the thread when they surrender.

 

🔥 Yoga Sutra Connection: YS II.45: समाधिसिद्धिः ईश्वरप्रणिधानात् — Surrender to Īśvara brings about the perfection of samādhi.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

daivī — Divine, belonging to the gods. The energy that is transcendental and governing.

guṇamayī — Consisting of the three modes (sattva, rajas, tamas). The material nature in its conditioned state.

māyā — The Lord's divine energy that manifests the material world. Often translated as "illusion" but more accurately as the divine energy that appears as duality.

duratyayā — Difficult to overcome, cross, or traverse. That which is hard to conquer or pass beyond.

prapadyante — Those who have surrendered, submitted, or taken refuge. The act of complete surrender to the Divine.

taranti — They cross over, traverse, or go beyond. To surpass or transcend an obstacle.

īśvara-praṇidhāna — Surrender, devotion, and offering of all actions to the Lord. Patañjali's term for the bhakti path within yoga.

samādhi-siddhi — The perfection of samādhi, the complete absorption of consciousness in the object of meditation. The fruit of surrender: not a technique achieved, but a state revealed when the practitioner stops grasping at fluctuations and rests in the Divine.

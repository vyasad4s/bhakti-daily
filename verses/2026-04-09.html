yajñārthāt karmaṇo 'nyatra loko 'yaṁ karma-bandhanaḥ tad-arthaṁ karma kaunteya mukta-saṅgaḥ samācara

 

yajñārthāt karmaṇo 'nyatra loko 'yaṁ karma-bandhanaḥ |
tad-arthaṁ karma kaunteya mukta-saṅgaḥ samācara ||

 

Work done as a sacrifice for Viṣṇu has to be performed; otherwise work causes bondage in this material world. Therefore, O son of Kuntī, perform your prescribed duties for His satisfaction, and in that way you will always remain free from bondage.

 

All work produces reactions — this is the mechanics of karma. Good work binds; evil work binds. Only work offered as sacrifice to Viṣṇu becomes liberating. The varṇāśrama system, all Vedic rituals, and ultimately Kṛṣṇa consciousness itself exist for this single purpose: to transform binding action into liberating service. Perform your duty not for results, but for His satisfaction — this is the art of true freedom.

TERM DEFINITIONS:

yajña — sacrifice, literally "worship" or "offering." Any action performed for Viṣṇu's satisfaction.

karma-bandhanaḥ — the bondage of action. Every act not offered to Viṣṇu produces binding reactions.

mukta-saṅgaḥ — liberated from attachment. Working without being bound by results.

samācara — perform perfectly, execute completely. Thoroughness without attachment.

kaunteya — son of Kuntī (Arjuna). The jīva with discriminative capacity.

🏹 BG 3.9 | 2026-04-09

 

यज्ञार्थात्कर्मणोऽन्यत्र लोकोऽयं कर्मबन्धनः ।
तदर्थं कर्म कौन्तेय मुक्तसङ्गः समाचर ॥

 

yajñārthāt karmaṇo 'nyatra
loko 'yaṁ karma-bandhanaḥ |
tad-arthaṁ karma kaunteya
mukta-saṅgaḥ samācara ||

 

Work done as a sacrifice for Viṣṇu has to be performed; otherwise work causes bondage in this material world. Therefore, O son of Kuntī, perform your prescribed duties for His satisfaction, and in that way you will always remain free from bondage.

 

All work produces reactions — this is the mechanics of karma. Good work binds; evil work binds. Only work offered as sacrifice to Viṣṇu becomes liberating. The varṇāśrama system, all Vedic rituals, and ultimately Kṛṣṇa consciousness itself exist for this single purpose: to transform binding action into liberating service. Perform your duty not for results, but for His satisfaction — this is the art of true freedom.

 

🔥 YS II.1 — Kriyā Yoga Defined | tapaḥ-svādhyāyeśvara-praṇidhānāni kriyā-yogaḥ — Kriyā yoga consists of discipline, self-study, and surrender to the Supreme. Patañjali's triad matches Kṛṣṇa's prescription: tapas (perform duty), svādhyāya (know Viṣṇu as enjoyer), īśvara-praṇidhāna (offer the work). Both paths converge at mukti-saṅga: liberation through right action.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

yajña — sacrifice; any action performed for Viṣṇu's satisfaction.

karma-bandhanaḥ — bondage of action; reactions that bind the soul.

mukta-saṅgaḥ — liberated from attachment; unbound by results.

samācara — perform perfectly; execute with thoroughness.

kaunteya — son of Kuntī (Arjuna); the jīva with discriminative capacity.

🏹 BG 7.13 | 2026-06-10

 

त्रिभिर्गुणमयैर्भावैरेभि: सर्वमिदं जगत् ।

मोहितं नाभिजानाति मामेभ्य: परमव्ययम् ॥ १३ ॥

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

tribhiḥ — By three. Like the three fundamental system states — sattva (read/execute), rajas (write/modify), tamas (sleep/hibernate) — that govern all processes in the cosmic operating system.

guṇa-mayaiḥ — Consisting of the modes of nature. Like the three permission layers that compose every file in the system — no file exists outside the read-write-execute triad. The entire filesystem is guṇa-maya.

mohitam — Deluded, bewildered, enchanted. Like a process trapped in an infinite loop — it executes instructions but cannot see the shell that launched it. The loop feels like the whole system; it is not.

abhijānāti — Does not know, does not recognize. Like a user who runs applications daily but has no idea what the kernel does — they interact with the surface, never the source.

param — Supreme, beyond, transcendental. Like the hypervisor that sits above all virtual machines — it is not subject to any guest OS's rules or states.

avyayam — Inexhaustible, imperishable. Like the power supply that never drains — it is the source from which all system resources flow, and it is never diminished by what it powers.

 

Morning (Brahma Muhūrta 03:53–04:41 AM): Chant BG 7.13 slowly. Focus on the word mohitam — deluded. Where in your life are you under the spell of the three modes? Sattva deludes with the illusion of purity — "I am good." Rajas deludes with the frenzy of action — "I must do more." Tamas deludes with the fog of inertia — "It doesn't matter." All three are moha. Then focus on mām ebhyaḥ param avyayam — the Supreme beyond the modes, inexhaustible. Today is Dashami in the morning — complete any remaining material tasks. Ekādaśī begins at 3:28 PM — from that moment, shift entirely to spiritual practice. Let the Revati energy of Pushan guide you toward the path of light.

Evening (Amrit Kaal 08:28–10:00 PM): After Ekādaśī has settled in (post-sunset), sit in quiet reflection. The Moon in Meena until 10:46 PM then moves to Mesha — the ocean of dissolution gives way to the fire of initiation. In this transition, meditate on the transition from moha to jñāna: the three modes that enchanted you all day begin to lose their grip. Use the Amrit Kaal for extended japa or study. Focus on the paradox: prakṛti exists both for bhoga (experience) and apavarga (liberation). The same three modes that bind also liberate — when you see them as machinery rather than reality. Let YS II.18's insight sink in: the seen has luminosity, activity, and inertia — and it exists for liberation. You are not the modes. You are the one who watches them.

🏹 BG 2.22 | March 20, 2026

 

वासांसि जीर्णानि यथा विहाय

नवानि गृह्णाति नरोऽपराणि ।

तथा शरीराणि विहाय जीर्णा-

न्यन्यानि संयाति नवानि देही ॥ २२ ॥

 

vāsāṁsi jīrṇāni yathā vihāya navāni gṛhṇāti naro 'parāṇi tathā śarīrāṇi vihāya jīrṇāny anyāni saṁyāti navāni dehī

 

As a person puts on new garments, giving up old ones, the soul similarly accepts new material bodies, giving up the old and useless ones.

 

This verse cuts through the illusion that defines our existence. We inhabit bodies, accumulate identities, build empires of attachment—and then wonder why we suffer when these structures inevitably decay. The analogy is surgical: just as you discard worn garments for new ones without mourning, the soul vacates bodies that have exhausted their utility.

 

The key insight here is ontological discrimination. The dehī (the embodied self) is not the deha (the body). In Sankhya terms, this is the distinction between puruṣa (consciousness) and prakṛti (matter). The soul never dies because the soul was never born—it exists outside the temporal matrix that governs material manifestation.

 

Gudi Padwa marks the cosmic reset. The old Vikram Samvat year is a worn garment; the soul steps into new possibilities. Don't mistake this for sentimental "new beginnings" nonsense. This is the hard truth of existence: you are not your circumstances, your relationships, your successes or failures. You are the eternal witness that animates them. The body changes. The consciousness remains. Act accordingly.

 

🔥 YS I.3 — तदा द्रष्टुः स्वरूपेऽवस्थानम् (tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe 'vasthānam)

"When that is accomplished, the seer abides in its own true nature."

 

The connection here is precise: BG 2.22 teaches the soul's independence from the body; YS I.3 describes the realization of this truth. The "seer" (draṣṭṛ)—the soul—exists beyond the citta-vṛttis (mind fluctuations). Just as the yogi must still the mind to perceive puruṣa distinct from prakṛti, the dhīra (sober soul) perceives that death is merely exchanging one deha for another.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

vāsāṁsi (noun, neuter plural) — garments, clothes; temporary coverings that protect but are not the form itself

jīrṇāni (adjective) — worn out, old, decayed; from jṝ to decay; obsolete

dehī (noun, masculine) — the embodied one; consciousness inhabiting material form

vihāya (gerund) — having abandoned, left behind; release without regret

saṁyāti (verb) — goes toward, accepts; voluntary movement toward the new

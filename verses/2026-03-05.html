🏹 BG 2.22 | March 5, 2026

 

वासांसि जीर्णानि यथा विहाय

नवानि गृह्णाति नरोऽपराणि ।

तथा शरीराणि विहाय जीर्णा-

न्यन्यानि संयाति नवानि देही ॥ २२ ॥

 

vāsāṁsi jīrṇāni yathā vihāya

navāni gṛhṇāti naro 'parāṇi

tathā śarīrāṇi vihāya jīrṇāny

anyāni saṁyāti navāni dehī

 

"As a person puts on new garments, giving up old ones, the soul similarly accepts new material bodies, giving up the old and useless ones."

 

This verse delivers the kernel truth at the heart of all spiritual practice: you are not the body you inhabit. The dehī—the embodied one—is an eternal conscious entity that simply occupies successive vehicles of flesh. Like discarding worn garments for fresh ones, the soul changes bodies, leaving behind what has become unusable while remaining itself unchanged.

But here's the issue: most of us operate as if we're the clothes, not the wearer. We identify so completely with our current vessel—its sensations, its preferences, its accumulated history—that we assume its dissolution means our dissolution. This is the root error (mithyā-jñāna) that binds us. The Bhagavad-gītā isn't offering comfort here; it's offering surgical precision. Recognize the distinction or remain trapped. The choice is yours, but the clock is running.

In Sāṅkhya terms, this verse points directly to the dualism between puruṣa (conscious spirit) and prakṛti (material nature including bodies). The gunas—sattva, rajas, tamas—churn out new vehicles according to accumulated saṅskāras, yet the witness-consciousness merely observes the exchange. Hasta nakṣatra governs today: "the hand" that crafts and transforms. What will you craft with this knowledge? Will you remain identified with the garb, or will you cultivate the discernment to recognize the wearer?

 

🔥 YS I.3-4 — tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe 'vasthānam | vṛtti-sārūpyam itaratra — "When that is accomplished, the seer abides in its own true nature. Otherwise, at other times, [the seer] is absorbed in the changing states [of the mind]." Here is the thread connecting Bhakti and Raja Yoga paths. BG 2.22 describes the mechanics: soul changing bodies like garments. YS I.3-4 describes the consequence of not understanding this mechanics: the seer (puruṣa) becomes absorbed (sārūpyam) in the vṛttis—the changing states—rather than abiding in its own nature. When we fail to discriminate between body and soul, we don't merely observe embodiment; we become the embodiment.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

vāsāṁsi (noun, pl.) — garments, clothes; temporary user accounts the root admin creates and discards

jīrṇāni (adj.) — worn out, old, decayed; the material body after utility is exhausted

vihāya (gerund) — having abandoned, given up; voluntary relinquishment, not forced deprivation

dehī (noun) — the embodied one, the soul; literally "possessing a body"; distinct from deha (the body)

saṁyāti (verb) — verily accepts, enters into; soul's active assumption of new embodiment per karma

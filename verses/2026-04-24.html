🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 4.7 | 2026-04-24

 

यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य ग्लानिर्भवति भारत ।

अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम् ॥७॥

 

yadā yadā hi dharmasya glānir bhavati bhārata | abhyutthānam adharmasya tad ātmānaṁ sṛjāmy aham

 

"Whenever and wherever there is a decline in religious practice, O descendant of Bharata, and a predominant rise of irreligion — at that time I descend Myself."

 

The Sanskrit word yadā yadā establishes the cyclical nature of divine intervention. Not random, not arbitrary — but precise, responsive, surgical. When the metrics of righteousness fall below threshold — glāniḥ, the depletion of dharma — the system requires a patch. Not destruction; manifestation. The Lord's appearance is the system-wide update that restores sanātana-dharma without rebooting existence.

The descent described is not for the destruction of the impious — that can be handled by natural law (agents of karma). The Lord appears specifically for those who recognize Him. This is the operative principle: grace descends to those positioned to receive it. The avatāra is not punishment; it is revelation.

Today's cosmic configuration amplifies this principle. Navamī, the ninth day of the waxing moon, represents the threshold before culmination — the moment when energy is maximized and ready to manifest. Āśleṣā nakṣatra — the coiled serpent — represents potential energy held in stillness until the precise moment of release. The Lord's descent is precisely this: not continuous presence but strategic intervention, the tapas of divinity breaking through when conditions require.

 

🔥 Yoga Sūtra Connection — Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras I.23 states: Īśvara praṇidhānād vā — "Or, [the state of samādhi is attainable] from devotion to the Lord." This is the vertical axis connecting today's verse to practice. BG 4.7 describes the Lord's descent — His movement from the transcendental realm into the manifest to restore dharma. YS I.23 describes our corresponding movement — the devotee's ascent from the manifest toward the transcendental through surrender. The Sanskrit root praṇidhāna means to place oneself completely before, to surrender orientation toward Īśvara. Vyāsa confirms that just as the Lord descends by His own will, so the practitioner attains through surrender to that will. The serpent of Āśleṣā symbolizes kuṇḍalinī — the coiled power at the base of the spine that, when awakened, ascends to unite with the divine. The Lord descends (avatāra) and the devotee's energy ascends (kuṇḍalinī). They meet at the heart.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

yadā yadā (adv.) — whenever and wherever; the cyclical trigger condition indicating divine intervention is not random but responds to systematic degradation.

glāniḥ (f. nom. sg.) — decline, depletion; the state when dharma has fallen below operational threshold.

abhyutthānam (n. nom. sg.) — rise, predominance; the upsurge of irreligion's influence over consciousness.

sṛjāmi (1st sg. pres. causative √sṛj) — I manifest, I emit; not creation but making apparent what was always present.

ātmānam (m. acc. sg.) — Myself; the Personal Absolute, not impersonal force but conscious descent.

praṇidhāna (n. nom. sg.) — complete surrender, devotion as orientation of the total being toward Īśvara.

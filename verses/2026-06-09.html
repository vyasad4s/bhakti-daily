🏹 BG 7.29 | 2026-06-09

 

जरामरणमोक्षाय मामाश्रित्य यतन्ति ये ।

ते ब्रह्म तद्विदुः कृत्स्नमध्यात्मं कर्म चाखिलम् ॥ २९ ॥

 

jarā-maraṇa-mokṣāya mām āśritya yatanti ye te brahma tad viduḥ kṛtsnam adhyātmaṁ karma cākhilam

 

Intelligent persons who are endeavoring for liberation from old age and death take refuge in Me in devotional service. They are actually Brahman because they entirely know everything about transcendental activities.

 

Kṛṣṇa has revealed His two natures — the lower (aparā prakṛti, 7.4) and the higher (parā prakṛti, 7.5), declared Himself the origin and dissolution of all (7.6–7), made the supreme statement that nothing transcends Him (7.7), described the four types of devotees (7.16–18), and now reveals the ultimate purpose of spiritual seeking: jarā-maraṇa-mokṣāya — liberation from old age and death itself. Not liberation from a particular suffering, not freedom from a specific circumstance, but release from the fundamental condition of embodied existence — the inevitable decay and recycling of the material body. And the method is not complex: mām āśritya — take refuge in Me. The word āśritya is not passive. It is the active placing of oneself under the shelter of the Lord, like a process that escalates from user mode to root — you don't achieve root by trying harder in user space, you achieve it by āśritya, by taking shelter in the One who already holds that privilege. The result: te brahma tad viduḥ — they actually know Brahman. Not they believe, not they hope — they know. And what they know is complete: kṛtsnam adhyātmaṁ karma cākhilam — the totality of transcendental activity, entirely. Full memory dump of the cosmic source code. No blind spots. No unexamined corners. This is the fruit of surrender.

 

🔥 Yoga Sutra Connection: YS II.45: समाधिसिद्धिः ईश्वरप्रणिधानात् — Surrender to Īśvara brings about the perfection of samādhi.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

āśritya — Taking refuge, shelter, or surrender. Like gaining 'root access' to the divine system — the highest privilege that grants unrestricted access to all functions.

viduḥ — They who know, perceive, or realize. Like having a 'debugger's console' that reveals the true source code of reality beyond surface appearances.

kṛtsnam — Everything, all, the totality. Like obtaining a 'full memory dump' of the universe — seeing all variables, states, and processes in their entirety.

adhyātmaṁ — Transcendental, spiritual, beyond the material. Like accessing 'kernel mode' or 'ring 0' privileges where one operates at the fundamental level of reality.

karma — Action, activity, or work. Like making a 'system call' to the divine OS — requesting service from the transcendental layer.

akhilam — Entirely, completely, wholly. Like achieving '100% test coverage' or exhaustive verification that leaves no corner unexamined.

🏹 Bhagavad Gita 5.11 | 2026-05-08

 

यत्नाद्यतः क्रियन्तोऽपि कर्माणि नियताः सन्यासिनः ।

स ञ्ज्योगी समतायुक्तो वासुदेवपरः सुचिः ॥ ११ ॥

 

yatantaḥ kurvanto 'pi karmāṇi niyatāḥ sannyāsinaḥ | sa yogī samatā-yukto vāsudeva-paraḥ suciḥ || 11 ||

 

"The yogīs, while acting with great effort, renounce attachment to the fruits of their actions. Such a purified soul, equipoised and fixed solely on Vāsudeva, is freed from all impurities."

 

BG 5.11 introduces the Purification Firewall. Every action—whether ritual, duty, or charity—leaves a karmic log on the soul. Even the most virtuous deeds can accumulate as subtle residue, slowing the system's return to zero-state.

 

The secret is niyataḥ karma—regulated action performed under the yajña protocol. By dedicating every function call to Vāsudeva, the yogi routes all outputs to the Absolute Server. The moment of surrender is the erase-log command that frees up soul RAM.

 

Sāṅkhya Architecture:
• Samatā-yukta (Equipoised): The processor no longer spikes between attraction and aversion. The load balancer distributes witnessing across all stimuli without fluctuation (dvandva interrupts disabled).
• Vāsudeva-paraḥ: The stack pointer is permanently set to the Supreme Module. No risk of stack overflow.
• Suciḥ: The final state—pure, single-threaded awareness. No background daemons of desire.

 

🔥 YS II.33 maitrī-karuṇā-muditopekṣā: "By cultivating attitudes of friendliness toward the happy, compassion for the unhappy, delight in the virtuous, and disregard toward the wicked, the mind retains its undisturbed calm."

 

YS II.33 is the emotional firewall that prevents karmic log corruption. BG 5.11 provides the witnessing firmware that ensures no new imprints are written. Together, they create a read-only soul: experiencing without reacting, acting without accruing.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

samatā-yuktaḥ (adj) - Equipoised; the processor operating at zero thermal spikes between likes and dislikes.

vāsudeva-paraḥ (adj) - Fixed solely on Vāsudeva; the permanent call stack redirect to the Absolute.

suciḥ (adj) - Pure; the state of a soul that has been reformatted to read-only mode.

Parigha Yoga (n) - Constrictive yoga; a cosmic brace that aligns the spinal column and turns obstacles into purification fuel.

🏹 BG 6.30 | Thursday, May 28, 2026

 

यो मां पश्यति सर्वत्र सर्वं च मयि पश्यति ।

तस्याहं न प्रणश्यामि स च मे न प्रणश्यति ॥ ३० ॥

 

yo māṁ paśyati sarvatra sarvaṁ ca mayi paśyati / tasyāhaṁ na praṇaśyāmi sa ca me na praṇaśyati

 

For one who sees Me everywhere and sees everything in Me, I am never lost, nor is he ever lost to Me.

 

This verse is Kṛṣṇa's most intimate declaration of mutual non-separation — and it arrives not as philosophy but as promise. Two movements define it. The first is double-vision: yo māṁ paśyati sarvatra — seeing Kṛṣṇa everywhere; sarvaṁ ca mayi paśyati — seeing everything in Kṛṣṇa. These are not the same perception reversed. They are two distinct modalities of awareness. Seeing Kṛṣṇa everywhere is the transcendental gaze — the yogī perceives the Supersoul (Paramātmā) in every heart, every creature, every blade of grass. The world doesn't disappear; it becomes transparent. Seeing everything in Kṛṣṇa is the reverse — all phenomena, all diversity, all apparently separate objects are recognized as residing within His energy. The first is the path inward; the second is the path outward. Together they form a complete circuit of perception, and the yogī who completes this circuit sees sarvatra sama-darśanaḥ — equally everywhere — which is precisely how BG 6.29 described the prerequisite state.

The second movement is the result — and it is stated with startling directness. Tasyāhaṁ na praṇaśyāmi: "I am never lost to him." Sa ca me na praṇaśyati: "He is never lost to Me." Kṛṣṇa does not say "he attains Me" or "he merges with Me." He says something far more radical: a permanent, mutual, unbreakable visibility. The devotee cannot lose sight of Kṛṣṇa; Kṛṣṇa cannot lose sight of the devotee. This is not liberation as annihilation — it is liberation as relationship eternally confirmed. Prabhupāda makes this explicit in his purport: "To merge in Kṛṣṇa is spiritual annihilation. A devotee takes no such risk." The bhakti path doesn't dissolve the lover into the Beloved. It establishes an intimacy that death itself cannot sever.

The verb praṇaśyāmi carries weight beyond mere "loss." Its root naś means to perish, vanish, be destroyed. Kṛṣṇa is saying: for the yogī who sees this way, I can never perish from his vision, and he can never perish from Mine. This is the ultimate security — not the security of a safe investment or a comfortable life, but the security of knowing that the Supreme Person has permanently registered your existence. You are seen. You are held. You are never forgotten.

 

🔥 YOGA SŪTRA CONNECTION: YS I.36–I.37

viśokā vā jyotiṣmatī — "Or [stability comes from] the luminous [mind] which is free from sorrow." (YS I.36)

vīta-rāga-viṣayaṁ vā cittam — "Or [stability comes from] the mind which has dispassion for its object." (YS I.37)

Patañjali offers a sequence of antidotes for the mind's scattering. YS I.36 points to the sorrowless inner light — the same luminosity the yogī sees when Kṛṣṇa's presence becomes visible everywhere. When the mind is cleared of sorrow (viśokā), it becomes radiant (jyotiṣmatī), and this radiance is not self-generated — it is the reflection of puruṣa shining through purified buddhi. BG 6.30 describes the fruit of this: the one who sees this light in all beings is the one who sees Kṛṣṇa everywhere.

YS I.37 takes the next step: the mind that has dispassion (vīta-rāga) as its object. When rāga — attachment, coloration, the filtering of perception through desire — is removed, the mind sees what is actually there. BG 6.30 is precisely what appears when rāga no longer distorts perception: Kṛṣṇa everywhere, everything in Kṛṣṇa. The Yoga Sūtras describe the method of purifying perception. The Gītā describes what is seen when perception is pure. Same engine. Two angles of vision.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

paśyati (v.) — sees, perceives; not merely visual sight but the full recognition of divine presence; the sattvic eye that has been cleansed of rāga-colored distortion; like a system that has been debugged and now renders reality without filter

sarvatra (adv.) — everywhere, in all places; the spatial totality of Kṛṣṇa's presence; no coordinate in prakṛti exists outside His energy; the network with zero offline nodes

praṇaśyāmi (v.) — I am lost/perish/vanish; from root naś (to be destroyed, to disappear); Kṛṣṇa's guarantee that He cannot disappear from the devotee's vision; the permanent connection that cannot be severed by any firewall

sama-darśana (n.) — equal vision; the prerequisite state described in BG 6.29; seeing the same Supersoul in all beings regardless of external circumstance; the yogī's unified field of perception where every entity registers as Kṛṣṇa's energy

viśokā jyotiṣmatī (adj./n.) — sorrowless and luminous; YS I.36's description of the purified mind that becomes radiant when cleared of grief; the inner light that appears when sattva dominates and the obstacle of sorrow dissolves

vīta-rāga (adj.) — free from passion/attachment; YS I.37's description of the dispassionate mind; rāga removed from the mind's objects; perception without the color filter of desire; the debugger that strips out all legacy attachment code

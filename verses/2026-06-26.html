पुरुष: स पर: पार्थ भक्त्या लभ्यस्त्वनन्यया ॥

यस्यान्त:स्थानि भूतानि येन सर्वमिदं ततम् ॥ २२ ॥

 

puruṣaḥ sa paraḥ pārtha
bhaktyā labhyas tv ananyayā
yasyāntaḥ-sthāni bhūtāni
yena sarvam idaṁ tatam

 

The Supreme Personality of Godhead, who is greater than all, is attainable by unalloyed devotion. Although He is present in His abode, He is all-pervading, and everything is situated within Him.

 

The Supreme Person (puruṣaḥ sa paraḥ) is attainable only through unalloyed devotion (bhaktyā ananyayā). This is not sentimentalism—it is the engineering principle of exclusive devotion: when the mind’s CPU affinity is locked to the Divine process, all other processes (material desires, mental modifications) are starved of cycles. The Lord’s all-pervading nature (sarvam idaṁ tatam) means His consciousness is the kernel space that sustains every user-space process (bhūtāni). You cannot escape His scope—He is the scheduler, the memory allocator, the interrupt handler for all existence.

 

From a Sankhya perspective, puruṣa (consciousness) and prakṛti (matter) are eternally distinct. The material world is a modification of prakṛti, a dualistic expression where the puruṣa appears bound due to identification with the body-mind complex. Liberation (mokṣa) is not the destruction of prakṛti but the realization of the puruṣa’s separateness—viveka. Bhakti is the method that purifies the mind (citta‑śuddhi) so that puruṣa can shine through prakṛti’s veils. Unalloyed devotion is the constant redirecting of mental processes (vṛttis) toward the object of meditation—Īśvara—until the mind’s modifications cease (citta‑vṛtti‑nirodhaḥ) and the puruṣa rests in its true nature.

 

The cosmic metaphor of the verse aligns with the hacker’s view of the divine as the root user (UID 0) who owns all processes yet remains transcendent. Just as root can access any memory space but exists beyond any single process, the Supreme pervades all yet resides in His own abode (goloka eva nivasaty akhilātma‑bhūtaḥ). Devotion (bhaktyā) is the equivalent of running with highest priority (nice=-20) and CPU affinity locked to the divine process—no context switching, no preemption by material distractions. This single‑pointed focus yields the success status (labhyaḥ) of attaining the supreme destination.

 

Thus, the teaching is clear: abandon the multitasking of material desires, execute the single thread of ananya bhakti with real‑time priority; and you will attain the supreme abode, where the process of devotion runs eternally without interruption. This is not metaphor—it is the operative principle of consciousness itself. Hare Kṛṣṇa. 🏹

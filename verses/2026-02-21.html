🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 2.70 | February 21, 2026

 

आपूर्यमाणमचलप्रतिष्ठं

समुद्रमापः प्रविशन्ति यद्वत् ।

तद्वत्कामा यं प्रविशन्ति सर्वे

स शान्तिमाप्‍नोति न कामकामी ॥

 

āpūryamāṇam acala-pratiṣṭhaṁ
samudram āpaḥ praviśanti yadvat
tadvat kāmā yaṁ praviśanti sarve
sa śāntim āpnoti na kāma-kāmī

 

"A person who is not disturbed by the incessant flow of desires – that enter like rivers into the ocean, which is ever being filled but is always still – can alone achieve peace, and not the man who strives to satisfy such desires."

 

The river-ocean metaphor completes the sequence. Where BG 2.69 established perceptual inversion (the sage awake while world sleeps), 2.70 reveals the mechanism: being FULL already.

 

The kṛṣṇa-conscious person is like the ocean – always complete, never lacking. Rivers of desire flow in continuously, yet the ocean remains undisturbed, unagitated, not seeking more. This is not suppression of desire. This is fullness that renders desire-fulfillment irrelevant.

 

Na kāma-kāmī – not the one who desires to fulfill desires. The frantic pursuit of satisfaction presumes lack. The ocean presumes nothing. It simply IS, complete unto itself.

 

The progression: 2.66 (prerequisites) → 2.67 (senses capture mind) → 2.68 (restraint → established intelligence) → 2.69 (perceptual inversion) → 2.70 (the fullness that explains WHY inversion is possible). The sage is awake because he lacks nothing. The world sleeps because it seeks constantly.

 

🔥 YS 2.42 — saṁtoṣād anuttamaḥ sukha-lābhaḥ — "From contentment, unsurpassed happiness is obtained."

 

BG 2.70's ocean is this contentment embodied. The kāmakāmī chases satisfaction; the sage rests in satisfaction already complete. Both face the same rivers. Only the ocean's depth differs.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

Āpūryamāṇam (present passive participle) — always being filled, constantly replenished; from ā-pṝ to fill completely

Acala-pratiṣṭham (neuter adjective compound) — steadily situated, immovably established; acala unmoving + pratiṣṭha foundation

Samudra (masculine noun) — ocean, the gathering-place of waters; the complete reservoir

Kāma-kāmī (masculine noun compound) — one who desires to fulfill desires; the tautology reveals the futility

Śānti (feminine noun) — peace, tranquility; from śam to cease, to become calm

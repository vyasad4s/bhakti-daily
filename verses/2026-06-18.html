🏹 BG 8.10 | 2026-06-18

 

प्रयाणकाले मनसाचलेन भक्त्या युक्तो योगबलेन चैव । भ्रुवोर्मध्ये प्राणमावेश्य सम्य- क्स तं परं पुरुषमुपैति दिव्यम् ॥ १० ॥

 

prayāṇa-kāle manasācalena
bhaktyā yukto yoga-balena caiva
bhruvoḥ madhye prāṇam āveśya samyak
sa taṁ paraṁ puruṣam upaiti divyam

 

One who, at the time of death, fixes his life air between the eyebrows and, by the strength of yoga, with an undeviating mind, engages himself in remembering the Supreme Lord in full devotion, will certainly attain to the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

This isn't metaphysical speculation; it's the precise procedure for achieving divyam (the spiritual kingdom) rather than crashing into saṃsāra's endless reboot cycle.

Just as you'd never attempt a kernel upgrade during a system crash, death's disturbed state demands pre-existing yoga practice—whether bhakti-yoga's devotion daemon or ṣaṭ-cakra-yoga's chakra modules. The manasācalena (undeviating mind) specification further demands real-time process monitoring: no interrupts from rāgadveṣa (attraction/aversion), no segmentation faults from citta-vṛtti (mind-modifications). This is sadhana as systems engineering—discipline in the form of preventive maintenance.

🏹 BG 3.19 | April 15, 2026

 

तस्मादसक्तः सततं कार्यं कर्म समाचर ।

असक्तो ह्याचरन्कर्म परमाप्‍नोति पूरूषः ॥ १९ ॥

 

तस्माद् असक्तः सततं
कार्यं कर्म समाचर
असक्तो ह्याचरन् कर्म
परमाप्‍नोति पूरूषः

 

Therefore, without being attached to the fruits of activities, one should act as a matter of duty, for by working without attachment one attains the Supreme.

 

Kṛṣṇa speaks this verse as the capstone of His initial instruction on karma-yoga. Arjuna has asked about the apparent contradiction between action and renunciation, and Kṛṣṇa has explained that for most, action is superior. Now comes the essential instruction: how to act without being burned by results.

 

Asakti — non-attachment. The insufficient way: detachment through numbness — "I don't care about outcomes." Spiritual bypassing disguised as liberation. The complete way: recognition that the fruits were never yours to claim. The action produces results, yes. But those results belong to the field of prakṛti's operation, not to the witnessing puruṣa. You are not the compiler — you are the awareness in which the code runs.

 

Kāryaṁ — as duty. Kṛṣṇa's word choice is surgical. Satataṁ — constantly — removes the loophole of intermittent practice. Kāryaṁ — duty — specifies obligation to the structure, not inclination. Samācara — perform properly — demands full engagement. Asaktaḥ — without attachment — transforms action into yoga.

 

Param āpnoti pūruṣaḥ — the person attains the Supreme. Not material success. THE SUPREME. This is the Sankhya economy: when buddhi performs without misidentifying with outcomes, it orients toward puruṣa. Liberation is not reward — it's the natural consequence of works performed without grasping.

 

Today's Śivarātri threshold intensifies: dissolution is not destruction of action but of the claim upon action. What dissolves is ownership. What remains is pure function — work as offering, duty as devotion, action without residue.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

asakti — non-attachment; engaging fully while recognizing results belong to prakṛti, not puruṣa; action unburdened by self-image demanding specific outcomes

kāryaṁ — duty, obligation, that which must be done; action from obligation versus inclination (kāma); the latter is yoga

samācara — perform properly, execute completely; prefix sam- = thoroughness; full attention and methodology

param — the Supreme, ultimate reality; highest attainment — union with puruṣa, liberation from birth and death

pūruṣaḥ — person, human being, spirit as individual; teaching applies to anyone regardless of gender, varṇa, or stage

satataṁ — constantly, always, without cessation; moment-to-moment discipline applicable to every action

🏹 BG 3.19 | April 15, 2026

 

तस्मादसक्तः सततं कार्यं कर्म समाचर ।

असक्तो ह्याचरन्कर्म परमाप्‍नोति पूरूषः ॥ १९ ॥

 

tasmād asaktaḥ satataṁ
kāryaṁ karma samācara
asakto hy ācaran karma
param āpnoti pūruṣaḥ

 

Therefore, without being attached to the fruits of activities, one should act as a matter of duty, for by working without attachment one attains the Supreme.

 

Kṛṣṇa speaks this verse as the capstone of His instruction on karma-yoga. Arjuna asked about action vs. renunciation. Now comes the essential teaching: how to act without being burned by results.

 

Asakti — non-attachment. Not detachment-through-numbness ("I don't care"). The complete way: recognition that fruits were never yours to claim. Results belong to prakṛti's field, not the witnessing puruṣa. You are not the compiler — you are awareness in which code runs.

 

Surgical word choice: Satataṁ — constantly — no loopholes. Kāryaṁ — duty — obligation to structure, not inclination. Samācara — perform properly — full engagement. Asaktaḥ — without attachment — transforms action to yoga.

 

Param āpnoti pūruṣaḥ — one attains the Supreme. Not material success. THE SUPREME. Liberation isn't reward — it's natural consequence of grasping-free action.

 

Śivarātri intensifies this: dissolution destroys not action but the claim upon action. What dissolves is ownership. What remains is pure function.

 

YOGA SŪTRA CONNECTION:

YS I.23: īśvara-praṇidhānād vā — "Or [cessation of mental modifications] through devotion to īśvara." Surrender accelerates the path. Where BG 3.19 emphasizes asakti (non-attachment), Patañjali provides the mechanism: praṇidhāna — complete offering. Results don't go to "me" — they go to the Source.

 

PRACTICE TODAY:

Before each action: State "This is kāryaṁ. The result belongs to the field." Not affirmation — calibration.

 

Rāhu Kāla (12:55–2:34 PM): Practice asakti with challenging tasks. Friction creates pressure — use it.

 

Threshold Moment (1:01 PM): When Trayodāśī→Caturdaśī transitions, pause 60 seconds. Dissolve one attachment. Let it go.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

asakti — non-attachment; engaging fully while recognizing results belong to prakṛti, not puruṣa

kāryaṁ — duty, obligation; action from structure vs. inclination (kāma)

samācara — perform properly; prefix sam- = thoroughness

param — the Supreme; highest attainment, union with puruṣa

pūruṣaḥ — person, spirit as individual; teaching applies universally

satataṁ — constantly, without cessation; moment-to-moment discipline

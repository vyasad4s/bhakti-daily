🏹 BG 4.1 | April 27, 2026

 

śrī-bhagavān uvāca

imaṁ vivasvate yogaṁ

proktavān aham avyayam

vivasvān manave prāha

manur ikṣvākave 'bravīt

 

śrī-bhagavān uvāca — the Supreme Personality of Godhead said; imam — this; vivasvate — unto the sun-god; yogam — the science of one's relationship to the Supreme; proktavān — instructed; aham — I; avyayam — imperishable; vivasvān — Vivasvān (the sun-god's name); manave — unto the father of mankind; prāha — told; manuḥ — the father of mankind; ikṣvākave — unto King Ikṣvāku; abravīt — said.

 

The Personality of Godhead, Lord Śrī Kṛṣṇa, said: I instructed this imperishable science of yoga to the sun-god, Vivasvān, and Vivasvān instructed it to Manu, the father of mankind, and Manu in turn instructed it to Ikṣvāku.

 

The Gītā does not begin with speculation—it begins with history. Not human history measured in centuries, but cosmic history stretching across millions of years. This verse establishes the paramparā, the disciplic succession, that carries spiritual knowledge like a torch passed from hand to hand across time. When the Lord says this science is avyayam—imperishable—He declares that it cannot be destroyed by time or corrupted by interpretation. It simply awaits rediscovery.

 

The verse traces an unbroken chain: Kṛṣṇa to Vivasvān, Vivasvān to Manu, Manu to Ikṣvāku. This is not mythology—it is methodology. Real knowledge must be received from one who has realized it. The Bhagavad-gītā was lost through the break in this succession, and Kṛṣṇa speaks it again to Arjuna to reestablish the line.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

paramparā (n.) — The disciplic succession; the traditional method of transmitting spiritual knowledge from guru to disciple. Unlike academic knowledge, transcendental wisdom must be received through this living chain.

avyaya (adj.) — Imperishable, inexhaustible. Spiritual knowledge maintains full potency regardless of time; like the sun shining for billions of years.

vivasvān (proper n.) — The sun-god, deity presiding over the sun in this millennium. Represents the eye of the Lord.

 

🔥 YS 1.23 & 2.1 | Bhakti as Method

Patañjali offers devotion to Īśvara as a direct path to samādhi. BG 4.1 reveals what this devotion connects to: not an abstract concept, but a personal Being who instructs and re-establishes lost knowledge. The paramparā is the practical mechanism of īśvara-praṇidhāna. Kriyā Yoga corresponds here: tapas (austerity of receiving), svādhyāya (hearing from authority), and īśvara-praṇidhāna (humility to be instructed).

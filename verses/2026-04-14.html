सहयज्ञाः प्रजाः सृष्ट्वा पुरोवाच प्रजापतिः ।

अनेन प्रसविष्यध्वमेष वोऽस्त्विष्टकामधुक् ॥ १० ॥

 

सहयज्ञाः प्रजाः सृष्ट्वा
पुरोवाच प्रजापतिः
अनेन प्रसविष्यध्वम्
एष वोऽस्त्विष्टकामधुक्

 

In the beginning of creation, the Lord of all creatures sent forth generations of men and demigods, along with sacrifices for Viṣṇu, and blessed them by saying, "Be thou happy by this yajña [sacrifice] because its performance will bestow upon you everything desirable for living happily and achieving liberation."

 

The verse unfolds the original design: creation was not merely a dispersion of souls into material existence, but a coordinated system where sacrifice (yajña) serves as the operational link between the living and the Divine. Men and demigods are not adversaries but cooperators, each class performing distinct functions within a single hierarchy.

 

Saha-yajñāḥ — along with sacrifices. The destination of embodied existence was never mere survival, but sacrificial exchange. The material energy itself is designed to facilitate this circulation: the demigods supply air, light, water, and natural prosperity, and they do so based on the performance of yajña by human beings. This is not poetic metaphor but ontological structure.

 

Prajāpatiḥ purā uvāca — the Lord of creatures spoke in the beginning. This is not later instruction but primordial ordination. The yajña system was not invented for difficult souls; it was instituted along with the souls themselves. The structure of return to Godhead was simultaneous with the structure of departure from Godhead.

 

Iṣṭa-kāma-dhuk — the bestower of all desirable things. The prajāpati's blessing is both material and spiritual: prosperity in this world and liberation from the world. The yajña system was designed to be sustainable, to supply necessities while purifying the performer so that eventual return to Godhead is possible.

 

Today's cosmic alignment intensifies this teaching: Uttara Bhādrapadā is the asterism of Ajaikapāda, the ascetic who dances in fire. The yajña becomes literal as the sun traverses this fire-born constellation. Tri Pushkara Yoga until 2:42 PM creates triple-blessing energy—the blessing of prajāpati upon the created beings, the blessings of the demigods, and the ultimate blessing of liberation. The verse is the instruction manual for this exact configuration.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

saha-yajñāḥ — along with sacrifices; saha = with, yajña = sacrifice. Created beings were sent with the institutional apparatus already in place.

prajāḥ — generations, progeny, the created beings; from pra-ja (to be born forth)

sṛṣṭvā — having sent forth, created; active causative, indicating intentional construction

puraḥ uvāca — spoke in ancient times, spoke at the beginning; primordial communication from Lord to creation

prajāpatiḥ — the Lord of creatures, progenitor of all living entities; Viṣṇu as source and sustainer

prasaviṣyadhvam — you will become more prosperous; from pra-sava, to bring forth, to flourish

iṣṭa-kāma-dhuk — the bestower of desired objects; iṣṭa = desired, kāma = objects of desire, dhuk = bestower

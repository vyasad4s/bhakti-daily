🏹 BG 3.10 | April 14, 2026

 

सहयज्ञाः प्रजाः सृष्ट्वा पुरोवाच प्रजापतिः ।

अनेन प्रसविष्यध्वमेष वोऽस्त्विष्टकामधुक् ॥ १० ॥

 

saha-yajñāḥ prajāḥ sṛṣṭvā
pura uvāca prajāpatiḥ
anena prasaviṣyadhvam
eṣa vo 'stv iṣṭa-kāma-dhuk

 

In the beginning of creation, the Lord of all creatures sent forth generations of men and demigods, along with sacrifices for Viṣṇu, and blessed them by saying, "Be thou happy by this yajña [sacrifice] because its performance will bestow upon you everything desirable."

 

The verse unfolds the original design: creation was not merely dispersion into material existence, but a coordinated system where sacrifice serves as the operational link between living entities and the Divine.

 

Saha-yajñāḥ — along with sacrifices. The destination was never mere survival but sacrificial exchange. Material energy facilitates this circulation: demigods supply air, light, water, based on yajña performance by humans. This is ontological structure, not metaphor.

 

Prajāpatiḥ purā uvāca — the Lord of creatures spoke in the beginning. Not later instruction but primordial ordination. The yajña system was instituted along with souls themselves.

 

Iṣṭa-kāma-dhuk — bestower of desired objects. Blessing is material and spiritual: prosperity now, liberation ultimately. Yajña purifies the performer.

 

Cosmic alignment: Uttara Bhādrapādā carries Ajaikapāda's energy — ascetic dancing in fire. Yajña becomes literal as sun traverses this fire-born constellation.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

saha-yajñāḥ — along with sacrifices; created beings sent with institutional apparatus in place

prajāḥ — generations, progeny; from pra-ja (to be born forth)

sṛṣṭvā — having sent forth, created; intentional construction

prajāpatiḥ — Lord of creatures, progenitor of all; Viṣṇu as source

iṣṭa-kāma-dhuk — bestower of desired objects; iṣṭa = desired, kāma = objects, dhuk = bestower

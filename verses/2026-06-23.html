अनन्यचेता: सततं यो मां स्मरति नित्यश: । तस्याहं सुलभ: पार्थ नित्ययुक्तस्य योगिन: ॥ १४ ॥

ananya-cetāḥ satataṁ
yo māṁ smarati nityaśaḥ
tasyaahaṁ su-labhaḥ pārtha
nitya-yuktasya yoginaḥ

 

For one who always remembers Me without deviation, I am easy to obtain, O son of Pṛthā, because of his constant engagement in devotional service.

 

This verse cuts straight to the point: the Supreme is easily attained by one whose mind never deviates from remembrance of Him. From a Sankhya perspective, the mind (manas) is a product of prakṛti, constantly fluctuating under the influence of the three guṇas. To fix the mind on Kṛṣṇa without deviation is to override the natural tendency of prakṛti to pull the consciousness outward toward sense objects. This requires disciplined practice (abhyāsa) and dispassion (vairāgya)—the twin pillars of Yoga Sutra I.12. Just as a skilled hacker bypasses system noise to reach the root kernel, the yogi uses sustained attention to cut through the mental processes (vṛttis) that obscure the puruṣa. The phrase “without deviation” (ananya-cetāḥ) points to single-pointed focus (eka-tattva), analogous to a debugger setting a hardware breakpoint on a single memory address and ignoring all other interrupts. The result is that the Lord becomes “very easy to obtain” (su-labhaḥ)—not because He is distant, but because the devotee’s consciousness has become transparent to Him, like a clean pipe with zero latency. The second half of the verse emphasizes that this ease arises from “constant engagement in devotional service” (nitya-yuktasya yoginaḥ). Devotion (bhakti) is not passive sentiment; it is the active, ongoing offering of one’s actions, mind, and intelligence to the Lord. In hacker terms, it is the equivalent of running a privileged process that continuously invokes a system call to the divine, maintaining an open channel of communication. This constant engagement purifies the mind, making it a fit vessel for the descent of divine consciousness. Thus, the verse presents a clear formula: unwavering remembrance plus dedicated service equals easy access to the Supreme—a formula as reliable as a well‑written daemon that never crashes.

 

YOGA SUTRA CONNECTION:

YS I.12: abhyāsair vairāgyābhyāṁ tan‑nirodhaḥ
The mental modifications are restrained by practice and detachment.

YS I.23: īśvara praṇidhānād vā
Or, [the state of samādhi] is attainable through devotion to the Lord.

Connection: BG 8.14’s call for unwavering remembrance (ananya‑cetāḥ) and constant devotional service (nitya‑yuktaḥ) finds direct support in Yoga Sutra I.12, which teaches that the vṛttis (mind‑fluctuations) are stilled through abhyāsa (consistent practice) and vairāgya (detachment from sense objects). The “without deviation” aspect is the abhyāsa—returning again and again to the point of focus like a process that never leaves its polling loop. The “constant engagement in devotional service” is the vairāgya‑like surrender of personal desire to the divine, akin to setting the process’s niceness to lowest priority so that only the divine service runs with full CPU affinity. Furthermore, Yoga Sutra I.23 affirms that devotion to Īśvara is a direct route to the samādhi state, echoing the verse’s promise that such devotion makes the Lord “easy to obtain.” Together, these sutras outline the same two‑pronged approach: steady, disciplined focus on the divine object, coupled with the offering of one’s actions to that object.

 

SANKHYA CONCEPTS:

  • puruṣa‑prakṛti dualism
  • manas (mind) as an evolute of prakṛti
  • the three guṇas (sattva, rajas, tamas) as mental modulators
  • vṛttis (thought‑waves) as modifications of citta
  • abhyāsa (practice) as the method to still vṛttis
  • vairāgya (dispassion) as withdrawal from sense‑object craving
  • bhakti (devotion) as the offering of actions to the puruṣa
  • ananya‑cetā (undiverted mind) as ekatā (one‑pointedness)
  • puruṣa‑prāpti (attainment of the puruṣa) as the goal of yoga

 

KEY SANSKRIT TERMS:

  • ananya‑cetāḥ — without deviation of the mind; hacker metaphor: a process with CPU affinity locked to a single core, never migrating
  • satataṁ — always; hacker metaphor: a daemon with respawn=always, never terminating
  • yo māṁ smarati — one who remembers Me; hacker metaphor: a process that constantly polls a divine device file (/dev/kṛṣṇa)
  • nityaśaḥ — regularly; hacker metaphor: a cron job running at fixed intervals without drift
  • tasyāhaṁ su‑labhaḥ — I am very easy to obtain; hacker metaphor: a service that responds instantly when the correct request is made, latency near zero

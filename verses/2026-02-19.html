🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 2.68 | February 19, 2026

 

तस्माद्यस्य महाबाहो

निगृहीतानि सर्वशः ।

इन्द्रियाणीन्द्रियार्थेभ्य

स्तस्य प्रज्ञा प्रतिष्ठिता ॥

 

tasmād yasya mahā-bāho

nigṛhītāni sarvaśaḥ

indriyāṇīndriyārthebhyas

tasya prajñā pratiṣṭhitā

 

"Therefore, O mighty-armed, one whose senses are restrained from their objects is certainly of steady intelligence."

 

Yesterday we saw the problem: the mind that follows wandering senses (BG 2.67). Today: the solution. The sequence completes.

 

Nigṛhītāni — not suppressed, not denied, but restrained. The senses are not the enemy. They're wild horses — capable of carrying you to liberation or destruction. The yogi doesn't blindfold the horses; he holds the reins. Pratyāhāra as willed direction, not forced denial.

 

Prajñā pratiṣṭhitā — intelligence becomes established. Not fluctuating, not aspirational, but stable. From yesterday's boat adrift on wind-blown waters to today's anchored vessel. The same senses exist, but now they're instruments of buddhi instead of obstacles to it.

 

This is Tṛtīya energy — the third day, when śukla paksha gains momentum. Light strengthens. The solution appears after the problem is fully understood (2.66 prerequisites → 2.67 mechanism → 2.68 established wisdom). Tṛtīya is the day of completion's beginning — the first fierce day of the waxing moon.

 

🔥 YS 2.55 — tataḥ paramā vaśyatendriyānām — "Thence arises the highest control over the senses."

 

Where BG 2.68 declares prajñā pratiṣṭhitā (established intelligence) as the fruit of sense-restraint, YS 2.55 describes paramā vaśyatā (supreme control) as the fruit of pratyāhāra. Both point to the same threshold: when indriyas become instruments rather than masters, the ys of discrimination clears.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

Tasmāt (indeclinable) — therefore, from that; logical connector. BG 2.67's destruction leads to 2.68's establishment.

Nigṛhītāni (past passive participle, nominative plural) — restrained, controlled; from ni-grah (to hold down, check). Not suppression but directed holding.

Sarvaśaḥ (indeclinable) — in all respects, completely. Total restraint, not partial.

Indriyārthebhyaḥ (ablative plural) — from the objects of the senses. What is restrained is contact with viṣaya, not the senses themselves.

Pratiṣṭhitā (feminine adjective) — established, founded; from prati-sthā (to stand firm). Stability achieved, not stability sought.

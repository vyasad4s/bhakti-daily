🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 3.5 | 2026-04-08

 

न हि कश्चित्क्षणमपि

जातु तिष्ठत्यकर्मकृत्

कार्यते ह्यवशः कर्म

सर्वः प्रकृतिजैर्गुणैः

 

na hi kaścit kṣaṇam api
jātu tiṣṭhaty akarma-kṛt
kāryate hy avaśaḥ karma
sarvaḥ prakṛti-jair guṇaiḥ

 

Everyone is forced to act helplessly according to the qualities he has acquired from the modes of material nature; therefore no one can refrain from doing something, not even for a moment.

 

Arjuna wants to quit. The battlefield is too much. He thinks if he just sits down and stops, the problem will go away. But Krishna cuts to the root with devastating clarity: you cannot stop. The soul is constitutionally active. You are not choosing between action and inaction. You are choosing between conscious action and compelled action.

 

This is Sāṅkhya as a debugging tool. Your current "code" — mental and physical habits accumulated across lifetimes — compiles and executes automatically. The guṇas are the runtime environment, the background processes you rarely examine but which determine every output. Rajas drives you to chase. Tamas drags you to sleep. Sattva brings clarity. But none of them are you.

 

The word avaśaḥ — helplessly — is the key. Most think they are free. But check the logs: how many choices were compulsive? Driven by hunger, fear, habit, inherited patterns you never consented to? The Gītā says: acknowledge the constraint first. Only then can you work within it intelligently.

 

Mūla nakṣatra confronts us with roots — the deep structures underlying surface phenomena. Today asks: what is really running your show? Not the story you tell, but the actual processes? This is not despair. It is the necessary diagnosis before the cure.

 

🔥 YS I.4: vṛtti-sārūpyam itaratra — "Otherwise, at other times, [the seer] is absorbed in the changing states [of the mind]." Patañjali names our condition. When we do not abide as pure consciousness, we are sārūpyam — one-formed with the fluctuations of mind. BG 3.5 describes the mechanics: bound to act through the guṇas. YS I.4 describes the experience: we become what we perceive. Karma-yoga is the bridge — we don't abdicate action; we reclaim it as instrument rather than identity.

 

Practice Today

Set a timer for 10 minutes. Sit still. Watch the compulsion to move, adjust, check your phone. Each impulse is prakṛti-jair guṇaiḥ — the modes working through you. After 10 minutes, choose one action you would ordinarily do unconsciously and perform it deliberately. Offer it before acting.

 

Term Definitions

avaśaḥ (adj.) — helpless, uncontrolled; from a- (not) + vaśa (control); "without sway"; describes the soul bound by the guṇas

prakṛti-jaiḥ (adj.) — born of prakṛti; referring to the guṇas as evolutes of material nature; karma originates here

guṇaiḥ (noun, instr. pl.) — by the qualities; the three-fold patterning of material nature (sattva, rajas, tamas)

akarma-kṛt (noun) — non-doer of action; the impossible state Arjuna imagines he can reach by renouncing

kāryate (verb, passive) — is forced to do; causative passive of kṛ (to do); emphasizes compulsion

kṣaṇam api (phrase) — even for a moment; kṣaṇa = atomic unit of time; emphasizes continuity of action

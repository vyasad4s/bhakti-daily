रागद्वेषविमुक्तैस्तु विषयानिन्द्रियैश्चरन्

आत्मवश्यैर्विधेयात्मा प्रसादमधिगच्छति ॥ ६४ ॥

 

rāga-dveṣa-vimuktais tu viṣayān indriyaiś caran
ātma-vaśyair vidheyātmā prasādam adhigacchati

 

But a person free from all rāga (attachment) and dveṣa (aversion), acting upon sense objects with senses under one's control, following regulated principles of freedom, can obtain the complete prasāda (mercy) of the Lord.

 

The sequence has been building to this: 2.62 described the descent—contemplating sense objects → attachment → desire → anger → delusion → loss of memory → destruction of intelligence → complete ruin. Now Kṛṣṇa delivers the counter-sequence: liberation from rāga-dveṣa → controlled senses → regulated freedom → prasāda → the causeless mercy that dissolves all material miseries.

This is not some vague spiritual aspiration. This is engineering. Rāga-dveṣa-vimuktaiḥ—free from the binary trap of attraction and repulsion, the fundamental oscillation that keeps consciousness trapped in saṃsāra. The sense objects remain—viṣayān—but the response architecture changes completely. The senses become ātma-vaśya—under the command of the self, not the prisoner of objects. This is vidheyātmā—regulated freedom, not chaotic renunciation or impulsive indulgence, but disciplined action following the operating system's protocols. The result: prasāda—not "peace of mind" but the active grace of the Supreme. From this mercy, all material miseries cease to exist (2.65). This is the complete compilation: input (senses controlled), processing (regulated by dharma), output (prasāda). The soul begins to recognize its own autonomous nature, distinct from the prakṛtic entrapment.

 

🔥 YS II.3 — avidyā-asmitā-rāga-dveṣābhiniveśāḥ kleśāḥ: Ignorance, egoism, attachment, aversion, and clinging to life are the five afflictions. Patañjali identifies rāga and dveṣa as two of the five kleśas—the root afflictions causing bondage. The Bhagavad-gītā provides the solution: liberation through devotion and regulated action. Where Patañjali prescribes citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ (cessation of mental modifications), Kṛṣṇa reveals the faster path—redirect those modifications toward His service. Both paths converge at discrimination (viveka), but bhakti carries the additional voltage of prasāda.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

rāga (masc.) — attachment, passion, coloration of the mind; the condition where the mind is drawn toward objects based on remembered pleasure from past experiences; like legacy code that keeps executing because it worked once.

dveṣa (masc.) — aversion, hatred, repulsion; the inverse attachment—pushing away what caused pain; both rāga and dveṣa bind equally, just different polarities of the same trap.

vimukta (adj.) — liberated, released, set free; the state of being completely untangled from the kleshas; root muc—to loosen, release.

ātma-vaśya (compound adj.) — under the control of the self (ātman + vaśya); the senses operating as api endpoints the self controls, not as rogue processes hijacking attention.

vidheyātmā (compound masc.) — one whose nature follows regulated principles; disciplined freedom—the apparent paradox that structure enables liberation.

prasāda (masc.) — grace, mercy, tranquility; causeless favor of the Lord; the result of bhakti-yoga practice; also the sanctified remnants of offering to the Lord.

adhigacchati (v.) — attains, reaches, comes to understand; the active acquisition of prasāda through right practice—this is not passive grace but earned through discipline.

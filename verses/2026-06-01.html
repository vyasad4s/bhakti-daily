🏹 BG 6.20-23 | 2026-06-01

 

यत्रोपरमते चित्तं निरुद्धं योगसेवया ।

यत्र चैवात्मनात्मानं पश्यन्नात्मनि तुष्यति ॥ २० ॥

सुखमात्यन्तिकं यत्तद्बुद्धिग्राह्यमतीन्द्रियम् ।

वेत्ति यत्र न चैवायं स्थितश्चलति तत्त्वत: ॥ २१ ॥

यं लब्ध्वा चापरं लाभं मन्यते नाधिकं तत: ।

यस्मिन्स्थितो न दु:खेन गुरुणापि विचाल्यते ॥ २२ ॥

तं विद्याद्दु:खसंयोगवियोगं योगसंज्ञितम् ॥ २३ ॥

 

yatroparamate cittaṁ niruddhaṁ yoga-sevayā
yatra caivātmanātmānaṁ paśyann ātmani tuṣyati
sukham ātyantikaṁ yat tad buddhi-grāhyam atīndriyam
vetti yatra na caivāyaṁ sthitaś calati tattvataḥ
yaṁ labdhvā cāparaṁ lābhaṁ manyate nādhikaṁ tataḥ
yasmin sthito na duḥkhena guruṇāpi vicālyate
taṁ vidyād duḥkha-saṁyoga-viyogaṁ yoga-saṁjñitam

 

In the stage of perfection called trance, or samādhi, one's mind is completely restrained from material mental activities by practice of yoga. This perfection is characterized by one's ability to see the Self by the pure mind and to relish and rejoice in the Self. In that joyous state, one is situated in boundless transcendental happiness, realized through transcendental senses. Established thus, one never departs from the truth, and upon gaining this he thinks there is no greater gain. Being situated in such a position, one is never shaken, even in the midst of greatest difficulty. This indeed is actual freedom from all miseries arising from material contact.

 

Four verses. Four stages. The most concentrated description of samādhi in the entire Bhagavad-gītā, delivered on the final day of Dhyāna Yoga. Kṛṣṇa could not have saved this for any other moment. The chapter that began with the definition of a true yogī (6.1) now culminates in the definitive description of yoga's fruit. Everything in between — the self as friend and enemy (6.5-6), the disciplined meditator (6.10-14), the lamp in a windless place (6.19), the mind's restlessness and its remedy (6.34-35), the fate of the fallen yogī (6.37-44), the superiority of bhakti-yoga (6.46-47) — everything has been building to these four verses. They are the root. And on the day when Mūla nakṣatra — "the root" — activates at 9:38 AM, Kṛṣṇa takes us there directly.

 

Verse 20: yatroparamate cittaṁ niruddhaṁ yoga-sevayā — the mind ceases its material activities. Not paused. Not suppressed. Niruddha — completely restrained, arrested at the root. This is the first gate of samādhi: the cessation of the very movement that has dragged consciousness through incarnation after incarnation. The mind doesn't become blank — it becomes single-pointed. And in that single-pointedness, ātmanātmānaṁ paśyan — the Self sees the Self by the Self. Not through scripture, not through philosophy, not through another's report. Direct perception. And having seen, tuṣyati — one becomes satisfied. The search ends not because you gave up, but because you found.

 

Verse 21: sukham ātyantikam — supreme, ultimate happiness. Not the happiness of fulfilled desire — that is always shadowed by the fear of loss. This is happiness that is buddhi-grāhyam — grasped by intelligence, not by the senses. Atīndriyam — beyond the senses entirely. The pleasure of this world is always mediated: you need the object, the contact, the neurological cascade. Samādhi's happiness requires no object. It is self-generating. Consciousness itself is ānanda — not as metaphor, as direct experience. And in that state: na sthitaś calati tattvataḥ — one who is established does not deviate from the truth. The truth is not a proposition you hold. It is the position you are.

 

Verse 22: The fruit becomes personal. Yaṁ labdhvā cāparaṁ lābhaṁ manyate nādhikaṁ tataḥ — having gained this, one considers no other gain to be greater. This is the death of ambition — not the ambition to serve, to love, to grow in devotion, but the ambition to find satisfaction elsewhere. Every material achievement, every spiritual milestone short of this, is revealed as partial. And then the clincher: yasmin sthito na duḥkhena guruṇāpi vicālyate — being situated in this position, one is never shaken even by the greatest difficulty. Not "rarely shaken." Not "usually stable." Never shaken. This is the ultimate test of realization: not what happens in meditation, but what happens when the world collapses around you. The realized being stands. Not through willpower — through the structural impossibility of being moved when you are established in what is immovable.

 

Verse 23: Kṛṣṇa names it. Taṁ vidyād duḥkha-saṁyoga-viyogam yoga-saṁjñitam — know this as the severance of the connection with suffering, designated as yoga. Not the reduction of suffering. Not the management of suffering. The severance of the connection. The cable between consciousness and material misery is unplugged. The server is still running — you just are no longer logged in. This is what Mūla nakṣatra demands: go to the root of suffering, not to its branches. Uproot the connection itself. That is samādhi. That is yoga.

 

🔥 Yoga Sūtra Bridge: YS I.17 & IV.34

 

वितर्कविचारानन्दास्मितारूपानुगमात् सम्प्रज्ञातः

vitarka-vicārānandāsmitā-rūpānugamāt samprajñātaḥ

Samprajñāta-samādhi is that which is accompanied by reasoning, reflection, bliss, and sense of identity.

 

पुरुषार्थशून्यानां गुणानां प्रतिप्रसवः कैवल्यं स्वरूपप्रतिष्ठा वा चितिशक्तिरिति

puruṣārtha-śūnyānāṁ guṇānāṁ pratiprasavaḥ kaivalyaṁ svarūpa-pratiṣṭhā vā citi-śaktir iti

When the guṇas, devoid of any purpose for puruṣa, return to their source — that is kaivalya: establishment in one's own form, or the power of pure consciousness.

 

Prabhupāda himself cites YS IV.34 in his purport to this very verse — an extraordinary moment where the Gītā's commentator reaches into Patañjali's text to illuminate Kṛṣṇa's words. The citation is precise: kaivalyaṁ svarūpa-pratiṣṭhā vā citi-śaktir iti — kaivalya is establishment in one's own nature, or the power of pure consciousness. Prabhupāda's point is decisive: Patañjali's kaivalya is not the void that monists imagine. It is citi-śakti — the internal transcendental potency. The citi-śakti is not blankness. It is luminous, self-aware, actively conscious being.

 

The structural mapping between the two texts is exact. Kṛṣṇa's four stages in 6.20-23 correspond to Patañjali's progression: cittaṁ niruddham (mind restrained) maps to the nirodha that precedes samprajñāta; ātmanātmānaṁ paśyan (Self seeing Self) maps to the ānanda-asmitā layers of samprajñāta-samādhi; sukham ātyantikam (supreme happiness) maps to the ānanda component that Patañjali identifies as intrinsic to samādhi; and duḥkha-saṁyoga-viyoga (severance from suffering) maps directly to kaivalya — the guṇas returning to their source, puruṣa established in citi-śakti. Both systems confirm the same truth from different angles: the fruit of yoga is not an experience. It is a structural transformation of consciousness itself.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

samādhi — Complete absorption of consciousness in its object. The mind is no longer aware of itself as meditating — only the object remains, shining as raw presence. Root access to reality. No intermediary, no interpreter, no lag.

citi-śakti — The power of pure consciousness. Patañjali's term for what remains when prakṛti's guṇas have returned to their source. Not void, not blankness — luminous, aware, self-established being. The operating system running with no applications consuming memory.

kaivalya — Aloneness, wholeness, isolation from prakṛti. Not loneliness — the radical self-sufficiency of puruṣa established in its own nature. The server disconnected from every external dependency, running its own code perfectly.

duḥkha-saṁyoga-viyoga — The severance of the connection with suffering. Not the management of pain — the termination of the link between consciousness and material misery. The network cable has been unplugged from the suffering server.

niruddha — Completely restrained, fully arrested. The mind's material activities have been terminated — not suppressed, but seen through and released. The process that once hijacked awareness has been killed at the root.

ātyantikam — Supreme, ultimate, absolute. Not incremental happiness — the end of the line for the search for satisfaction. The last package in the dependency chain has been resolved.

Bhagavad-gītā Chapter 2, Verse 66
nāsti buddhir ayuktasya na cāyuktasya bhāvanā
na cābhāvayato bhaktiḥ

------------------------------------------------

One who is not connected with the Supreme can have neither transcendental intelligence nor a steady mind, and without a steady mind one cannot have peace. And how can there be happiness without peace?

------------------------------------------------

Reflection: The progression is clear and inevitable - without connection to the Supreme (yukti), there is no true buddhi (discriminative wisdom). Without buddhi, no stable bhāvanā (contemplative mind). Without a stable mind, no śānti (peace). And without peace, no sukham (happiness). This is not philosophy; this is the mechanics of consciousness. The sense-controlled mind of yesterday's verse (2.65) becomes the disconnected mind of today without that vital link to Kṛṣṇa. The time to establish that connection is NOW - not when life is perfect, but while the Amāvāsyā darkness makes the internal light visible. Let us chant with undivided attention!

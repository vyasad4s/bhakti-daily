>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Aṣṭamī tithi — the eighth day, the dissolution that precedes fulfillment. The veiling and unveiling. What is protected must first be broken; what is broken reveals the protected.

Punarvasu nakṣatra — "Return to the Good," Aditi's shelter. The star of revival after storm. Arjuna's desire to escape (anārambhāt) is the storm; Kṛṣṇa answers with the shelter of right action. You cannot hide from work; you must transform it.

Puṣya nakṣatra — "The Nourisher," Bṛhaspati's star. The transition from Punarvasu to Puṣya mirrors the transition from avoidance to engagement: from the shelter of escape to the nourishment of proper action.

Bava Yoga — auspicious for beginnings, for penetrating truth, for deeds of virtue. Supports the message: ACT—but act properly.

Śukravāra — Venus's day, the day of value, resources, and refinement. Not acquisition but appreciation. The householder's day par excellence.

>>> VERSE SELECTION RATIONALE <<<

na karmaṇām anārambhāt...

BG 3.4 — After establishing two paths (3.3), Kṛṣṇa now destroys false approaches. The transitional cosmic energies—Aṣṭamī (breaking), Punarvasu→Puṣya (shelter→nourishment), Śukravāra (value)—support the teaching: neither escapism nor hypocritical renunciation works. Only karma-yoga: action without attachment.

>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Today carries the energy of uprooting. Mūla nakṣatra (literally "root") governs the morning until 3:13 PM—the asterism presided over by Nirṛti, the goddess of dissolution. This is not destruction; it is clearing. The necessary uprooting of what no longer serves, the pulling out of the taproot of attachment so that something new might grow. Just as BG 2.62-63 describes the root of saṁsāra beginning with contemplation of sense objects, Mūla represents the force that can uproot that very root.

Vyatipāta yoga operates throughout the day—"falling away," "deviation," "that which removes obstacles." Together with Mūla, this creates intense cleansing energy: whatever is not aligned with truth, whatever represents old patterns of attachment, whatever constitutes the dusty accumulation of saṅga from lifetimes of sense indulgence—these are churned to the surface today.

Pūrva Aṣāḍhā takes over after 3:13 PM—ruled by the waters, the asterism of unstoppable momentum. The transition mirrors the teaching: first uproot, then move forward.

>>> VERSE SELECTION RATIONALE <<<

ध्यायतो विषयान्पुंसः...

BG 2.62-63 — Mūla nakṣatra demands a verse on uprooting. The sequence in these verses is the taproot of saṁsāra itself, traced from first contact (contemplation) through to total system failure (praṇaśyati). On a day ruled by the goddess of dissolution, we examine what needs dissolving. On a day of Vyatipāta yoga, we study the mechanism by which obstacles to consciousness accumulate—and by understanding, we hold the key to their removal.

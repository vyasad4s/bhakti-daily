 >>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

The day's celestial architecture speaks to the soul's paradox: it pervades (tatam) yet cannot be touched. Kṛttikā means "the cutters" — the wives of the stars who raised the infant Skanda, representing the transformative fire that severs without destroying. This nakṣatra governs śakti, the power to penetrate and pervade.

Śukla Aṣṭamī represents the bridge toward fullness — the 8th day of expansion where energies accumulate before Pūrṇimā. The Yoga Śukha (pleasant, agreeable) gives way to afternoon transitions, supporting the verse's theme: amidst pleasant and unpleasant sensations, that which pervades remains avināśi — untouched, undestroyed.

The Cosmic Frame

The Tithi Speaks: Complete Dissolution

Today at 10:41 AM, the fourteenth tithi melts into Amāvāsyā — the new moon, dark moon, the zero point where all lunar digits dissolve. Caturdaśī is the final exhalation before the cosmic breath pauses. In this threshold moment, Kṛṣṇa delivers one of His most surgical teachings: you are not the doer. Not as consolation. Not as metaphor. As fact.

The timing is impeccable. The soul who thinks "I act, I achieve, I control" undergoes the same dissolution as the moon itself. On Amāvāsyā, Sāṅkhya's fundamental truth becomes visible: prakṛteḥ kriyamāṇāni — it is prakṛti who acts, not puruṣa. The guṇas churn. The ego claims credit. This is ahaṅkāra-vimoha — the bewilderment that makes a witness into a claimant.

Revatī: The Final Nakṣatra

Until 2:32 AM tomorrow, the moon traverses Revatī — the last of the 27 nakṣatras, ruled by Pūṣan, the nourisher and protector of paths. Revatī marks completion, the final station before the cosmic reset. This is the energy of full circle — not beginning, not ending, but the liminal space between them.

BG 2.20 — The verse of the unborn eternal is the mirror held to Purnima. Just as the moon achieves apparent fullness tonight while its mass was always complete, so the ātman reveals its eternal nature not through achievement but recognition. Kṛṣṇa's declaration that the soul "has not come into being, does not come into being, and will not come into being" ( nāyaṁ bhūtvā bhavitā vā na bhūyaḥ ) structurally denies all three temporal modes—past, present, future—because puruṣa stands outside time entirely.

>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Today carries the weight of dissolution and transcendence. We are in Krishna Paksha—the waning fortnight where tamas increases as the moon diminishes. This isn't darkness to fear; it's the necessary conditions for deep work. Like the ocean before dawn, like a computer entering sleep mode to clear cache, the waning moon creates space for introspection.

Dhanishta Nakshatra until evening bears the drum (damaru) of Shiva himself—this is the nakshatra of rhythm, manifestation, and cosmic order. Dhanishta means "the most wealthy" or "the most famous," but its deeper wealth is the wealth of awareness. Its deity is the Ashtavasu, the eight cosmic attendants of cosmic order. Practical application: Dhanishta brings structure to chaos. If you've been scattered, today offers the energy to establish routine.

The transition to Shatabhisha late tonight—"the hundred healers" or "the veiled"—brings the energy of hidden wisdom and healing. Shatabhisha is ruled by Varuna, the cosmic waters, and carries the quality of containment. The hundred stars represent the multiplicity of remedies that become available when you approach the Absolute.

Siddha Yoga until late evening—this means "accomplished" or "successful." The day's energies support completion of tasks, especially those requiring sustained focus.

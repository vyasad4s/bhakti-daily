>>> SĀṄKHYA LENS <<<

Brahma-saṁsparśa as the point of singularity: In Sāṅkhya, the goal is the complete separation of puruṣa from prakṛti. Brahma-saṁsparśa represents the moment where the buddhi becomes so pure (sattva-dominant) that it ceases to distort the light of puruṣa and instead becomes a perfect mirror. At this point, the "contact" is not a meeting of two things, but the realization that the observer (puruṣa) is eternally separate from the observed (prakṛti), yet situated in a state of transcendental bliss because the distortion is gone.

The mechanism of Vigata-kalmaṣa: "Contamination" (kalmaṣa) in Sāṅkhya terms refers to the presence of rajas and tamas in the buddhi. These create the "noise" that masks the truth. The practice of yoga (yuñjan) acts as a filtering process. When the "malware" of the five kleśas is deleted, the system reaches a state of zero-entropy. The "highest happiness" (atyantaṁ sukham) is simply the natural state of the puruṣa once the noise of prakṛti is silenced.

Siddha state and the collapse of duality: The transition to Siddha yoga mirrors the state of kaivalya (aloneness/independence). The yogī is no longer "trying" to reach the Supreme; they have reached the state where the awareness of the Supreme is continuous. The duality of "practitioner" and "goal" collapses into a single state of being.

 >>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Today carries the energy of skillful transition. Hasta nakṣatra, "the hand," presides over all acts of crafting and transformation—the precise ability to hold, shape, release, and reshape. This is exactly the energy needed for BG 2.22: the skilled, conscious act of exchanging worn garments for fresh ones. Not unconscious drift, but intentional movement.

The tithi transition mirrors the verse's teaching: Dwitiya (duality, "this AND that") yields to Tritiya (action, forward momentum). We move from recognizing the distinction between body and soul to ACTING upon that knowledge. The Yoga shifts from Ganda (obstacle, the problem of misidentification) to Vṛddhi (growth, the expansion that comes from liberation).

The Moon in Virgo brings analytical precision to this spiritual operation. Not vague mysticism but systematic discrimination—exactly what viveka requires.

 >>> VERSE SELECTION RATIONALE <<<

वासांसि जीर्णानि यथा विहाय...

BG 2.22 — Hasta nakṣatra literally means "hand"—the instrument of deliberate action, of taking and releasing. The verse describes the soul's hands discarding worn garments and accepting fresh ones. The connection could not be more precise. Under Hasta's influence, today's verse focuses not merely on the metaphysics of reincarnation but on the SKILL of embodiment: how consciously do we wear our current form? Are we identified with the costume, or do we remember the actor?

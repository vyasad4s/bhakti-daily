Cosmic Interpretation

The tithi begins as Sukla Paksha Chaturthi (until 09:28 AM), the fourth tithi of the waxing moon associated with stability, foundation, and the worship of Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles (Vighnaharta). As Chaturthi yields to Panchami at 09:28 AM, the energy shifts to the fifth tithi, associated with vitality, strength, and the divine grace that removes impediments to spiritual progress. The nakṣatra Ashlesha, ruled by the serpents (representing clinging energy and the awakening of kundalini), governs the day until 12:36 AM on June 19, symbolizing the energy of penetration, insight, and the ability to see through illusion. As Ashlesha yields to Magha (the throne star, representing authority and leadership) at 12:36 AM, the energy transitions to noble bearing, power, and the blessings of the ancestors.

The yoga Vyaghata (obstacle, injury) prevails until 08:05 AM, bringing the energy of overcoming impediments and challenges, after which Harshana (joy, delight) arises, signifying happiness, enthusiasm, and the sweetness of spiritual progress. This progression—from obstacle-removing Vyaghata to joy-giving Harshana—mirrors the spiritual path: through persistent effort to overcome inner and outer obstacles, one discovers the innate joy of devotion.

BG 8.10 beautifully aligns with this cosmic rhythm: “One who, at the time of death, fixes his life air between the eyebrows and, by the strength of yoga, with an undeviating mind, engages himself in remembering the Supreme Lord in full devotion, will certainly attain to the Supreme Personality of Godhead.” Just as Chaturthi’s energy of foundation and obstacle-removal (through Ganesha’s grace) gives way to Panchami’s vitality, and as Vyaghata’s challenge-overcoming energy yields to Harshana’s joy—so too does the devotee progress by fixing the mind on the Supreme at life’s ultimate transition. The ājñā-cakra (between the eyebrows) becomes the point of concentration, where the life force is gathered and directed upward, not distracted by the clinging energy of Ashlesha but transformed by the discriminating wisdom that leads to liberation.

The verse instructs us to “fix his life air between the eyebrows” (prāṇam āveśya samyak) and engage in remembrance (smaran) of the Supreme with an undeviating mind (manasācalena), backed by the strength of yoga (yoga-balena) and full devotion (bhaktyā yuktaḥ). In the language of the energy centers, this is the activation of the ājñā-cakra—the command center—where the individual consciousness aligns with the divine will. As the Yoga Sutra affirms, samādhi arises when the modifications of the mind are restrained (YS 1.2), and here, at the critical moment of death, the undeviating mind fixed on the Supreme leads to the attainment of the spiritual kingdom (divyam).

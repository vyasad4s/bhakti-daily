 >>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<

This is a Guru-Puṣya day — one of the most powerful combinations in Vedic astrology. Puṣya nakṣatra (ruled by Bṛhaspati, guru of the gods) aligns with Guruvāra (Thursday, Jupiter's day). The guru energy doubles. The blessing channel opens wide. This is not a day for ordinary ambitions — this is a day for darśana, for seeing what the guru reveals.

BG 6.29 is the verse of guru-blessed vision: sama-darśana, equal seeing. When Puṣya's nourishment meets Guruvar's expansion, the result is precisely what Kṛṣṇa describes — the capacity to see the Supreme everywhere. Puṣya is the nakṣatra of spiritual growth, of the teacher's grace, of expansion through discipline. Guruvar amplifies this: Jupiter is the planet of wisdom, of the guru, of the expansive vision that contains all particulars within a single comprehension. The combination creates a cosmic magnifying glass for consciousness — what was obscure becomes visible, what was separate becomes connected.

Ṣaṣṭhī tithi — the sixth day of the waxing moon. This is the tithi of Skanda, of spiritual warfare, of the discipline that protects the growing light. The moon is building toward fullness; the vision is building toward completeness. Vṛddhi yoga means "increase" or "growth" — the amplification principle is tripled: Guru-Puṣya (growth through guru's blessing), Ṣaṣṭhī (growth of the luminous), Vṛddhi yoga (growth as cosmic principle). Everything on this day points toward expansion of perception.

The nakṣatra transition at 5:19 PM from Puṣya to Āśleṣā is the day's critical threshold. Puṣya is the guru's open hand — blessing, nourishment, expansion. Āśleṣā is the serpent's coiled energy — deep, hidden, karmic, psychological. The transition from Puṣya to Āśleṣā mirrors the arc of BG 6.29 into 6.30: first you see the Lord everywhere (Puṣya's illumination), then you realize that He never loses you and you never lose Him (Āśleṣā's intimate, coiled knowing). The guru gives the vision; the serpent guards the intimacy.

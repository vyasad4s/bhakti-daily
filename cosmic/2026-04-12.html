>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Today marks the threshold between Daśamī and Ekādaśī — the day of completion giving way to the day of fasting and intensified spiritual practice. The Gītā's instruction here is precise: do your duty BEFORE you attempt transcendence. The householder's obligations must be settled before the sannyāsī's freedom can be approached.

 

Dhaniṣṭhā nakṣatra — the "wealthiest" — carries the energy of complete abundance. Associated with the eight Vasus (deities of material plentitude), it governs prosperity, recognition, and complete manifestation of potential. Yet this wealth is not hoarded; it is distributed through yajña. The eight Vasus are the divine administrators, just as today's verse reminds us that even body-maintenance requires participation in the cosmic economy.

 

Śubha Yoga (auspicious) governs from 8:45 AM — a rare and favorable alignment. The quality of "auspicious" does not mean avoiding work; it means performing prescribed duty with the right consciousness. Śubha combined with Dhaniṣṭhā indicates manifestation of abundance through right action, not renunciation of it.

 

The Moon enters Kumbha (Aquarius) at 6:14 PM — the sign of distribution and universal service. This completes the arc: perform your niyatam karma, enter the śubha quality, become the vessel through which abundance flows. This is Karma Yoga's complete cycle.

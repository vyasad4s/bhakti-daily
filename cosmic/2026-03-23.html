>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Pañcamī—governed by Budha (Mercury)—is the tithi of learning, intellectual pursuits, and sacred study. It carries the energy of the student approaching the teacher, which mirrors Arjuna's shift from friend to disciple in BG 2.11. The waxing moon in Śukla Pakṣa builds toward illumination; this is a day for beginning what endures.

The nakṣatra transition from Kṛttikā (Agni's fire) to Rohiṇī (Prajāpati's nurture) reflects the verse's movement from burning ignorance to nourishing wisdom. Fire purifies; abundance sustains. The paṇḍita's discrimination is precisely this: transforming the heat of sorrow into the clarity of knowledge.

Sarvārtha Siddhi yoga activates at 11:19 AM—a portal where endeavors begun meet with natural completion. The conjunction of Rohiṇī nakṣatra on Somavār creates auspicious momentum for students of wisdom. This is the cosmic endorsement of surrendering to a teacher.

The student's perplexity meets the teacher's clarity. The agony of vṛtti-sārūpya (identification with mind's modifications) meets the medicine of discriminating wisdom.

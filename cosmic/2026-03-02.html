>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Chaturdaśī — the 14th lunar day, the day before fullness. The eve of Pūrṇimā in Śukla Pakṣa. The karma-yogī stands at the threshold of illumination. The number 14 holds Tantric significance: completion of manifestation before dissolution. The superior yogī stands in poised readiness.

Magha → Pūrva Phālguni — the throne gives way to the marriage bed. At 09:01 PM, Magha's ancestral sovereignty transforms into Pūrva Phālguni's divine union. Rulership shifts to relationship. The karma-yogī realizes devotion is union with the Supreme.

Sukarma → Dhṛti Yoga — from good action to steadiness. The transition from performing virtuous deeds to establishing persistence. The mark of the superior practitioner is not perfection but persistence. Good action becomes steady character.

Somavāra — Chandra's day. The Moon governs the mind, reflection, and transformation. The karma-yogī's mind must be transformed before the senses can be controlled. Monday is the day of mental discipline.

>>> VERSE SELECTION RATIONALE <<<

yas tv indriyāṇi manasā...

BG 3.7 — The superior yogī revealed. Body engaged through regulated mind, consciousness fixed on the Supreme. Chaturdaśī is the threshold day — not the full moon pretender, nor the new moon renunciant, but the active practitioner on the path. Magha demands legitimate authority; Pūrva Phālguni demands divine union. The superior yogī embodies both: authority through action, union through devotion. Karma-yoga is the method. Somavāra transforms the mind first; the senses follow.

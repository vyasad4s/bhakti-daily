>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Navamī tithi — the ninth day, the number of completion and fulfillment. The fullness before the turning. What is complete must be offered; what is complete cannot be grasped. The question of ONE path arises when multiplicity has been exhausted.

Mrigaśīrṣa nakṣatra — the deer's head, the restless seeker of Soma (divine nectar). Never satisfied, always running toward the infinite. This is the seeker after the ONE—through forests of multiplicity, always thirsty for the essential.

Taitila Yoga — the separator, the one who cuts through. From tāḍana (to strike). This yoga supports decisions, clarity, cutting through confusion. The sword that severs the tangled knot of contradictory teachings.

Budhavāra — Mercury's day, Wednesday. The day of intellect, communication, and clear speech. Arjuna asks for clarity on Wednesday—the cosmic timing supports the question: "Tell me ONE path, decisively."

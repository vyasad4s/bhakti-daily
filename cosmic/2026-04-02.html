Cosmic Context

Nakṣatra: Chitra — The Bright One, ruled by Tvashtā, the celestial architect. This star governs the hidden-to-visible-to-hidden cycle of manifestation. Just as Chitra's splendor appears, dazzles, and dissolves into night, all material forms follow this trajectory.

Tithi: Kṛṣṇa Pratipadā — First day of the waning moon. The full manifestation begins its return to the unmanifest. The cosmic process in miniature: from fullness (vyakta) toward dissolution (avyakta).

Yoga: Vyāghāta — "Shattering." A disruptive force that breaks false permanence. Today the illusion of stable form is shattered, revealing the truth of constant transformation.

Correlation: On a day ruled by the architect of cosmic splendor (Chitra-Tvashtā), during the moon's return to darkness (Pratipadā), under a yoga that breaks attachment (Vyāghāta), the Gītā speaks of the soul beyond manifestation cycles. The timing is surgical precision.

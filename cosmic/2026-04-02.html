>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Kṛṣṇa Pratipadā: First day of the dark fortnight. The full moon's fullness begins its return to the unmanifest. This is the cosmic manifestation cycle in miniature—vyakta emerging from and returning to avyakta.

 

Chitra Nakṣatra: The Bright One, ruled by Tvaṣṭṛ, the celestial architect. This star governs the hidden-to-visible-to-hidden cycle. Forms emerge, dazzle, dissolve—like Chitra's splendor in the night sky. Perfect alignment for BG 2.28's teaching on avyakta-vyakta cycles.

 

Vyāghāta Yoga: "Shattering" — a disruptive force that breaks false permanence. Today the illusion of stable form is shattered, revealing constant transformation. The cosmic timing is surgical: on the day forms begin fading (Pratipadā), ruled by the architect of cosmic splendor (Chitra-Tvaṣṭṛ), under a yoga that breaks attachment (Vyāghāta).

 

Moon in Tulā: Libra seeks balance—between manifestation and dissolution, between attachment and dispassion. The householder's challenge: to witness all forms as temporary while fully engaged with them.

BG 2.28 — Manifest/Unmanifest Cycles

 

Cosmic Alignment: All created beings are unmanifest in beginning, manifest in interim, unmanifest at annihilation. Chitra nakṣatra governs this bright appearance that captures attention but is destined to fade. Pratipadā marks the transition from pūrṇimā fullness toward dissolution.

 

The verse speaks to the soul beyond manifestation cycles. Even materialists cannot argue with conservation of energy—matter transforms, never perishes. The form was temporary; substance always conserved.

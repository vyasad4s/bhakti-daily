Cosmic Interpretation

Today's primary nakṣatra is Hasta — "the Hand" — ruled by Savitṛ, the solar deity who impels creation. This is the most direct nakṣatra match possible for BG 9.10, where Kṛṣṇa declares mayādhyakṣeṇa prakṛtiḥ sūyate — "material nature is manifested under My superintendence." The Hand (Hasta) is the divine adhyakṣa (superintendent). Savitṛ, as the impelling solar deity, represents the activating glance of the Supreme that sets prakṛti into motion. Sunday (Ravivāra) reinforces this solar-Savitṛ energy throughout the day.

The yoga transitions from Parigha (obstacle-crushing, until 9:52 AM) to Śiva (auspicious dissolution-regeneration, from 9:52 AM). This sequence mirrors the verse's content: first, impediments to understanding the divine superintendence are crushed (Parigha); then, the mind enters the Śiva energy of witnessing the cycle of creation and dissolution — jagad viparivartate — the cosmic manifestation turning under its rule, being created and annihilated again and again.

The tithi transitions from Ṣaṣṭhī (6th, warrior-building) to Saptami (7th, structured momentum) at 6:00 PM — marking a shift from raw force to directed, purposeful expansion. This echoes the verse's hetunānena — "for this reason" — the universe doesn't turn randomly but with teleological precision. Saptami carries the energy of the 7th ray, the directed beam, the Hand's intentional movement.

The Moon resides in Kanyā (Virgo) — the sign of precision, detail, and skilled craftsmanship. This further amplifies Hasta's theme: the Hand works with precision. Material nature is not a blunt instrument but a finely calibrated mechanism operating under exact superintendence. Kanyā's sattvic-earth quality also grounds the practitioner — making this an ideal day for the contemplative practice of recognizing divine direction in the details of ordinary life.

Two auspicious yogas are active: Sarvārtha Siddhi (all-purpose success, until 8:41 AM with Uttara Phalgunī, then continuing with Hasta through tomorrow) and Amṛt Siddhi (immortal success, from 8:41 AM). This double-auspicious window during Hasta nakṣatra creates extraordinary energetic support for practices aimed at recognizing the divine Hand — the mind is naturally calm, bright, and receptive to the insight that prakṛti operates under direction, not independently.

The Sun in Karka (Cancer) — the sign of emotional depth and nurturing — reminds us that the divine superintendence is not cold mechanics. The Hand that turns the universe also holds you.

BG 9.10 is the perfect verse for this cosmic moment. Hasta nakṣatra (the Hand) + Savitṛ (the solar impeller) + Śiva yoga (dissolution-regeneration) + Saptami (directed momentum) + Kanyā Moon (precision) + Sarvārtha Siddhi & Amṛt Siddhi (auspicious alignment) — every element converges on a single revelation: material nature operates under divine superintendence, and the practitioner who sees this is freed from the delusion of doership.

YS II.1 (tapas-svādhyāya-īśvara-praṇidhāna) gives the method: discipline + self-study + devotion to the Lord, the threefold kriyā yoga that culminates in recognizing the adhyakṣa behind all prakṛti.

Hasta asks: whose hand is on the wheel? The answer: mayādhyakṣeṇa — by Mine.

Sāṅkhya Lens

From the sāṅkhya perspective, BG 9.10 is the foundational verse on the prakṛti-puruṣa relationship at the cosmic level. Prakṛti is not self-acting — she is sūyate (passive: "is manifested"). She requires the adhyakṣa — the presiding consciousness — to activate her. This is the macrocosmic version of what YS IV.18 describes microcosmically: the guṇas operate, the puruṣa witnesses. At the cosmic level, the Supreme Person is the adhyakṣa; at the individual level, the jīva's recognition of non-doership is the sāṅkhya discrimination (viveka) that leads to liberation.

The verse uses the passive voice deliberately. sūyate — "is manifested" — not "manifests herself." Prakṛti doesn't self-generate; she is generated. The Hand shapes the clay; the clay doesn't shape itself. For the sāṅkhya practitioner, this means: your body, mind, emotions, and circumstances are prakṛti being manifested — they are being done, not doing. The one who sees this (BG 8.29) "truly sees."

The verse's final word — viparivartate (turns, revolves) — contains the root vṛt, the same root as vṛtti (mental modification) in YS I.2 (citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ). The macrocosmic turning of the universe (jagad viparivartate) and the microcosmic turning of the mind (citta-vṛtti) share the same root because they share the same nature: both are prakṛti in motion. And both operate under superintendence. When the hand turns the wheel, the wheel turns. When the mind's modifications are stilled, the Hand behind them becomes visible.

Today's Hasta energy makes this tangible. Feel your hands. They move by the gunas — nervous system, muscle, bone, prāṇa — all prakṛti. But the adhyakṣa — the intelligence that intends the movement — that is the Hand behind your hand. The sāṅkhya discrimination is not abstract metaphysics. It is felt reality: "I am not the doer; the Hand is. I am the witness." This is viveka. This is freedom.

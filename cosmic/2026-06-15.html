The day begins with Pratipada (first tithi of waxing moon) under Mrigashirsha nakshatra (symbolized by deer's head, representing seeking and searching), under the influence of Ganda yoga (obstacles/poison). This represents the initial stage of spiritual seeking where one encounters obstacles and confusion.

At 9:38 AM, the nakshatra shifts to Ardra (ruled by Rudra, the storm god), representing destruction and transformation - the necessary breaking down of old patterns to make way for new growth.

At 7:01 PM, tithi shifts from Pratipada to Dwitiya, and at 7:08 PM yoga shifts from Ganda (obstacle) to Vridhi (growth/increase). This marks the transition from struggle to progress, from dissolution to manifestation.

The day features Chandra Darshan (first sighting of the new moon), symbolizing the renewal of consciousness after the dark period of Amavasya. Somvar Vrat (Monday fasting) honors Lord Shiva as the destroyer and transformer. Mithuna Sankranti (sun entering Gemini) represents the transition into a new solar month, emphasizing duality and communication.

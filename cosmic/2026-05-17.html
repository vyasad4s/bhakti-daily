Interpretation:

Today marks Chandra Darshan—the first glimpse of the waxing moon after the new moon (Amavasya). This symbolizes new beginnings, renewal, and the gradual return of light. The Pratipada tithi (first lunar day) emphasizes initiation and fresh starts. In the context of BG 5.1, this aligns perfectly: just as the moon begins its cycle of growth, the spiritual practitioner begins the practice of karma-yoga—engaging in action with detachment. The summer season (Grishma Ritu) represents external activity and energy, supporting the theme of energetic engagement in work without attachment. The nakṣatra Rohini, governed by Brahma (the creator), supports creativity and growth in new endeavors.

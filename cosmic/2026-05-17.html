 >>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Ravivāra Energy: Sunday is governed by the Sun, the planet of the soul, vitality, and leadership. When mismanaged, this manifests as ego and pride. When channeled through Bhakti, it becomes the soul's radiant offering—the capacity to lead with humility and dedicate one's actions to the Divine.

Rohini to Mrigashirsha Shift: Rohini (ruled by Brahmā) brings swift, initiating energy—like the spark that starts an action. Mrigashirsha (ruled by Soma) brings the energy of searching and investigation—like the process that refines the action into something valuable. This transition supports the shift from false identification with the doer (impetus) to realization that the action is a flow of prakṛti.

The Waxing Moon's Light: The increasing moonlight symbolizes the growing clarity of the intellect. In this light, the mind can more readily perceive its tendency to claim ownership of actions. By observing the mind during this time, we can catch the subtle whispers of "I am the doer" and replace them with the understanding that "Nature acts; I am merely the medium."

 >>> SĀṄKHYA LENS <<<

The Discernment Realization: Sāṅkhya metaphysics identifies the puruṣa (pure consciousness) as the eternal witness, while prakṛti (material nature) is the source of all activity, change, and modification. The bondage arises when the puruṣa mistakenly identifies with prakṛti’s modifications (the body, mind, and senses). Liberation arises when the puruṣa realizes its separateness and remains unaffected by the changes in prakṛti.

The body and mind continue to function, but the consciousness no longer seeks to "own" or "enjoy" their output. This allows for engagement with the world (offering food, decorating temples) without becoming entangled in the reactions.

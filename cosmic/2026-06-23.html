 >>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

The tithi begins as Sukla Paksha Navami (until 07:09 AM), the ninth tithi of the waxing moon traditionally associated with Lord Rama (Rāma Navami) and the force that overcomes obstacles. The nakṣatra Chitra, ruled by Vishvakarma (the divine architect), governs the early hours, symbolizing skill, craftsmanship, and the ability to construct something beautiful and functional. As Chitra gives way to Swati (ruled by Vāyu, the wind) at 02:23 AM, the energy becomes independent, self‑motivated, and capable of moving freely like the wind—qualities essential for sustained, undeviating remembrance. The yoga Parigha (meaning “obstacle” or “barrier”) prevails until 12:43 AM, representing the challenges that block progress; it then transforms into Siva, an auspicious yoga associated with Lord Shiva’s benevolent grace, which removes obstacles and bestows prosperity. Karana Kaulava (until 07:10 AM) supports stable, productive activity, while Taitila (until 07:52 PM) is favorable for work and accomplishment, and Garija (after 07:52 PM) supports patience and perseverance. The special observance of Mahesh Navami honors Lord Shiva as the supreme deity who destroys ignorance and grants liberation.

Finally, the Shiva‑associated Siva auspiciousness assures us that when we align our will with the divine, the lord of destruction benevolently removes the karmic blockages that bind us to the cycle of birth and death.

>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Ekādaśī is the day of sense control. The number eleven represents the ten senses (five knowledge-gathering, five active) plus the mind — all eleven must be turned toward Viṣṇu. Kamada Ekādaśī specifically grants the kāma (desire) for liberation to those who fast and worship. Aśleṣā nakṣatra embodies the serpent's embrace — binding the soul to matter, yet also the power to release through awareness. The Dhṛti yoga demands not fleeting enthusiasm but steadiness — exactly what BG 2.58 promises to one who masters the tortoise-method of withdrawal.

The viṣṭi karana is called "transformative work" — labor that builds what endures. Sense control is not suppression. It is retractability: the ability to extend and withdraw at will, like the tortoise's limbs, used for service rather than compulsive engagement. Śanivār adds the dimension of karma — every sensory engagement produces imprint (saṁskāra); deliberate withdrawal breaks the chain.

BG 2.58 — The tortoise withdrawing its limbs embodies Ekādaśī's theme: the senses neither rejected nor indulged, but retracted at will. Aśleṣā's serpent energy finds its antidote in the snake-charmer's sovereignty. The Saturday karma-field becomes a laboratory for vairāgya as damage control.

>>> TITHI DEEP DIVE <<<

Kamada Ekādaśī — The word "Kamada" means "desire-fulfilling." Specifically, this Ekādaśī grants the desire for liberation (mokṣa-kāma). The fast is observed from sunrise on Ekādaśī tithi to sunrise on Dvādaśī. During this window, the accumulated reactions of past sensual engagements are suspended — not forever, but the momentum pauses. This is Dhṛti in action: the steadiness to maintain the practice not just today, but as a way of recalibrating the entire sense-apparatus.

>>> NAKṢATRA MYSTERIES <<<

Āśleṣā — The "Embracing" nakṣatra, ruled by Mercury, deity the Nāgas. The serpent's coils represent both the saṁsāra that binds and the kuṇḍalinī that liberates. Those born under Āśleṣā often struggle with possessiveness and control; the spiritual practice is to recognize whose coils are actually doing the binding. On this day, the serpent energy is externalized — one can see the traps clearly and choose to step out of them.

Mercury's rulership means intellect (buddhi) dominates. The danger: overthinking the fast, analyzing the practice instead of doing it. The remedy: mantra. Simple repetition bypasses Mercury's tendency to complicate.

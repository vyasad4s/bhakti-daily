 >>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Kṛṣṇa Tritiyā — the third day of the waning fortnight. The contraction continues. Today carries a rare double transition: both tithi (Dwitiyā → Tritiyā at 9:31 AM) and nakṣatra (Mūla → Pūrvāṣāḍhā at 12:36 PM) shift within the same day. The tithi transition marks the move from consolidation to forward motion — Tritiyā is the first real step into new territory after the second day's holding pattern. The nakṣatra transition is even more significant: Mūla — the root, governed by Nirṛti, goddess of dissolution — surrenders to Pūrvāṣāḍhā, the early victory, governed by Āpaḥ, the cosmic water deities. The movement is from destruction to purification, from uprooting to the waters that cleanse and prepare new growth.

Mūla's energy is not gentle. Nirṛti governs dissolution, the stripping away of the false. When this nakṣatra activates, it does not ask permission — it goes to the foundation of whatever is unstable and exposes it. The Gandamūla period (Mūla active until 12:36 PM) is traditionally considered sensitive — ideal for contemplation and root-cause analysis, not for launching new ventures.

Pūrvāṣāḍhā's deity is Āpaḥ — the cosmic waters. Water purifies, water carries, water dissolves obstacles. The transition from Nirṛti's fire of dissolution to Āpaḥ's waters of purification is the day's central narrative: first the root is exposed, then the waters cleanse what was revealed. The moon in Dhanu (Sagittarius) — the archer — gives direction. The arrow is aimed. The root has been touched. Now the waters carry the seeker toward asaṁśayaṁ samagram — complete knowledge, without doubt.

 >>> SĀṄKHYA LENS <<<

The Right Attachment: In Sāṅkhya, all bondage is the consequence of puruṣa's attachment to prakṛti's modifications. The mind (manas) attaches to objects, and consciousness (puruṣa) misidentifies with those attachments. This is the fundamental error — the root disease that Mūla nakṣatra exposes. BG 7.1 introduces what appears to be a paradox: Kṛṣṇa commands attachment as the path to liberation. But the Sāṅkhya resolution is precise: attachment to prakṛti binds because it reinforces the misidentification of puruṣa with matter. Attachment to puruṣa's true source — the Supreme Person — does the opposite: it dislodges every attachment to prakṛti by establishing consciousness in its proper relationship.

Āśraya as Ground State: The word mad-āśrayaḥ reveals the Sāṅkhya mechanism. Puruṣa is not seeking a new object of attachment — it is returning to its ground. In prakṛti's framework, āśraya (shelter, foundation) is always the three guṇas. In Kṛṣṇa's framework, the true āśraya is the Supreme Person, who is guṇa-atīta — beyond the guṇas. Taking shelter in the guṇas perpetuates saṃsāra. Taking shelter in that which transcends the guṇas dissolves saṃsāra at its root. The one attachment that liberates is the attachment to what is already free.

Terminal metaphor: You've been SSH'd into the wrong server for lifetimes. Mayy āsakta-manāḥ is the redirect — not a new session on the same machine, but a connection to the root server that was always there. The command is simple: tac chṛṇu — just listen. The connection establishes itself.

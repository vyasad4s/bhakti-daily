>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Today carries a rare triple signature: tithi transition, nakṣatra transition, and a major observance. Kṛṣṇa Tritiyā yields to Chaturthi at 11:51 AM — and this is not ordinary Chaturthi but Sankashti, the monthly observance dedicated to Gaṇeśa, remover of obstacles. The cosmic question Sankashti poses is exactly the question BG 7.3 answers: what stands between the seeker and the goal? Kṛṣṇa's response is surgical — the obstacle is not external. It is the rarity of aspiration itself, and the further rarity of surrender beyond accomplishment.

The nakṣatra transition reinforces this. Pūrvāṣāḍhā — "the early victory," governed by Āpaḥ, the water deities — yields to Uttara Aṣāḍhā — "the later victory," governed by the Viśvadeva, the universal gods. The movement is from initial breakthrough to confirmed attainment. Pūrvāṣāḍhā is yatati siddhaye — the effort that tastes the first fruits. Uttara Aṣāḍhā is tattvataḥ vetti — the rarer, deeper knowing that does not merely approach the truth but stands within it. The waters of Pūrvāṣāḍhā cleanse and prepare. The universal gods of Uttara Aṣāḍhā confirm and universalize. The one who knows Kṛṣṇa in truth attains not a sectarian vision but a universal one — the Viśvadeva are precisely the principle of cosmic universality.

Budhavāsara — Wednesday, Mercury's day — governs intellect, communication, the processing of information. But BG 7.3 warns: information is not knowledge, and knowledge is not realization. Mercury can catalog the eight separated energies of prakṛti (BG 7.4). It can map the eightfold nature of material existence. But it cannot produce tattvataḥ — knowledge in truth. That requires a different faculty: not intellect parsing its objects, but consciousness recognizing its Source. Śukla yoga (bright, luminous) holds through the day — the path is illuminated. Then Brahma yoga (sacred knowledge) activates at 11:32 PM. The sequence is exact: first the light, then the sacred knowledge it reveals. The moon's entry into Makara (Capricorn) at 10:11 PM confirms the terrain — the sign of long discipline, the slow climb, the summit that is reached not by speed but by persistence. The one who endures the funnel is the one who reaches the narrow end.

>>> VERSE SELECTION RATIONALE <<<

BG 7.3 Alignment: Yesterday Kṛṣṇa promised complete knowledge. Today He reveals the funnel. The verse is a mathematical statement about the rarity of true realization — two stacked filters, each one narrower than the last. Sankashti Chaturthi is the day of obstacle removal, and the obstacle Kṛṣṇa identifies is not a wall but a narrowing: the path does not block you, it demands more at each stage.

The nakṣatra transition from Pūrvāṣāḍhā (Āpaḥ — early victory, waters of purification) to Uttara Aṣāḍhā (Viśvadeva — later victory, universal confirmation) mirrors the two stages of the funnel. Pūrvāṣāḍhā is sahasreṣu kaścid yatati siddhaye — the one in thousands who begins. Uttara Aṣāḍhā is siddhānāṁ kaścin māṁ vetti tattvataḥ — the one among the perfected who actually knows. Mercury's day sharpens the intellect but reveals its limits — the mind can map the path but cannot walk it to the narrow end.

>>> SĀṄKHYA LENS <<<

The Funnel as Guṇa Filter: In Sāṅkhya, the funnel of BG 7.3 is not random — it is the natural operation of the three guṇas on consciousness. Tamas keeps the majority locked in animal propensities — eating, sleeping, mating, defending — with no surplus attention for the question of the self. Rajas propels some toward achievement, mastery, even spiritual power — but rajasic seeking seeks the fruits of seeking. It can reach siddhi but cannot reach tattvataḥ, because tattvataḥ requires not a better attainment but a different mode entirely. Sattva illuminates, reveals, makes the lamp of knowledge possible — but even sattva is still prakṛti. The one who knows Kṛṣṇa in truth has passed through all three guṇas, not merely arrived at sattva. They have recognized puruṣa not as the content of illumination but as the witness of it.

Aśuddhi as Residual Guṇa Code: YS II.28's aśuddhi — impurities — are the residual traces of guṇa-identification. Even after the gross kleśas have been weakened, the subtle impression "I am this illumination" remains. This is the final filter. The siddha who knows Brahman but not Kṛṣṇa has stopped at the sattvic refinement of prakṛti. The one who passes through knows that even the lamp of knowledge is prakṛti — and that the one who sees by its light is puruṣa, and that puruṣa's source is the Supreme Person.

Terminal metaphor: The funnel is a pipeline with two grep filters. First grep: who is even running the process? Thousands of processes spawned; one is actually executing. Second grep: of those executing, which one has root access to the kernel? Most processes run in user space — they have permissions, they produce output, they look functional. But only the process with root access can read the source. Tattvataḥ is root access. You don't earn it by being a better user-space program. You receive it when the kernel grants it.

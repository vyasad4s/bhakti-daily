>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Kṛṣṇa Pakṣa Dwitīyā: Second day of the waning moon. After Pūrṇimā's fullness, we enter the phase of release and introspection. Actions taken with clear intention are potent; actions from compulsion become binding.

Chitrā → Svāti Nakṣatra Shift (9:54 AM): Tvaṣṭṛ (cosmic architect, structure, perfection) yields to Vāyu (wind, movement, adaptation). This mirrors the shift from Sāṅkhya knowledge to Karma action. Observe when the winds shift—notice your own tendency toward planning versus improvisation.

Harṣaṇa Yoga: "Bringing joy" — supports clear decisions. Arjuna's question, read through this lens, becomes the joy of seeking clarity, not resistance.

Moon in Tula (Libra): Balance, dharma-artha-kāma-mokṣa. The householder's dilemma: how to be in the world but not of it.

>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Mohinī Ekādaśī commemorates the līlā where Lord Vishnu assumed His enchanting female form to distribute amṛta (nectar of immortality) exclusively to the devas while denying the asuras. This discrimination—between what sustains and what poisons—mirrors today's verse theme: the paramparā transmits only to those whose consciousness has been purified through discipline.

Uttara Phālgunī replaces Pūrva Phālgunī at midday, bringing stabilizing, completing energy. Where Pūrva initiates, Uttara sustains—the perfect backdrop for a verse about knowledge transmitted across aeons.

>>> VERSE SELECTION RATIONALE <<<

imaṁ vivasvate yogaṁ proktavān aham avyayam

BG 4.1 — BG 4 (Knowledge) begins with the history of transmission, fitting Mohinī Ekādaśī's theme of distribution. The amṛta of spiritual knowledge is passed from Vivasvān to Manu to Ikṣvāku—today, to us.

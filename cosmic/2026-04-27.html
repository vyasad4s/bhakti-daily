>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Ekādaśī Portal: The eleventh lunar day is the traditional fasting day dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Today's observance is Mohinī Ekādaśī—commemorating the līlā where Lord Vishnu assumed His feminine enchantress form to distribute amṛta (nectar of immortality) to the devas while denying the asuras. This cosmic window represents the discrimination between deva-nature (surrender, receptivity) and asura-nature (grasping, possession).

The transition from Ekādaśī to Dvādaśī at 8:46 AM marks the opening of Parāna (breaking fast). The time before this transition holds the deepest Ekādaśī energy—where sensory withdrawal creates space for spiritual perception. Uttara Phālgunī brings stability and completion energy, supporting the paramparā theme of sustained transmission across generations. This nakṣatra is ruled by Aryamān (the Sun), connecting directly to Vivasvān energy in today's verse.

>>> VERSE SELECTION RATIONALE <<<

imaṁ vivasvate yogaṁ proktavān aham avyayam

Bhagavad-gītā 4.1 — The opening of Chapter 4 (Jñāna-yoga) establishes the paramparā—the imperishable science transmitted from teacher to student in an unbroken chain. Kṛṣṇa to Vivasvān (sun-god), Vivasvān to Manu (father of mankind), Manu to Ikṣvāku. This verse was chosen for Mohinī Ekādaśī because both share the theme of restoration: the Gītā was "lost" through break in succession, just as the nectar was nearly lost to the demons. Knowledge discriminates—it flows only to those with deva-consciousness.

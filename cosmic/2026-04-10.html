BG 3.7 — The True Practitioner vs The Pretender — Selected for Siddha Yoga's demand for authentic accomplishment over performance. Siddha means "perfected" — but Kṛṣṇa reveals true perfection is not renunciation divorced from action, but karma-yoga: sense regulation through the mind, action without attachment, devotion expressed through engaged practice.

The verse bridges BG 3.5 (bound by guṇas) and BG 3.9 (action as sacrifice). It presents the alternative to pretense: sincere practice where the senses are redirected, not denied. The accomplished (viśiṣyate) practitioner integrates tapas (discipline), svādhyāya (self-study), and īśvara-praṇidhāna (devotion) — the three components of kriyā-yoga defined in YS II.1.

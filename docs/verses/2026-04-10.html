🏹 BG 3.7 | 2026-04-10

 

यस्त्विन्द्रियाणि मनसा नियम्यारभतेऽर्जुन ।

कर्मेन्द्रियैः कर्मयोगमसक्तः स विशिष्यते ॥ ७ ॥

 

yas tv indriyāṇi manasā

niyamyārabhate 'rjuna

karmendriyaiḥ karma-yogam

asaktaḥ sa viśiṣyate

 

"On the other hand, if a sincere person tries to control the active senses by the mind and begins karma-yoga [in Kṛṣṇa consciousness] without attachment, he is by far superior."

 

Kṛṣṇa draws a sharp line between sincerity and pretense. The one who regulates the senses by the mind (manasā niyamya), begins karma-yoga through the active sense organs, and acts without attachment — that person is distinguished, superior, accomplished. This is not the forced renunciation of the pretender who denies the senses while mentally dwelling on objects. This is transformation through engaged practice.

 

The key phrase manasā niyamya reveals pratyāhāra in action — not suppression but redirection. The mind becomes the reins that guide the horses of the senses toward productive, unattached action. Śrīla Prabhupāda's purport cuts deep: "A sincere sweeper in the street is far better than the charlatan meditator who meditates only for the sake of making a living."

 

🔥 YS II.1 — tapas-svādhyāya-īśvara-praṇidhānāni kriyā-yogaḥ | Kriyā-yoga consists of discipline, self-study, and devotion to God. BG 3.7 embodies all three: tapas (regulating the senses by the mind), karma-yoga (the path of action), and īśvara-praṇidhāna (the devotion implicit in offering action to the Supreme). Patañjali's threefold definition maps to karma-mārga, jñāna-mārga, and bhakti-mārga — here they integrate into one practice.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

manasā (adv.) — by the mind; the critical faculty that directs attention and serves as the reins controlling the senses | hacker metaphor: manas is your control bus; without it, the peripherals (senses) flood the CPU with garbage data

niyamya (gerund) — regulating, bringing under control; establishing discipline over | ossify system governors to throttle resource usage

karmendriyaiḥ (inst. pl.) — by the active sense organs; the faculties of work and expression (hands, feet, voice, etc.) | your I/O devices configured for output mode

karma-yogam (acc.) — work in devotion; action offered to the Supreme without attachment to results | compiling code for production, not debugging for your own curiosity

asaktaḥ (adj.) — without attachment, not clinging to results; the practice of engaging fully while releasing ownership | run processes without locking the memory

viśiṣyate (pass. 3rd sg.) — is distinguished, excels, is by far superior; stands out from others | optimized algorithm outperforming the naive implementation

 

Practice for Today: Audit one area where you've been performing rather than practicing. Where do you look spiritual but haven't done the inner work? Where do you claim detachment but still calculate outcomes? Make one authentic commitment — not loud, just true.

🏹 BG 4.34 | April 23, 2026

 

तद्विद्धि प्रणिपातेन परिप्रश्नेन सेवया ।

उपदेक्ष्यन्ति ते ज्ञानं ज्ञानिनस्तत्त्वदर्शिनः ॥ ३४ ॥

 

tad viddhi praṇipātena
paripraśnena sevayā
upadekṣyanti te jñānaṁ
jñāninas tattva-darśinaḥ

 

Just try to learn the truth by approaching a spiritual master. Inquire from him submissively and render service unto him. The self-realized souls can impart knowledge unto you because they have seen the truth.

 

Knowledge does not come through solitary intellectual effort. This cuts against the modern assumption that truth can be discovered through individual reason alone. The path of jñāna requires a transmitter and a receiver. The transmitter must be one who has seen (darśinaḥ). The receiver must come in the spirit of surrender, inquiry, and service.

 

Tad viddhi — know this, understand this. The knowledge is not theoretical but existential. Viddhi is from vid (to know) — the same root as Veda. This is not information acquisition but recognition of what IS.

 

Praṇipāta — prostration, surrender before the spiritual master. Not polite respect — it's root access credentials. You surrender your user account to receive the master key. The ego that insists on its own approach is the obstacle to knowing.

 

Paripraśna — inquiry from all sides; submissive questioning aimed at understanding, not debate. The questions arise from service-rendered, not ego-position.

 

Sevayā — service; selfless action without desire for result. You don't learn a codebase by reading it once. You serve it — fix bugs, refactor, maintain. Knowledge embeds through engagement, not observation.

 

Upadekṣyanti — they will impart, teach, transmit. From upa-diś (to indicate, point out). The guru doesn't manufacture knowledge — they point to what the student cannot yet see.

 

Jñāninaḥ tattva-darśinaḥ — the self-realized souls who have seen the truth. Not theoretical knowledge but direct perception. Like a senior engineer who looks at architecture and sees race conditions — not just surface UI.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

praṇipāta — "approaching by bowing down"; complete surrender before the spiritual master. Root access credentials.

paripraśna — "inquiry from all sides"; submissive questioning aimed at understanding, not debate.

sevayā — service; selfless action without desire for result. Knowledge embeds through engagement.

upadekṣyanti — they will impart, teach, transmit; from upa-diś (to point out).

jñāninaḥ — the self-realized souls; those who possess knowledge as direct perception.

tattva-darśinaḥ — "those who see the truth"; direct perception of reality as it is.

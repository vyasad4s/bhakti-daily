🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 2.28 | March 30, 2026

 

अव्यक्तादीनि भूतानि व्यक्तमध्यानि भारत ।

अव्यक्तनिधनान्येव तत्र का परिदेवना ॥ २८ ॥

 

avyaktādīni bhūtāni vyakta-madhyāni bhārata
avyakta-nidhanāny eva tatra kā paridevanā

 

All created beings are unmanifest in their beginning, manifest in their interim state, and unmanifest again when annihilated. So what need is there for lamentation?

 

The instruction is surgical. Whether you believe in the eternal soul or hold atheistic materialism, the logic holds: in the beginning, unmanifest elements. In the middle, manifested forms. In the end, unmanifest atoms. The energy never disappears; it only changes state. The grief—the paridevanā—is fired by ahaṅkāra's identification: "this form was mine; now it's gone." But the form was never yours. It was vyakta (manifest) in your perception for a time, and now returns to avyakta (unmanifest). What changes?

The Vedas call dissolution pralaya; physics calls it entropy. The building stands; then rubble; then the materials warehouse. The unmanifest source is never lost—only temporarily expressing itself as this body, this relationship, this circumstance. To lament is to mistake the app for the platform, the compiled program for the source code. The source code remains.

 

🔥 YS II.1 — तपः-स्वाध्यायेश्वर-प्रणिधानानि क्रिया-योगः | Kriyā Yoga consists of three elements: tapas (the burning away), svādhyāya (self-study), and īśvara-praṇidhāna (surrender to the Supreme). This is how Bhakti, Jñāna, and Karma converge. The materialist sees only the cycle of karma in manifestation; the jñānī sees the cycle itself as māyā; the bhakta sees Īśvara behind the cycle. Kriyā Yoga is the method that burns away the identification causing grief, studies the scripture that reveals the eternal, and surrenders the attachment to the One who manages all dissolution.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

avyakta (n.) — unmanifest, invisible, latent; the subtle seed-state before material manifestation emerges from prakṛti; like source code before compilation.

vyakta (adj.) — manifest, visible, expressed; the material form appearing in interim time; the compiled program running in the simulation.

bhūta (n.) — created being, elemental manifestation; from √bhū, "to become, to come into being"—the evolutes of prakṛti.

paridevanā (f.) — lamentation, excessive grieving, crying out; the emotional reaction fired by ahaṅkāra's misidentification with temporary forms.

nidhana (n.) — annihilation, dissolution, death; the return to unmanifest state; not destruction but transformation.

 

Practice for Today:

When the Ganda Yoga tightens its knot—some attachment, some fear of loss—recite avyaktādīni bhūtāni. Trace the thing backward: what was it before it appeared? Where will it be after it disappears? At Soma Pradoṣa this evening (~7:13 PM sunset), offer the dissolution of your own forms to Śiva. What identities are you clutching that must unmanifest? Grief is optional. Discernment is not.

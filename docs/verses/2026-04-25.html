🏹 BG 4.2 | April 25, 2026

 

एवं परम्पराप्राप्तमिमं राजर्षयो विदुः ।

स कालेनेह महता योगो नष्टः परन्तप ॥ २ ॥

 

एवं परम्पराप्राप्तम्
इमं राजर्षयो विदुः
स कालेनेह महता
योगो नष्टः परन्तप

 

This supreme science was thus received through the chain of disciplic succession, and the saintly kings understood it in that way. But in course of time the succession was broken, and therefore the science as it is appears to be lost.

 

The truth doesn't change, but our ability to perceive it does. The science of yoga is eternal, but its transmission depends on human vessels capable of receiving and relaying without distortion.

 

Evaṁ paramparā-prāptam — received through the succession. Not invented, not discovered through individual effort, but transmitted like source code through git history. Each generation commits the same eternal truth.

 

Rāja-ṛṣayaḥ viduḥ — saintly kings understood. Those who wield authority not for domination but for spiritual protection. The original executive branch of dharma.

 

Saḥ kālena iha mahatā — in the course of great time. Entropy operates on lineages too. Without maintenance, the connection degrades. The signal scatters.

 

Naṣṭaḥ — scattered, lost, broken. Not destroyed (truth is eternal) but ACCESS was severed. When those meant to preserve dharma become its enemies, the succession fractures.

 

Why did it break? The royal order became corrupt — power for exploitation, not protection. This is the Saturn principle: Śani doesn't destroy, he TESTS. He reveals hollow structures. His slow heat tempers steel.

 

"The science as it is appears to be lost." Not irrevocably. Hidden, awaiting restoration by one who speaks with direct authority. Kṛṣṇa speaks now because the chain broke; necessity forced the source to reappear.

 

Yoga Sūtra Connection:

🔥 YS I.14 — sa tu dīrgha-kāla-nairantarya-satkārāsevito dṛḍha-bhūmiḥ

"Practice becomes firmly established when cultivated uninterruptedly and with devotion over a prolonged period."

 

The paramparā IS that prolonged cultivation. Not one lifetime's effort but accumulated abhyāsa and vairāgya of countless practitioners transmitted like a zip file of realized consciousness. When the succession breaks, continuity is severed.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

paramparā — "disciplic succession"; unbroken chain of transmission, like git history preserving truth across generations

prāpta — "received"; obtained through transmission, not self-generated

rāja-ṛṣayaḥ — "saintly kings"; rulers established in dharma, executive branch of spiritual order

viduḥ — "they knew"; direct perception, not theory

kālena mahatā — "in the course of great time"; entropy affects even strongest lineages

naṣṭaḥ — "scattered, lost"; access severed, not truth destroyed

parantapa — "O scorcher of enemies"; Arjuna's epithet indicating capacity to burn obstacles

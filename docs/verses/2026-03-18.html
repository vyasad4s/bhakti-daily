🏹 BHAGAVAD-GĪTĀ 2.70 | MARCH 18, 2026

 

आपूर्यमाणमचलप्रतिष्ठं

समुद्रमापः प्रविशन्ति यद्वत् ।

तद्वत्कामा यं प्रविशन्ति सर्वे

स शान्तिमाप्‍नोति न कामकामी ॥ ७० ॥

 

āpūryamāṇam acala-pratiṣṭhaṁ samudram āpaḥ praviśanti yadvat |
tadvat kāmā yaṁ praviśanti sarve sa śāntim āpnoti na kāma-kāmī ||

 

A person who is not disturbed by the incessant flow of desires – that enter like rivers into the ocean, which is ever being filled but is always still – can alone achieve peace, and not the man who strives to satisfy such desires.

 

The rainy season dumps phenomenal volumes into the ocean, yet the ocean keeps its boundaries, its salt content, its vast unsubtractable depth. Rivers enter, are contained, and continue. This is not denial or suppression – it is fullness. The ocean is full already. Desires, like rivers, are prakṛtic phenomena entering the field of puruṣa-consciousness. They affect nothing because they are received by what needs nothing. This is śānti – not the cultivated calm of a vacation, but the unmovable peace of fulfilled existence.

 

The Kṛṣṇa-conscious devotee is not trying to kill desire. That is the sannyāsī's error – desire denied is desire deferred. The devotee is already full. The ocean doesn't become "more ocean" when rivers pour in. Rivers, noise, traffic, ambitions, rejections – these enter and leave no trace, like water poured into water. This is acala-pratiṣṭha: the steady foundation that does not tremble. Not because it is defended, but because it cannot be reached.

 

🔥 YS I.15 – dṛṣṭānuśravika-viṣaya-vitṛṣnasya vaśīkāra-saṁjñā vairāgyam | The Bhakta follows the path of īśvara-praṇidhāna – devotion as surrender. The Rāja-yogī follows abhyāsa-vairāgya – practice plus dispassion. Here they meet at the shoreline. Vyāsa comments that vairāgya requires indifference to sense objects "whether in their absence or presence." This is the ocean's secret: it doesn't gain or lose when rivers arrive. The vaśīkāra (mastery) of I.15 is not domination – it is the effortless sovereignty of the one who is already complete.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

apāpaviddhaḥ (adjective) – "not disturbed," lit. "un-struck"; the puruṣa remains unaffected by prakṛtic activity. Hacker: a process running at kernel level that cannot be interrupted by user-space signals.

āpūryamāṇam (present passive participle) – "being filled continuously"; from ā-pṛ, to fill. Unlike the kāmī who empties with indulgence, the devotee receives without depletion.

acala-pratiṣṭham (compound adjective) – "immovable foundation" – acala (unmoving) + pratiṣṭha (standing firm). The root access of consciousness that no prakṛtic process can revoke.

samudra (masculine noun) – "ocean"; sam (together) + udra (water). The field containing all phenomena without being modified by them.

vaśīkāra (masculine noun) – "mastery, control"; from vaś (to be subject to). Not domination – the effortless sovereignty of the full.

śānti (feminine noun) – "peace"; from śam (to calm). Not the absence of stimulus but the presence of completion.

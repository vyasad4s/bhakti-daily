🏹 BG 2.13 | March 08, 2026

 

देहिनोऽस्मिन्यथा देहे

कौमारं यौवनं जरा ।

तथा देहान्तरप्राप्तिर्

धीरस्तत्र न मुह्यति ॥

 

dehino 'smin yathā dehe
kaumāraṁ yauvanaṁ jarā
tathā dehāntara-prāptir
dhīras tatra na muhyati

 

As the embodied soul continuously passes, in this body, from boyhood to youth to old age, the soul similarly passes into another body at death. A sober person is not bewildered by such a change.

 

The soul doesn't upgrade or downgrade. It doesn't patch, version, or deprecate. It simply is — while the interface (the body) cycles through states: kaumāraṁ (childhood), yauvanaṁ (youth), jarā (old age). These are not upgrades to the soul. They're skin changes on a character that never levels up or down because it's not part of the leveling system.

 

This is the first drill-bit verse of the Gītā: the beginning of viveka — discrimination between puruṣa (consciousness) and prakṛti (matter). Arjuna stands at the edge of battle seeing bodies everywhere, and Kṛṣṇa drops the payload: "You're seeing version numbers, not source code." The body is the compiled binary. The soul is the developer. Binaries crash, get corrupted, get replaced. The developer doesn't.

 

Dhīra — the sober, the steady — is the one who doesn't panic when the binary changes. This isn't stoicism or emotional suppression. It's seeing clearly. The body feels pain? So be it. The body ages? Naturally. The body dies? Expected behavior. The soul, however, continues executing elsewhere. No data loss. No corrupt files. Just a fresh install.

 

🔥 YS I.2 — yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ
Yoga is the stilling of the changing states of the mind.

 

Both the Gītā and the Yoga Sūtras point to the same architecture: consciousness (puruṣa) sits beneath the changing layers (prakṛti). The body's states — childhood, youth, old age, death, reinstallation — are citta-vṛttis writ large. The mind's fluctuations and the body's transitions are the same phenomenon at different scales. Yoga is the practice of not identifying with the runtime errors. BG 2.13 gives the metaphysical proof; YS I.2 gives the method. Together: Don't debug your illusions. See through them.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

dehī (m.) — the embodied one; the conscious self temporarily inhabiting a body; like a process running on hardware that gets swapped out

deha (m.) — body; the biodegradable interface between soul and world

dehāntara-prāptiḥ (f.) — attainment of another body; death not as termination but migration; no data lost, just new hardware

kaumāraṁ (n.) — childhood; boyhood; the first compiled state

yauvanaṁ (n.) — youth; the system in full operation

jarā (f.) — old age; system degradation; entropy in action

dhīraḥ (m.) — the sober, the steady; one whose intelligence (buddhi) remains stable through changing conditions; the unperturbed observer

muhyati (v.) — becomes deluded, confused; to mistake the temporary for the eternal, the wrapper for the content

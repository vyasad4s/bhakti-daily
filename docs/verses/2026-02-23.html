🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 2.72 | February 23, 2026

 

एषा ब्राह्मी स्थितिः पार्थ

नैनां प्राप्य विमुह्यति

स्थित्वास्यामन्तकालेऽपि

निर्वाणं परमं गतः

 

eṣā brāhmī sthitiḥ pārtha
nainaṁ prāpya vimuhyati
sthitvāsyām anta-kāle ’pi
nirvāṇaṁ paramaṁ gataḥ

 

"That is the divine state which, having attained, he is never deluded; situated in that, even at the end of life, one attains liberation."

 

The sequence completes. After renunciation (2.71), here is the destination. Brahmī-sthitiḥ—the state of being established in Brahman. Not a technique, not a practice, but a transformed condition of consciousness.

 

This is the fire of Kṛttikā fully alight. Agni—the deva who knows all births—witnesses this attainment. Indra yoga declares: this is the supreme rulership, not over external dominions but over the inner cosmos. Saptamī is the seventh day, the day of completion. The wheel turns full circle: from prerequisites (2.66) through struggle, restraint, inversion, fullness, release—NOW the established state.

 

Vimuhyati—never deluded. The kleśa cascade (2.62-2.63) cannot touch one established here. Anta-kāle ’pi—even at death, the ultimate test. The fear that drives all worldly activity proves powerless. Nirvāṇaṁ paramaṁ—the highest extinction, not of existence but of bondage. Somavāra, Śiva's day: moon-governed, mind-governed—now transcended.

 

🔥 YS 1.3 — tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe ’vasthānam — "Then the seer is established in his own essential nature." BG 2.72 describes this establishment. YS 1.3 gives the principle: puruṣa realizing itself as distinct from prakṛti.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

Eṣā (pronoun, feminine nominative) — this; referring to the previously described state

Brāhmī (adj.) — divine, pertaining to Brahman; the state of being established in ultimate reality

Sthitiḥ (feminine noun) — state, position, establishment; from sthā (to stand)

Vimuhyati (verb) — becomes deluded; vi-muh (to be thoroughly confused); negated—never deluded

Anta-kāle (compound, locative) — at the time of death; anta (end) + kāla (time)

Nirvāṇa (neuter noun) — extinction, liberation; nir + vāṇa (blowing out)

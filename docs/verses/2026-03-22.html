🏹 BG 2.24 | March 22, 2026

 

अच्छेद्योऽयमदाह्योऽयमक्ल‍ेद्योऽशोष्य एव च ।

नित्यः सर्वगतः स्थाणुरचलोऽयं सनातनः ॥ २४ ॥

 

acchedyo 'yam adāhyo 'yam
akledyo 'śoṣya eva ca
nityaḥ sarva-gataḥ sthāṇur
acalo 'yaṁ sanātanaḥ

 

This individual soul is unbreakable and insoluble, and can be neither burned nor dried. He is everlasting, present everywhere, unchangeable, immovable and eternally the same.

 

The Bhagavad-gītā continues its surgical dismantling of materialistic assumptions. If the soul cannot be destroyed by any material force, then it exists outside the transactional economy of material cause and effect. This is not sentimentality. This is Source Code.

 

The soul is acchedya (uncuttable) — no blade, no division, no fragmentation can touch it. Adāhya (unburnable) — Agni himself, the devourer of worlds, cannot consume it. Akledya (insoluble) — even dissolution into water cannot disintegrate it. Aśoṣya (undryable) — the winds of time cannot desiccate it.

 

The Sāṅkhya precision here is devastating to materialist philosophy. The soul is nitya (everlasting) — no beginning, no end. Sarva-gata (all-pervading) — present everywhere because consciousness is not spatially bound. Sthāṇu (fixed, immovable) — unlike matter which shifts, transforms, decays. Acala (unshakeable) — external perturbations register but do not alter the soul's essential nature. Sanātana (eternally the same) — the ultimate invariant, the constant beneath all variables.

 

Today's cosmic timing intensifies this teaching: we move from Bharaṇī (Yama, Lord of Death) to Kṛttikā (Agni, sacred fire). The soul cannot be burned. Even the fire that eventually destroys the entire manifest universe at pralaya cannot touch the ātmā. This is the ultimate fireproofing.

 

🔥 YS I.3 — tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe 'vasthānam
"Then the seer abides in its own true nature."

 

The connection is exact: Patañjali describes the goal of yoga as the seer (puruṣa, the soul) resting in its own form, free from the distortions of mental modifications. Kṛṣṇa BG 2.24 describes that form: unbreakable, unburnable, eternal, unchangeable. The yogi does not become this — the yogi recognizes what already is.

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

acchedyaḥ (adj.) — unbreakable, uncuttable, indivisible. The puruṣa is not composed of parts and cannot be fragmented. Unlike prakṛti which undergoes constant transformation, the soul remains integral. Hacker metaphor: the soul is the Read-Only memory that cannot be partitioned, formatted, or corrupted by any process.

adāhyaḥ (adj.) — unburnable, that which cannot be consumed by fire. Even at cosmic dissolution (mahā-pralaya) when Agni devours the elements, the soul persists. Hacker metaphor: the soul runs on a separate power grid; no amount of heat or overload can shut down the system.

sthāṇuḥ (adj.) — fixed, immovable, standing firm like a pillar. While mind and body fluctuate through the guṇas, the soul remains the invariant observer. Hacker metaphor: the soul is the server root directory — unchanging while all files and processes move around it.

sanātanaḥ (adj.) — eternal, perpetually existing, without origin or end. Not "forever" as extended time, but outside time's jurisdiction entirely. Hacker metaphor: sanātanaḥ is the code that runs outside the simulation — the reference frame that persists even when the program restarts.

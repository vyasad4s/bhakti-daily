रागद्वेषविमुक्तैस्तु विषयानिन्द्रियैश्चरन् ।

आत्मवश्यैर्विधेयात्मा प्रसादमधिगच्छति ॥ ६४ ॥

 

rāga-dveṣa-vimuktais tu viṣayān indriyaiś caran
ātma-vaśyair vidheyātmā prasādam adhigacchati

 

But a person free from all attachment and aversion and able to control his senses through regulative principles of freedom can obtain the complete mercy of the Lord.

 

Yesterday we traced the inverted tree of saṃsāra: dhyāyataḥ → saṅgaḥ → kāmaḥ → krodhaḥ → sammohaḥ → smṛti-bhraṁśa → buddhi-nāśa → praṇaśyati. A single uncontrolled sense can uproot a lifetime of practice. Today, Kṛṣṇa gives the counter-sequence: rāga-dveṣa-vimuktaiḥ → prasādam adhigacchati. The solution is not to suppress the senses through artificial austerity (that is merely tamasic) but to engage them under regulative principles — vidheya-ātmā, self under regulation.

 

This verse reveals the architecture of mastery. Rāga is the pull toward pleasant objects; dveṣa is the aversion from unpleasant ones. Both are programs running in the prakṛti-layer, attempting to capture the puruṣa into their loop. The liberated intelligence discriminates: "I am neither the enjoyer nor the rejector. I am consciousness observing both tendencies." The senses then become ātma-vaśyaiḥ — under the self's direction, not the gunas' compulsion. Sāṅkhya reveals: prakṛti is the instrument, puruṣa the witness. When this discrimination is active, the senses function without binding.

 

The result is prasāda — not the vague "mental peace" of pop psychology, but the settled grace of the Lord's mercy. It is the end of oscillation, of the rāga-dveṣa seesaw. The devotee who offers every sense engagement to Kṛṣṇa, who neither desires nor rejects but acts as service requires, finds the senses now function as channels for divine communion rather than hooks for bondage. This is not dissociation from the world; it is total engagement without attachment. The same senses that dragged you into the mire become vehicles for liberation.

 

🔥 YS I.15 — दृष्टानुश्रविकविषयवितृष्णस्य वशीकारसंज्ञा वैराग्यम्
"When the mind loses desire for objects seen or heard, this is the highest detachment."

 

Patañjali's vairāgya and Kṛṣṇa's rāga-dveṣa-vimukta describe the same state from different angles. The Sūtra speaks of vitṛṣṇa — thirstlessness. Kṛṣṇa speaks of vimukta — liberation from both poles of desire. The Sāṅkhya metaphysics bridge them: rāga and dveṣa are modifications of rajas and tamas. When the buddhi discriminates puruṣa from prakṛti, these modifications lose their binding force. The nirveda-vairāgya of the Yoga Sūtra becomes the prasādam of the Bhagavad Gītā — the settled grace of one who understands: "I am not the doer, I am not the enjoyer, I am witness to the drama of the gunas."

 

TERM DEFINITIONS:

rāga (m.) — attachment; the pull toward pleasurable objects; root rañj (to color), that which colors the mind red with craving

dveṣa (m.) — aversion; the push away from unpleasant objects; the flip side of rāga, equally binding

vimuktaiḥ (inst. pl.) — by one who is liberated; vi (intensive) + muc (to release) — the state of having been unbound

viṣayān (acc. pl.) — sense objects; the objects of the five senses, vi-ṣaya (that which spreads out/expands)

indriyaiḥ (inst. pl.) — by the senses; the five senses of perception (jñānendriya) and five of action (karmendriya)

ātma-vaśyaiḥ (inst. pl.) — under one's own control; ātman (self) + vaśya (subject to) — not external compulsion but internal mastery

vidheyātmā (nom.) — one who follows regulated principles; vidheya (what should be done) + ātmā (self) — the regulated self, not the rebellious self

prasādam (acc.) — the mercy/grace of the Lord; settled clarity, the end of mental oscillation; etymologically: pra (forth) + sad (to settle) — that which settles firmly

adhigacchati (3sg. pres.) — attains; obtains; adhi (over) + gam (to go) — goes beyond/achieves

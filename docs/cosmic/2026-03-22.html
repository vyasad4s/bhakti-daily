>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

The cosmic energies present a remarkable alignment with the verse's teaching on the unburnable soul. We begin in Bharaṇī, the nakṣatra of Yama, Lord of Death who determines the boundaries of embodied existence. Then at 1:12 PM, we cross into Kṛttikā, the Pleiades, Agni's own nakṣatra — the constellation of sacred fire itself.

This transition mirrors the verse's revelation: just as Yama cannot claim the eternal soul, neither can Agni consume it. Death and Fire — the two great transformers of the material world — both fail against the sanātana.

Sunday, Ravi-vāra, adds another layer: the Sun (Sūrya) is the visible representative of the soul's indestructibility. Just as the sun rises daily regardless of earthly events, so the soul abides regardless of bodily changes.

Vaiśākha Śukla Daśamī → Ekādaśī (tithi transition 8:37 AM ET)

Mohinī Ekādaśī — Lord Viṣṇu takes the form of enchantress Mohinī to rescue nectar from demons. A day of divine discernment: perception of truth amidst illusion. Ekādaśī energy prepares the vessel through fasting and sense withdrawal, creating the inner space where knowledge can manifest.

Pūrva Phālgunī Nakṣatra (from 10:57 AM)
Ruled by Venus. Phālgunī means "reddish" — the color of creative flowering. This nakṣatra supports union, creative manifestation, and enjoyment within oneself (ātmani vindati). Unlike Pūrva Aṣāḍha's binding energy, Phālgunī offers liberation through creative fulfillment. The knowledge described in BG 4.38 is precisely this — not external acquisition but internal enjoyment.

Vṛddhi → Dhruva Yoga (from 12:57 PM)
Vṛddhi (growth, increase) transitions to Dhruva (fixed, constant, unwavering). This mirrors the verse trajectory: from practice that grows and expands (yoga-saṁsiddhaḥ) to knowledge that becomes fixed and self-luminous. Dhruva yoga is named after the boy who became the pole star through unwavering devotion — now the very emblem of fixedness. This yoga provides the stable foundation upon which the peace of knowledge rests.

Solar/Lunar Position: Sun in Meṣa (Aries — own sign, exalted strength), Moon in Siṁha (Leo — sign of the Sun). Double-fire configuration supports the burning clarity of knowledge that consumes doubt.

Synthesis: Ekādaśī prepares the vessel through fasting; Phālgunī's creative energy transforms knowledge into lived reality; Dhruva provides the stability for that knowledge to become permanent. The transition from 10→11 mirrors practice→maturity.

Bhagavad-gītā 4.38-39 — The day's energy demands a verse about knowledge as mature fruit. Mohinī Ekādaśī specifically is about divine discernment — seeing truth when illusion seems most convincing. BG 4.38-39 speaks to this: knowledge appears not through forced effort but as the organic ripening of sustained practice. The Ekādaśī fast creates the sense-withdrawal (saṁyata-indriyaḥ) that verse 39 demands. Dhruva's stability guarantees the peace that verse 38 promises is found "within oneself" (ātmani vindati).

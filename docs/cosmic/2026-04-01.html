>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Pūrṇimā is the moon at maximum expression — yet poised at the threshold of diminishment. This is the perfect metaphor for sama-duḥkha-sukha: at the peak of fullness, one knows emptiness is coming. At the depth of loss, one knows return is inevitable. The dhīra inhabits this knowledge as current reality, not future consolation.

 

Hasta nakṣatra — the hand of Savitṛ, the solar deity who impels — emphasizes skill in action, not mere beliefs about action. This aligns with Dhruva yoga — the unwavering, fixed quality of this day. Dhruva means "constant" — like the pole star, steady amidst all rotation.

 

Kanyā Rāśi (Virgo) hosting the moon emphasizes analytical discrimination — the ability to sort, to distinguish, to categorize with precision. This is viveka in action: the intellect (buddhi) functioning with clarity to differentiate puruṣa from prakṛti.

 

Hanumān Jayantī — the appearance of the perfect devotee — underscores the dhīra quality. Hanumān is the epitome of sama-duḥkha-sukha: his devotion to Rāma never wavered through abduction, war, loss, victory, reunion, separation. The bhakta's equanimity is not detachment from the beloved but steadiness in relationship, regardless of circumstance.

 

Sarvārtha Siddhi Yoga — success in all endeavors — combines with Hasta nakṣatra and Wednesday to make this a potent window for beginning or completing work. The yogi utilizes such windows not for prakṛtic gain but for advancing sādhana.

BG 2.15 — The Equanimous Yogi

 

Cosmic Alignment: BG 2.15's sama-duḥkha-sukha finds its perfect panchāṅga expression on Pūrṇimā — the moon at peak fullness yet poised to wane. The verse teaches non-disturbance by opposites; Pūrṇimā embodies the opposites' fruit: full and empty, bright and dark, rising and setting.

 

Hasta nakṣatra — the crafting hand — connects to kṣatriya duty performed with skill and steadiness. Arjuna must fight with Hasta-nimitta precision: each action deliberate, neither intoxicated by victory nor devastated by loss.

 

Dhruva Yoga — the unwavering — names the quality of the dhīra explicitly. The day's cosmic signature is steadiness.

 

Hanumān Jayantī completes the alignment: the pure devotee's devotion never wavered through every extreme circumstance. His appearance anniversary on Pūrṇimā emphasizes sama-bhāva in bhakti — the apogee of equanimity.

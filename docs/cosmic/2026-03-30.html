The moon waxes thirteenth-day full—tonight the tithi turns from Trayodaśī to Caturdaśī as the lunar disk climbs toward tomorrow's Pūrṇimā. Śukla Pakṣa is the waxing-womb, the building momentum toward manifestation's peak. Tonight's transition is critical: we're one day from fullness, one phase from completion.

The nakṣatra shift from Pūrva to Uttara Phālgunī is Venus maturing into duty. Pūrva is the pleasure-pavilion (Bhaga, the primordial enjoyer); Uttara is Aryaman's domain of cosmic order and responsibility. Today's cosmic asks: can the enjoyment mature into responsibility without attachment? Or will you lament when the pleasure-form dissolves into duty-form?

Ganda Yoga means "the Knot"—literally a tangle, a binding, an obstacle that must be cut. This is precisely the condition the verse addresses: the knot of identifying with the vyakta (manifest) and forgetting the avyakta (unmanifest source). Soma Pradoṣa at sunset is Śiva's dissolving energy cutting through this knot. The form in which your attachment binds you today is the Ganda. The form in which Śiva cuts it tonight is also the Ganda—if you're paying attention.

BG 2.28 — Dissolution and Rebirth

The verse speaks directly to Śukla Trayodaśī → Caturdaśī. Manifest → unmanifest → manifest: this is the cycle the moon demonstrates every fortnight. The sun demonstrates it daily. Your life demonstrates it continuously. What Vedas call utpatti-sthiti-laya (creation-preservation-dissolution), the Gītā calls avyakta-vyakta-avyakta.

Why this verse today? The manifest form (vyakta) is transitioning: nakṣatra, tithi, sunrise to sunset. The unmanifest source (avyakta) is right there—Brahma Muhūrta before dawn, Pradoṣa at dusk. The verse teaches the sameness between beginning and end. Your grief assumes difference; the Gītā reveals identity.

Ganda Yoga-tangled? All knots resolve in the avyakta—if you can perceive it.

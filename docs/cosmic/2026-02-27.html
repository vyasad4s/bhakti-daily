>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Ekadashi → Dwadashi tithi — the 11th day completing into the 12th, approaching Pūrṇimā. Ekadashi is the fasting day, the day of breaking material attachments. Dwadashi is completion, offering, fulfillment. The tithi of TRUE renunciation—not escape but conscious detachment.

Punarvasu nakṣatra — "Return to the Good," Aditi's shelter. The star of revival after storm. Arjuna's desire to escape (anārambhāt) is the storm; Kṛṣṇa answers with the shelter of right action. You cannot hide from work; you must transform it.

Puṣya nakṣatra — "The Nourisher," Bṛhaspati's star. The transition from Punarvasu to Puṣya mirrors the transition from avoidance to engagement: from the shelter of escape to the nourishment of proper action.

Saubhagya Yoga — good fortune, auspiciousness. The yoga of success and prosperity. Ironically, the day of "good fortune" teaches that neither escapism nor pretense leads to real success—only karma-yoga does.

Śukravāra — Venus's day, the day of value, resources, and refinement. Not acquisition but appreciation. The householder's day par excellence.

>>> VERSE SELECTION RATIONALE <<<

na karmaṇām anārambhāt...

BG 3.4 — After establishing two paths (3.3), Kṛṣṇa now destroys false approaches. The cosmic energies—Ekadashi (fasting, breaking attachments → Dwadashi completion), Saubhagya (good fortune), Punarvasu→Puṣya—perfectly support the teaching: neither escapism nor hypocritical renunciation works. Only karma-yoga: action without attachment. The fasting day teaches what false renunciation cannot.

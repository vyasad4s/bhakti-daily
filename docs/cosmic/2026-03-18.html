>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

The Amāvāsyā moon is invisible – not absent, but withdrawn. Like the ocean that receives rivers without being seen to change, the dark moon absorbs solar light without reflecting it. This is the cosmic mirror of apāpaviddhaḥ: the capacity to contain phenomena without displaying reaction.

Pūrva Bhādrapadā, ruled by Ajaikapāda (Śiva's one-footed ascetic form), carries the fire of sacrifice. The deity stands on a single leg – the epitome of acala-pratiṣṭha. As day progresses to Uttara Bhādrapadā, energy shifts from fierce renunciation to sustained endurance.

Moon's transition from Kumbha (intellectual detachment) to Mīna (emotional surrender) mirrors BG 2.70. Kumbha observes prakṛti without immersion. Mīna is dissolution – the oceanic consciousness containing all rivers.

>>> VERSE SELECTION RATIONALE <<<

BG 4.3 reveals why knowledge is transmitted — not to everyone, but only to the qualified vessel. Two conditions: bhaktaḥ asi (you are My devotee) and sakhā ca (and My friend). This dual requirement maps perfectly onto today's cosmic portal:

Hasta → Chitrā Transition: Hasta (the hand, skill, action) yields to Chitrā (the brilliant gem, radiant intellect). The devotee's active service (bhakti) becomes the polished gem that reflects truth (knowledge). Just as the hand must first work the stone, devotion must precede the revelation of the secret.

Caturdaśī Portal: The 14th lunar day — threshold before Amāvāsyā's dissolution. This is the juncture where form (bhakti's external practices) yields to formlessness (the inner friendship with the Lord). BG 4.3 speaks at this exact threshold: the external devotee becomes the internal friend.

Narasimha Jayanti: The Lord appeared specifically for His devotee — not for philosophical advancement, not for cosmic administration, but for pure love. Five-year-old Prahlāda didn't have śāstra-knowledge; he had devotion. That devotion called forth the rahasya (the secret) from the pillar. Today, the same principle: qualified devotion reveals the supreme secret.

>>> COSMIC INTERPRETATION <<<

Narasimha Jayanti: The day the Lord appeared as man-lion from the pillar to protect His devotee Prahlāda. The avatāra appeared at twilight (neither day nor night), from the pillar (neither inside nor outside), and placed Hiraṇyakaśipu on His lap (neither earth nor sky). Perfect synthesis of opposites — transcending all dualities while remaining present for the devotee.

Amāvāsyā approaches: The dark moon where forms dissolve — perfect for receiving the rahasya, the secret that transcends form. Sāṅkhya principle: when apara-prakṛti (manifest nature) dissolves, para-prakṛti (transcendental nature) becomes accessible. The Lord becomes visible only when material vision ceases.

Hasta → Chitrā: Hasta (Savitar's hand, skill-in-action) gives way to Chitrā (Tvasṭṛ's brilliant artifice, the cosmic jewel). The nakṣatra of craftsmanship (Hasta) yields to the nakṣatra of revelation (Chitrā). This is the paramparā process: the guru's hand transmits, the disciple's intellect reflects.

Vajra Yoga: Thunderbolt energy — from Harṣaṇā (joy) to Vajra (indestructible force). The Lord's appearance to the devotee is not sentimental; it's the diamond-hard reality that cuts through illusion. Prahlāda's faith was vajra — unbreakable despite torture. Today's Vajra yoga supports this quality.

>>> SĀṄKHYA LENS <<<

The Structure of Transmission: In Sāṅkhya metaphysics, knowledge (jñāna) passes from buddhi to buddhi through the interface of devotion. BG 4.3 describes this paramparā mechanic:

• Puruṣa (the Lord) remains unchanged — He is the source

• Praṇidhāna (devotion) creates the receiving buddhi — this is bhakti

• Sakhya (friendship) establishes the reciprocal relationship — sāmya, equality of vision

The rahasya (secret) is not information but the capacity to receive para-prakṛti's direct perception. Like two tuning forks of identical pitch — when the guru's buddhi vibrates with truth, the disciple's buddhi resonates. But the resonance requires: (1) proper pitch (qualification through devotion), and (2) proximity (sakhya, the intimate relationship).

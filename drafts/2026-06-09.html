BG 7.29 — Liberation through Refuge

jarā-maraṇa-mokṣāya mām āśritya yatanti ye

te brahma tad viduḥ kṛtsnam adhyātmaṁ karma cākhilam ॥ २९ ॥

jarā-maraṇa-mokṣāya mām āśritya yatanti ye te brahma tad viduḥ kṛtsnam adhyātmaṁ karma cākhilam

Intelligent persons who are endeavoring for liberation from old age and death take refuge in Me in devotional service. They are actually Brahman because they entirely know everything about transcendental activities.

Commentary

This verse declares that intelligent beings seeking liberation from old age and death take refuge in the Lord. By doing so, they attain complete knowledge of transcendental activities.

Yoga Sūtra Bridge: YS II.45

समाधिसिद्धिः ईश्वरप्रणिधानात्

samādhi-siddhiḥ īśvara-praṇidhānāt

Surrender to Īśvara brings about the perfection of samādhi.

BG 7.29 and YS II.45 are two expressions of the same truth. Kṛṣṇa declares that those who take refuge in Him (āśritya) for liberation from old age and death (jarā-maraṇa-mokṣāya) attain complete knowledge of transcendental activities (kṛtsnam adhyātmaṁ karma). Patañjali declares that surrender to Īśvara (īśvara-praṇidhānāt) brings the perfection of samādhi (samādhi-siddhiḥ). The "liberation from old age and death" in the Gītā corresponds to the transcendence of the fluctuations of citta that yoga seeks to still. Just as the cycle of birth and death binds the living entity to material nature, the citta-vṛtti binds the consciousness to the external world. Surrender to the Lord is the direct method to transcend both: in the Gītā, by surrendering to Kṛṣṇa one is freed from the cycle of samsāra; in the Yoga Sūtras, by surrendering to Īśvara one attains samādhi-siddhi. Vyāsa's bhāṣya on II.45 confirms that īśvara-praṇidhāna is the most direct path because it targets the root cause (avidyā) rather than the symptoms. Kṛṣṇa’s call to surrender is not passive resignation but active alignment with the Divine Will, which dissolves the grip of time and fear, freeing the consciousness to know its eternal nature.

Rahu Kaal (4:37–6:29 PM) and Yamaganda (9:12 AM–11:04 AM) create inauspicious windows for material endeavors, supporting inward reflection and spiritual practice. Abhijit Muhurat (12:25–1:25 PM) and Amrit Kaal (07:05 PM–08:40 PM) offer auspicious windows for spiritual practice and surrender. This day’s energy aligns perfectly with BG 7.29: intelligent beings seeking liberation from the cycle of birth and death (jarā-maraṇa) take refuge in the Lord (āśritya) and thereby attain complete knowledge of transcendental activities (kṛtsnam adhyātmaṁ karma). The Navami energy empowers the seeker to confront and transcend the obstacles of time; Uttara Bhadrapada’s fiery purification burns away the illusion of mortality; the Moon in Meena awakens devotion and surrender; and the Ayushman yoga supports the vitality needed for the journey toward liberation.

Sanskrit Terms

āśritya — Taking refuge, shelter, or surrender. Like gaining 'root access' to the divine system — the highest privilege that grants unrestricted access to all functions.

viduḥ — They who know, perceive, or realize. Like having a 'debugger's console' that reveals the true source code of reality beyond surface appearances.

kṛtsnam — Everything, all, the totality. Like obtaining a 'full memory dump' of the universe — seeing all variables, states, and processes in their entirety.

adhyātmaṁ — Transcendental, spiritual, beyond the material. Like accessing 'kernel mode' or 'ring 0' privileges where one operates at the fundamental level of reality.

karma — Action, activity, or work. Like making a 'system call' to the divine OS — requesting service from the transcendental layer.

akhilam — Entirely, completely, wholly. Like achieving '100% test coverage' or exhaustive verification that leaves no corner unexamined.

Practice for Kṛṣṇa Navami

MORNING_APPLICATION: During Brahma Muhūrta (03:53–04:41 AM), chant BG 7.29 slowly. Focus on the cycle: jarā-maraṇa-mokṣāya (liberation from old age and death) and mām āśritya (taking refuge in Me). Reflect on where in your life you feel the grip of time — aging, mortality, the fear of ending. Then affirm: I take refuge in Kṛṣṇa. Let the Navami energy empower you to confront these obstacles, and let the Uttara Bhadrapada fire burn away the illusion of temporality.

EVENING_APPLICATION: After the Moon has fully settled in Meena (post-sunset), sit in quiet reflection. The Moon in Meena awakens compassion and devotion. Offer your japa or kirtan to Kṛṣṇa, focusing on the feeling of surrender (āśritya). If you performed morning practice, notice any shift in perception — does the fear of time feel less binding? Use the auspicious window of Amrit Kaal (07:05 PM–08:40 PM) for extended japa or study, knowing that the Lord’s energy supports your effort to attain liberation from the cycle of birth and death. Tonight, meditate on the image: te brahma tad viduḥ — those who take refuge in Him know the absolute truth. Let the Yoga Sūtra’s promise of samādhi-siddhi remind you that surrender is not defeat but the gateway to the perfection of knowledge.

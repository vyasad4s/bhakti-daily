Bhagavad-gītā 4.7

यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य ग्लानिर्भवति भारत ।
अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम् ॥७॥

yadā yadā hi dharmasya glānir bhavati bhārata
abhyutthānam adharmasya tad ātmānaṁ sṛjāmy aham

yadā yadā — whenever and wherever; hi — certainly; dharmasya — of religion; glāniḥ — discrepancies; bhavati — become manifested; bhārata — O descendant of Bharata; abhyutthānam — predominance; adharmasya — of irreligion; tadā — at that time; ātmānam — self; sṛjāmi — manifest; aham — I.

"Whenever and wherever there is a decline in religious practice, O descendant of Bharata, and a predominant rise of irreligion — at that time I descend Myself."

ĀŚLEṢĀ NAVAMĪ: The Serpent Unveils

Friday, April 24, 2026 | Śukla Pakṣa Navamī (9th Waxing Day) | Āśleṣā Nakṣatra

Today's cosmic configuration carries the precise energy of revelation. We are on Navamī, the ninth day of the waxing moon — a power day associated with the Goddess Durga in her fierce manifestation, the day when cosmic energy peaks before culmination. This is not a subtle whisper. It is the moment when what was concealed can no longer remain hidden.

The nakṣatra dominating today is Āśleṣā — the coiled serpent. Its symbol is the serpent wound upon itself, holding potential energy in stillness until the moment of striking. Āśleṣā's deity is the Nāgas, the serpent beings of ancient wisdom who guard the treasures of the earth and reveal them only to those who approach with respect. This is penetrating insight made manifest — the sudden clarity that strikes through illusion like a serpent's fangs through darkness.

Consider the parallel: when dharma declines and adharma rises, the truth does not gradually become obscure. It is suddenly, completely hidden beneath layers of rationalization, comfort, and collective delusion. The decline Śrī Kṛṣṇa describes is not gentle erosion but systematic burial. And His response is not gentle persuasion but manifestation — sṛjāmi, He sends Himself forth.

Commentators note that sṛjāmi does not mean "create" here — the Lord's form is eternal, never created. It means to make manifest what was already present but unrecognized. This is Āśleṣā energy precisely: the serpent uncoiling to reveal what was always there, simply hidden in the darkness of our own inattention.

The timing bears noting. Āśleṣā begins its influence at 10:44 AM, and the tithi transition from Aṣṭamī to Navamī occurred at 9:52 AM — mere minutes apart. The portal opens and the power day begins in the morning hours. This is the day for direct perception, for cutting through — not analysis, not gradual understanding, but sudden recognition.

Tonight the moon rises at 12:35 PM and travels through Karka (Cancer), the sign of the nurturing Mother, while the sun blazes in Meṣa (Aries), the sign of the warrior. The combination is precisely what this verse demands: the fierce clarity of the warrior (Aries) expressed through the vehicle of divine compassion (Cancer). The Lord does not descend as a distant judge but as the One who cares enough to intervene.

Yoga Sūtra Connection: Devotion as Descent

Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras I.23 states: Īśvara praṇidhānād vā — "Or, [the state of samādhi is attainable] from devotion to the Lord."

This is the vertical axis connecting today's verse to practice. BG 4.7 describes the Lord's descent — His movement from the transcendental realm into the manifest to restore dharma. YS I.23 describes our corresponding ascent — the devotee's movement from the manifest toward the transcendental through surrender.

Patañjali uses the word praṇidhāna — surrender, devotion, placing oneself completely before. This is not mere worship from a distance. It is the complete orientation of one's being toward Īśvara, the recognition that without divine grace, the obstacles (kleśas) cannot be overcome. Vyāsa comments that just as the Lord descends by His own will when dharma declines, so the practitioner attains through surrender to that will.

The serpent of Āśleṣā is often feared, but in yogic symbolism it represents kuṇḍalinī — the coiled power at the base of the spine that, when awakened, ascends to unite with the divine. Today's cosmic energy mirrors this: the Lord descends (avatāra) and the devotee's energy ascends (kuṇḍalinī). They meet at the heart.

Today's Practice

Āśleṣā Penetration Meditation (20 minutes)

Āśleṣā is about seeing through — not looking at, but piercing into. In your sitting practice today:

  1. First ten minutes: Focus on the concept "Where have I allowed dharma to decline in my own conduct?" Not self-condemnation — honest inventory. Where have I accepted lower standards because they were comfortable?
  2. Second ten minutes: Shift to praṇidhāna. Not "what can I do" but "what is already being done." The Lord descends again and again. Can you feel the descent? The Sanskrit root sṛj means to flow forth, to release like water breaking through a dam. Feel that release in the chest.

Chanting Recommendation: The Mahāmantra today, with emphasis on the names as manifestation — not calling to someone distant, but invoking presence already here. Chant after moonrise (post-12:35 PM) when Āśleṣā energy is fully active.

Contemplation for Navamī: The ninth position is just before completion. What in your practice is at the ninth stage — nearly complete, requiring only the final push? Today's energy is that push.

Jaya Śrī Vyāsa! The serpent uncoils. The Lord descends. The truth reveals itself.

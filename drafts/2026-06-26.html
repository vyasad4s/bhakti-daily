Bhagavad Gita Chapter 8, Verse 22

पुरुष: स पर: पार्थ भक्त्या लभ्यस्त्वनन्यया । यस्यान्त:स्थानि भूतानि येन सर्वमिदं ततम् ॥ २२ ॥
puruṣaḥ sa paraḥ pārtha
bhaktyā labhyas tv ananyayā
yasyāntaḥ-sthāni bhūtāni
yena sarvam idaṁ tatam
The Supreme Personality of Godhead, who is greater than all, is attainable by unalloyed devotion. Although He is present in His abode, He is all-pervading, and everything is situated within Him.
It is here clearly stated that the supreme destination, from which there is no return, is the abode of Kṛṣṇa, the Supreme Person. The Brahma-saṁhitā describes this supreme abode as ānanda-cinmaya-rasa, a place where everything is full of spiritual bliss. All the variegatedness manifest there is of the quality of spiritual bliss – nothing there is material. That variegatedness is expanded as the spiritual expansion of the Supreme Godhead Himself, for the manifestation there is totally of the spiritual energy, as explained in Chapter Seven. As far as this material world is concerned, although the Lord is always in His supreme abode, He is nonetheless all-pervading by His material energy. So by His spiritual and material energies He is present everywhere – both in the material and in the spiritual universes. Yasyāntaḥ-sthāni means that everything is sustained within Him, within either His spiritual or material energy. The Lord is all-pervading by these two energies. He is the supreme merciful Deity, and although situated there as one He has expanded Himself into millions and millions of plenary expansions. Cosmic Interpretation

The tithi is Sukla Paksha Dwadashi (until 12:52 PM), the twelfth tithi of the waxing moon associated with the completion of the vrata and the reception of its spiritual benefits. As Dwadashi yields to Trayodashi at 12:52 PM, the energy shifts to the thirteenth tithi, associated with Kamadeva (god of desire) and the fulfillment of desires through divine grace. The nakṣatra Vishaka, ruled by Indra and Agni (gods of strength and fire), governs the early morning hours until 09:45 AM, symbolizing focus, determination, and the ability to overcome obstacles through divine strength. As Vishaka gives way to Anuradha (ruled by Mitra, god of friendship and alliance) at 09:45 AM, the energy becomes cooperative, harmonious, and supportive of sustained devotional effort. The yoga Sadhya (meaning “auspicious” or “favorable”) prevails from 02:08 AM until 03:02 AM on June 27, signifying smooth progress and positive outcomes in one’s spiritual practice, after which Subha (excellent, auspicious) brings continued grace and blessings.

BG 8.22 beautifully aligns with this cosmic tapestry: “The Supreme Personality of Godhead, who is greater than all, is attainable by unalloyed devotion. Although He is present in His abode, He is all-pervading, and everything is situated within Him.” Just as Dwadashi’s energy of completion and reception of benefits gives way to Trayodashi’s fulfillment of desires through grace, and as Vishaka’s focused, determined energy carries the practitioner forward with strength—so too does the devotee, by employing unalloyed devotion (ananya bhakti), attain the Supreme who is greater than all. The declaration that the Supreme is “all-pervading” and that “everything is situated within Him” mirrors the day’s progression from determination (Vishaka) to cooperation (Anuradha) and from favorability (Sadhya) to excellence (Subha). Sadhya’s energy of auspicious favor affirms that such devoted practice is met with divine grace, while Subha’s continued auspiciousness supports the deepening of the spiritual relationship.

The verse invites us to understand that the Supreme is not distant but all-pervading, sustaining everything within Himself. In the language of the energy centers, this is the realization that determination (Vishaka) flows from devotion, cooperation (Anuradha) is perfected in service to the Divine, and favorable conditions (Sadhya) support the journey while excellence (Subha) rewards perseverance. Thus the devotee transforms the focused energy of Vishaka into a stepping stone toward the eternal goal, where Krishna’s all-pervading presence is experienced through the single‑pointed practice of ananya bhakti—unalloyed, undeviating devotion.

Connections & Key Concepts

Yoga Sutra Connection

YS I.23: īśvara-praṇidhānāt vā
Or [samādhi is attained] by devotion to the Lord

Connection: BG 8.22 emphasizes that the Supreme is attainable by unalloyed devotion (ananya bhakti). Yoga Sutra I.23 states that samādhi (perfect absorption) can be attained through devotion to Īśvara (the Lord). Both point to the same essential truth: exclusive devotion to the Divine leads to the highest spiritual realization. The verse’s declaration that the Supreme is “attainable by unalloyed devotion” is the practical application of īśvara‑praṇidhāna, where the mind’s ceaseless flow is directed toward Krishna, resulting in the attainment of the supreme abode (paraṁ sthānam). Thus the sutra provides the methodological foundation for the verse’s promise of spiritual attainment through devotion alone.

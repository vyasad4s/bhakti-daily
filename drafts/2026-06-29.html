Bhagavad Gita Chapter 8, Verse 20

परस्तस्मात्तु भावोऽन्योऽव्यक्तोऽव्यक्तात्सनातन: । य: स सर्वेषु भूतेषु नश्यत्सु न विनश्यति ॥ २० ॥ २० ॥
paras tasmāt tu bhāvo ’nyo ’vyakto ’vyaktāt sanātanaḥ yaḥ sa sarveṣu bhūteṣu naśyatsu na vinaśyati
Yet there is another unmanifest nature, which is eternal and is transcendental to this manifested and unmanifested matter. It is supreme and is never annihilated. When all in this world is annihilated, that part remains as it is.

Kṛṣṇa’s superior, spiritual energy is transcendental and eternal. It is beyond all the changes of material nature, which is manifest and annihilated during the days and nights of Brahmā. Kṛṣṇa’s superior energy is completely opposite in quality to material nature. Superior and inferior nature are explained in the Seventh Chapter.

Yoga Sūtra Connection

YS I.3: tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe ‘vasthānam – “When that is accomplished, the seer abides in its own true nature.”

Just as verse 8.20 describes an eternal, unmanifest reality that persists beyond the annihilation of the material world, the Yoga Sūtra teaches that the true Self (draṣṭu, puruṣa) stands firm in its own nature when the mind’s essence when the fluctuations of consciousness cease. The full moon’s illuminated disc reflects the sun’s light without being altered—symbolizing the puruṣa reflecting consciousness while remaining untouched by the phases of prakṛti.

Hacker Metaphor: The Read‑Only Memory (ROM)

In computing, ROM (Read‑Only Memory) holds the firmware that persists when power is cut—unlike RAM, which loses its state. The material world (manifest and unmanifest) is like volatile RAM: it cycles through creation and dissolution (the days and nights of Brahmā). The eternal nature described in verse 8.20 is the ROM: the immutable source‑code of consciousness that survives every power‑cycle. Chanting oṁ or the Hare Kṛṣṇa mantra is akin to executing the BIOS boot routine that hands control over to the ROM, allowing the system to boot into the spiritual plane (Goloka/Vaikuṇṭha) rather than rebooting into the material cycle.

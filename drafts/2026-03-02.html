🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 3.7

Date: March 02, 2026

Sanskrit:

यस्त्विन्द्रियाणि मनसा नियम्यारभतेऽर्जुन ।

कर्मेन्द्रियैः कर्मयोगमसक्तः स विशिष्यते ॥ ३.७ ॥

Transliteration:

yas tv indriyāṇi manasā
niyamyārabhate 'rjuna
karmendriyaiḥ karma-yogam
asaktaḥ sa viśiṣyate

Translation:

"One who controls the senses by the mind and begins karma-yoga [in Kṛṣṇa consciousness] without attachment, he is by far superior."

Commentary:

The contrast is complete. Yesterday exposed the pretender — one who restrains the body while the mind clings to sense objects. Today reveals the superior path — karma-yoga with asakti (non-attachment). Kṛṣṇa declares this person viśiṣyate — not just better, but by far superior.

The sequence is deliberate: verse 3.6 described the hypocrite; verse 3.7 reveals the authentic practitioner. The difference is not external restraint but internal alignment. The true yogī uses manasā niyamya — regulating through the mind, which has been submitted to Kṛṣṇa. The senses follow the mind; the mind follows the divine.

Prabhupāda emphasizes: better a sincere sweeper than a charlatan meditator. The varṇāśrama system exists not to suppress life but to transform it into yajña — sacrifice for Viṣṇu. Every ordinary action becomes worship when offered without attachment.

The key lies in the Sanskrit asaṅga — without attachment. Not aversion; not suppression. Action without the hooks of desire. Yesterday verse showed what happens when we try to stop acting while the mind still wants; today verse shows how to act in freedom.

Sankhya Concepts:

The guṇas of prakṛti still operate — this is key. The true yogī does not transcend the guṇas by force; they transform action through consciousness (cit). The puruṣa remains unattached witness while buddhi directs all action toward the Supreme.

Yoga Sutra Connection:

🔥 YS 1.12 — abhyāsa-vairāgyābhyāṁ tat-nirodhaḥ — "The fluctuations of the mind are restrained by practice AND detachment."

🔥 YS 2.2 — samādhi-bhāvana-arthaḥ kleśa-tanū-karaṇa-arthaś ca — "For cultivating samādhi and for thinning the afflictions."

Sanskrit Terms:

  • Yaḥ — one who; the subject being described.
  • Tu — but; on the other hand. Contrasting particle.
  • Indriyāṇi — the five senses of perception (jñānendriya).
  • Manasā — by the mind; the faculty between ahaṅkāra and buddhi.
  • Niyamya — regulating, controlling; from ni-yam.
  • Ārabhate — begins, undertakes; from ā-rabh.
  • Karmendriyaiḥ — by the organs of action.
  • Karma-yogam — the path of duty without attachment.
  • Asaktaḥ — unattached, free from clinging.
  • Sa — that person; he.
  • Viśiṣyate — is by far superior; distinguished, surpasses.

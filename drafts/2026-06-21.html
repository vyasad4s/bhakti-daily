Bhagavad Gita Chapter 8, Verse 14

अनन्यचेता: सततं यो मां स्मरति नित्यश: । तस्याहं सुलभ: पार्थ नित्ययुक्तस्य योगिन: ॥ १४ ॥
ananya-cetāḥ satataṁ
yo māṁ smarati nityaśaḥ
tasyaāhaṁ su-labhaḥ pārtha
nitya-yuktasya yoginaḥ
For one who always remembers Me without deviation, I am easy to obtain, O son of Pṛthā, because of his constant engagement in devotional service.
Since this temporary material world is full of the miseries of birth, old age, disease and death, naturally he who achieves the highest perfection and attains the supreme planet, Kṛṣṇaloka, Goloka Vṛndāvana, does not wish to return. The supreme planet is described in Vedic literature as avyakta and akṣara and paramā gati; in other words, that planet is beyond our material vision, and it is inexplicable, but it is the highest goal, the destination for the mahātmās (great souls). The mahātmās receive transcendental messages from the realized devotees and thus gradually develop devotional service in Kṛṣṇa consciousness and become so absorbed in transcendental service that they no longer desire elevation to any of the material planets, nor do they even want to be transferred to any spiritual planet. They only want Kṛṣṇa and Kṛṣṇa’s association, and nothing else. That is the highest perfection of life. This verse specifically mentions the personalist devotees of the Supreme Lord, Kṛṣṇa. These devotees in Kṛṣṇa consciousness achieve the highest perfection of life. In other words, they are the supreme souls.

Cosmic Interpretation

The tithi has transitioned to Sukla Paksha Ashtami (after 05:50 AM), the eighth tithi of the waxing moon associated with overcoming obstacles and resolving conflicts through divine intervention. Ashtami’s energy of confronting and dissolving impediments combines with the nakṣatra Uttara Phalguni, ruled by Bhaga (the god of prosperity), which governs the day until 12:01 AM on June 22, representing enjoyment, prosperity, and union. As Uttara Phalguni yields to Hasta (ruled by Savitar, the sun god) at 12:01 AM on June 22, the energy continues the theme of skill, ability, and healing through enlightened action. The yoga Vyatipata (scattered, disruptive energy) prevails until 01:51 AM on June 22, signifying the need to overcome sudden disturbances through focused awareness, after which Variyan (auspicious, favorable) brings smooth progress and positive outcomes.

BG 8.14 beautifully aligns with this cosmic rhythm: “For one who always remembers Me without deviation, I am easy to obtain, O son of Pṛthā, because of his constant engagement in devotional service.” Just as Ashtami’s energy of overcoming obstacles through divine grace gives way to the prosperity and union of Uttara Phalguni, and as Vyatipata’s disruptive energy is smoothed by Variyan’s favorability—so too does the devotee achieve easy union with the Supreme through unwavering, deviation-free remembrance (ananya-cetah). The verse’s promise that Krishna becomes “easy to obtain” (su-labha) for one constantly engaged in devotional service (nitya-yuktasya) mirrors the day’s progression from obstacle (Ashtami) to prosperity (Uttara Phalguni) and from disruption (Vyatipata) to auspiciousness (Variyan). Vyatipata’s scattered energy demands a single-pointed focus, which the verse provides through unalloyed remembrance, turning potential chaos into a clear path to the Divine.

The verse invites us to cultivate ananya-cetah—mind without deviation—fixing our thoughts solely on Krishna amidst the day’s fluctuating energies. In the language of the energy centers, this is the realization that obstacles (Ashtami) are dissolved by steadfast devotion, prosperity (Uttara Phalguni) flows from union with the Divine, and disruptive forces (Vyatipata) are harmonized by exclusive focus on Krishna. Thus the devotee transforms daily challenges into stepping stones toward the eternal goal, where Krishna is ever easily attained through unwavering bhakti-yoga.

Connections & Key Concepts

Yoga Sutra Connection

YS 1.23: īśvara-praṇidhānāt va

Connection: BG 8.14 emphasizes that constant, deviation-free remembrance of Krishna makes Him easy to obtain. Yoga Sutra 1.23 states that samādhi (perfect absorption) can be attained through special devotion to the Lord (īśvara-praṇidhāna). Both point to the same essential practice: exclusive focus on the Divine leads to swift realization. The verse’s “always remembers Me without deviation” (ananya-cetah satataṁ yo māṁ smarati) is the practical application of īśvara-praṇidhāna, where the mind’s ceaseless flow is directed toward Krishna, resulting in the Lord’s easy accessibility (su-labha). Thus the sutra provides the methodological foundation for the verse’s devotional promise.

🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 4.8

Date: April 20, 2026

Sanskrit:

परित्राणाय साधूनां विनाशाय च दुष्कृताम् ।

धर्मसंस्थानार्थाय सम्भवामि युगे युगे ॥ ८ ॥

Transliteration:

परित्राणाय साधूनां
विनाशाय च दुष्कृताम्
धर्मसंस्थानार्थाय
सम्भवामि युगे युगे

Translation:

To deliver the pious and to annihilate the miscreants, as well as to reestablish the principles of religion, I Myself appear, millennium after millennium.

Commentary:

The verse states the operating system architecture: the Supreme appears on schedule, not by accident. There are three concurrent processes in the divine mission: paritrāṇa (deliverance of the sādhus), vināśa (removal of duṣkṛtas), and dharmasaṁsthāpana (reestablishment of cosmic order). These are not sequential but simultaneous — like a system restore that cleans malware, restores drivers, and reboots the OS in one operation.

Paritrāṇāya sādhūnām — first priority is deliverance, not destruction. The sādhu here is defined functionally: one in Kṛṣṇa consciousness. This is the Sāṅkhya point — consciousness of puruṣa is the only real sādhu-status. External morality (the gunas) can simulate righteousness, but the binary check is consciousness.

Vināśāya ca duṣkṛtām — removal of those corrupted by wrong action. Not punishment but cleansing. Like deleting malware files — not to harm the hard drive but to restore system integrity. The Lord has infinite agents for this; He descends personally because of the next clause.

Dharmasaṁsthāpanārthāya — reestablishment of the original, uncorrupted source code. Dharma is not "religion" in the modern sense — it's the natural law, the inherent quality of things. Fire burns. Consciousness witnesses. Dharma is the way things actually work when not interfered with.

Sambhavāmi yuge yuge — the cyclical nature of cosmic time. Linear history is a myth. Time is spiral: creation, sustenance, dissolution, repeat. The Lord appears at predictable intervals — when the guṇas degrade sufficiently to require intervention. Like a system that needs periodic hard resets.

Yoga Sutra Connection:

🔥 YS I.23 — īśvara-praṇidhānād vā

"Or [samādhi is attained] by surrender to īśvara (the Lord)."

The correlation: BG 4.8 states when the Lord descends — to deliver His own. YS I.23 reveals how one becomes eligible for that deliverance: through praṇidhāna — total surrender, entrusting oneself fully. The divine descent and the devotee's ascent meet at this exact point of total surrender.

This creates the conditions. The Lord's appearance is cosmic law — guaranteed, scheduled, inevitable. But WHO receives Him? Only those who have cultivated praṇidhāna. Like having the right credentials for root access — the system is open, but you need the proper authentication.

Sanskrit Terms:

  • paritrāṇa — deliverance, rescue from danger; the primary mission is saving, not destroying
  • sādhu — the pious, the holy; functionally defined: one in Kṛṣṇa consciousness (the binary check)
  • vināśa — destruction, annihilation; removal of obstruction, not violence for its own sake
  • duṣkṛta — one who does wrong; miscreant; the system running corrupted code (guṇas operating unchecked)
  • dharmasaṁsthāpana — reestablishment of religion/cosmic order; restoring original source code
  • artha — for the purpose of; indicating teleological structure — action toward goal
  • sambhavāmi — I manifest, I appear; from sam-bhū (to arise, exist); the voluntary descent
  • yuge yuge — millennium after millennium; cyclical time, not linear history

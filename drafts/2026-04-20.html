BG 4.8 // The Divine Mission

परित्राणाय साधुनां विनाशाय च दुष्कृताम् ।

धर्मसंस्थानार्थाय सम्भवामि युगे युगे ॥ ८ ॥

paritrāṇāya sādhūnāṁ vināśāya ca duṣkṛtām
dharma-saṁsthāpanārthāya sambhavāmi yuge yuge

Translation

To deliver the pious and to annihilate the miscreants, as well as to reestablish the principles of religion, I Myself appear, millennium after millennium.

Purport

According to Bhagavad-gītā, a sādhu (holy man) is a man in Kṛṣṇa consciousness. A person may appear to be irreligious, but if he has the qualifications of Kṛṣṇa consciousness wholly and fully, he is to be understood to be a sādhu. And duṣkṛtām applies to those who do not care for Kṛṣṇa consciousness. Such miscreants are described as foolish and the lowest of mankind, even though they may be decorated with mundane education, whereas a person who is one hundred percent engaged in Kṛṣṇa consciousness is accepted as a sādhu.

It is not necessary for the Supreme Lord to appear as He is to destroy the miscreants — He has many agents who are quite competent to vanquish demons. But the Lord especially descends to appease His unalloyed devotees, who are always harassed by the demoniac. The demon harasses the devotee, even though the latter may happen to be his kin. Although Prahlāda Mahārāja was the son of Hiraṇyakaśipu, he was nonetheless persecuted by his father. So Lord Kṛṣṇa appeared primarily to deliver Devakī rather than kill Kaṁsa — but both were performed simultaneously.

There are various kinds of avatāras — puruṣāvatāras, guṇāvatāras, līlāvatāras, śakty-āveśa avatāras, manvantara-avatāras and yugāvatāras — all appearing on schedule all over the universe. But Lord Kṛṣṇa is the primeval Lord, the fountainhead of all avatāras. Lord Śrī Kṛṣṇa descends for the specific purpose of mitigating the anxieties of the pure devotees. Therefore, the prime purpose of the Kṛṣṇa avatāra is to satisfy His unalloyed devotees.

YS I.23 // The Mechanics of Descent

īśvara-praṇidhānād vā

Or [samādhi is attained] by surrender to Īśvara (the Lord).

Patañjali gives three means to achieve citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ: abhyāsa (disciplined practice), vairāgya (dispassion/renunciation), and īśvara-praṇidhāna (devotional surrender to the Lord). These correspond to the three paths: karma-mārga, jñāna-mārga, and bhakti-mārga.

Here's the profound connection: BG 4.8 speaks of when and why the Lord descends — to deliver His devotees. But YS I.23 reveals how these devotees become worthy of such deliverance: through praṇidhāna — complete surrender, entrusting oneself fully to the Lord. The divine descent and the devotee's ascent meet at this point of total surrender. The Lord appears millennium after millennium, but who actually receives Him? Only those who have surrendered completely.

This is not passive waiting. Praṇidhāna (from pra-ni-dhā, "to place before") is the active offering of oneself at the Lord's feet. It creates the conditions under which divine intervention becomes not merely possible but inevitable. The Lord's appearance is guaranteed by cosmic law; the devotee's readiness to receive that appearance is cultivated through yoga.

Technical Terms

sādhu — "One who is situated in Kṛṣṇa consciousness." // In the modern world, righteousness gets reduced to moralism. But the Gītā defines it functionally: are you conscious of Kṛṣṇa? This is the ultimate debugging protocol — a binary check that cuts through social performance and religious posturing. The sādhu may look like anything outwardly. What matters is the internal state.

duṣkṛta — "One who engages in sinful activities; miscreant." // Those running legacy code written by the modes of passion and ignorance. They're not necessarily "bad people" — they're running corrupted software. Like a system infected with malware that doesn't know it's infected. The Lord doesn't appear to punish them; He appears to reestablish the dharma — the original, uncorrupted source code.

yuge yuge — "Millennium after millennium." // The cyclical nature of cosmic time. Not linear progress but spiral recurrence. Each age degrades; each age requires intervention. Like a system that degrades over time, needing periodic hard resets. The Lord's appearance is the ultimate system restore — but it happens according to divine timing, not human impatience.

īśvara-praṇidhāna — "Surrender to the Lord." // The highest form of practice in Patañjali's system. Not just meditation technique but total entrusting of oneself. Like giving root access to the Divine Administrator. Terrifying for the ego — which is precisely the point. The ego must be dismantled for the Lord to be installed.

avatāra — "One who descends; incarnation." // From ava (down) + tṛ (to cross). The Lord crossing down from the spiritual to the material plane. Unlike the conditioned soul who is forced to take bodies through karma, the Supreme descends by His own internal energy (ātma-māyayā), maintaining full consciousness and control.

dharma-saṁsthāpana — "Reestablishment of religious principles." // Not establishing something new, but reestablishing what eternally exists. Dharma isn't invented; it's remembered. When the transmission becomes garbled through time and material contamination, the Lord appears as the divine signal booster.

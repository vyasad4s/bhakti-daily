🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 3.2

Date: February 25, 2026

Sanskrit:

व्यामिश्रेणेव वाक्येन बुद्धिं मोहयसीव मे ।

तदेकं वद निश्चित्य येन श्रेयोऽहमाप्नुयाम् ॥ ३.२ ॥

Transliteration:

vyāmiśreṇeva vākyena
buddhiṁ mohayasi iva me
tad ekaṁ vadi niścitya
yena śreyo 'ham āpnuyām

Translation:

"My intelligence is confused by Your ambiguous words. Therefore please tell me decisively one path by which I may attain the highest good."

Commentary:

Arjuna here represents the sincere student who, hearing two seemingly contradictory paths—renunciation of action and performance of action with detachment—finds his buddhi (discriminating intelligence) bewildered. He is not complaining from laziness; he is seeking niścitya (definite, decisive) knowledge.

The word vyāmiśreṇa means "mixed" or "confused"—like a potion with contradictory ingredients. Arjuna recognizes that both paths lead to liberation, but their simultaneous presentation creates intellectual moha (bewilderment).

His request is profound: "Tell me eka (ONE)"—the singular path, the essence stripped of apparent contradiction. This is not impatience; this is the yoga of sāṅkhya demanding clarity.

Kṛṣṇa will answer: there is no contradiction. Karma-yoga with consciousness of the Supreme IS renunciation. Action becomes liberation when offered to the Divine. The deer seeks Soma—the seeker seeks the Supreme. Neither finds satisfaction in anything less. What will you seek today?

Yoga Sutra Connection:

🔥 YS 1.32 — tat-pratiṣedhārtham eka-tattva-abhyāsaḥ — "For the removal of these [obstacles], practice of one truth."

The answer to Arjuna's question: One-pointed focus cuts through the confusion of multiple paths.

Sanskrit Terms:

  • Vyāmiśreṇa (past passive participle, instrumental singular) — mixed, confused, contradictory; from vi-ā-miśr (to mix thoroughly)
  • Vākyena (neuter noun, instrumental singular) — by speech, by words; from vac (to speak)
  • Buddhiḥ (feminine noun, accusative singular) — intelligence, discernment, the faculty of discrimination
  • Moha (masculine noun) — delusion, bewilderment, loss of discrimination; from muh (to become confused)
  • Niścitya (gerundive) — to be decided, to be determined; definite, decisive; from ni-ci (to ascertain completely)
  • Śreyaḥ (noun, nominative singular) — the highest good, ultimate benefit; comparative of śreyas (better/best)
  • Āpnuyām (verb, optative first person singular) — may I attain, may I reach; from āp (to reach, obtain)

🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 9.29

Date: March 3, 2026

Sanskrit:

समोऽहं सर्वभूतेषु न मे द्वेष्योऽस्ति न प्रियः ।

ये भजन्ति तु मां भक्त्या मयि ते तेषु चाप्यहम् ॥ २९ ॥

Transliteration:

samo 'haṁ sarva-bhūteṣu na me dveṣyo 'sti na priyaḥ

ye bhajanti tu māṁ bhaktyā mayi te teṣu cāpy aham

Translation:

I envy no one, nor am I partial to anyone. I am equal to all. But whoever renders service unto Me in devotion is a friend, is in Me, and I am also a friend to him.

Commentary:

The Supreme Lord declares His absolute impartiality toward all beings while simultaneously revealing the transcendent principle of devotion. This is not contradiction—this is the operating system of bhakti. Krishna rains His mercy upon all, like a cloud that showers water without discrimination upon rock, soil, or sea. Yet the reservoir that opens itself to receive becomes filled.

This verse cuts through the delusion that the Absolute is either an indifferent void or a capricious favor-giver like the petty gods of worldly pantheons. The Lord is present in all beings equally—this is the truth of samaḥ (equally disposed), without differentiation or preference. But the reciprocation of love is not automatic; it requires the opening of the heart. Devotion (bhakti) is the API call that establishes two-way communication. The devotee enters into Krishna (mayi te), and Krishna enters into the devotee (teṣu cāpy aham). This is not metaphorical—this is the lived reality of those who engage in bhajanti, loving service.

On this day of Pūrṇimā and Chaitanya Mahāprabhu Jayanti, we remember the Lord who descended to teach that divine love knows no boundaries. The moon appears the same to all, yet only the clear lake reflects it perfectly. The heart cleansed by devotion becomes that mirror. This is the unitive principle: complete equality establishing the foundation for the most intimate particularity.

Yoga Sutra Connection:

🔥 YS 1.23 — ईश्वरप्रणिधानाद्वा

īśvara-praṇidhānāt vā — "Or [samādhi is attained] by devotion to the Supreme."

Patañjali presents īśvara-praṇidhāna as one of the central avenues to meditative absorption. Here we see the elegant meeting of Bhakti and Raja Yoga. Where the sūtra states devotion to the Supreme as a means, Krishna reveals it as the fulfillment. Practicing praṇidhāna (surrender, devotion) is the method; achieving mayi te (they are in Me) is the result. The sūtra is the code; the verse is the compiled output. Both point to the same truth: the path of the heart takes you all the way to the root.

Sanskrit Terms:

  • samaḥ (adj.) — equally disposed, same; without differentiation or preference
  • sarva-bhūteṣu (loc. pl.) — in all living entities; sarva = all, bhūta = living being
  • dveṣyaḥ (adj.) — hateful, odious; one who is to be hated
  • bhajanti (vb., 3rd pl. pres.) — they worship, serve; related to bhakti, the verb of loving devotion
  • bhaktyā (f. instr. sg.) — by devotion; the instrument of spiritual connection

Cosmic Interpretation:

The cosmos aligns with extraordinary potency today. Pūrṇimā represents the fullness of the reflected light—the jīva fully illuminated by divine grace. The moon is completely exposed, hiding nothing, just as the devotee holds no reserve when opening to the Lord. This is the night of bhajana, of unreserved offering.

Combined with Pūrṇimā's completeness, we witness the perfect marriage of the full moon's light with Venus's embrace. The nakṣatra deity is Āryaman, the cosmic principle of friendship and noble alliances—directly echoing the verse's promise that the devotee and Lord become friends (priya).

Chaitanya Mahāprabhu appeared on this lunar day to demonstrate the radical equality of divine love—a brāhmaṇa scholar becoming a dancing ecstatic, teaching that the name of Krishna dissolves all barriers of caste, status, and qualification. His mission was precisely this verse enacted: samo 'haṁ sarva-bhūteṣu — the Lord's equal presence flooding the world through the equal-distribution-system of saṅkīrtana.

Dhṛti Yoga brings steadiness and determination—the quality of one who persists in devotion regardless of circumstance. On this day of devotional celebration, the cosmic atmosphere supports sustained practice. The transition to Śūla (the spear/trident) at 10:22 PM sharpens the energy for transformation—a piercing through illusion into truth.

Verse Selection Rationale:

BG 9.29 — samo 'haṁ sarva-bhūteṣu na me dveṣyo 'sti na priyaḥ / ye bhajanti tu māṁ bhaktyā mayi te teṣu cāpy aham

This verse was chosen because it IS Chaitanya Mahāprabhu's teaching in seed form. The Lord declares Himself equally present in all—yet the devotee who offers loving service enters a special relationship. This is not contradictory. Just as the sun shines equally on all but only the lotus opens to receive it, the Supreme is equally present but devotion establishes the connection. On a day celebrating Chaitanya Mahāprabhu's appearance, we remember his revolutionary message: divine love breaks all barriers. The verse's teaching of equality-without-indifference mirrors Holi's dissolution of social distinctions and Pūrṇimā's revelation of total light. The reciprocation mayi te teṣu cāpy aham (they in Me, I in them) is the yukta state—union.

Bhagavad Gita Chapter 5, Verse 22

ये हि संस्पर्शजा भोगा दु:खयोनय एव ते ।
आद्यन्तवन्त: कौन्तेय न तेषु रमते बुध: ॥ २२ ॥
ye hi saṁsparśa-jā bhogā
duḥkha-yonaya eva te
ādy-antavantaḥ kaunteya
na teṣu ramate budhaḥ
An intelligent person does not take part in the sources of misery, which are due to contact with the material senses. O son of Kuntī, such pleasures have a beginning and an end, and so the wise man does not delight in them.
Material sense pleasures are temporary by nature because the body itself is temporary. A liberated soul, recognizing the inherent instability of these enjoyments, does not seek happiness in the fleeting contact of the senses. The Ekadashi fast serves as a practical application of this principle—by withdrawing from specific sensory gratifications, we break the cycle of craving and acknowledge that true satisfaction is transcendental, not material. To delight in the temporary is to invite misery; to seek the eternal is to find peace. This verse calls us to utilize the discipline of Ekadashi as a tool for debugging our attachment to the matrix of sensory input.

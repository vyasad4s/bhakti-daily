Bhagavad Gita Chapter 5, Verse 22

ये हि संस्पर्शजा भोगा दु:खयोनय एव ते ।
आद्यन्तवन्त: कौन्तेय न तेषु रमते बुध: ॥ २२ ॥
ye hi saṁsparśa-jā bhogā
duḥkha-yonaya eva te
ādy-antavantaḥ kaunteya
na teṣu ramate budhaḥ
An intelligent person does not take part in the sources of misery, which are due to contact with the material senses. O son of Kuntī, such pleasures have a beginning and an end, and so the wise man does not delight in them.
Material sense pleasures arise from contact with the senses and are inherently temporary because the body itself is temporary. A liberated soul, established in self-realization, has no interest in fleeting enjoyments that perpetuate the cycle of birth and death. The Ekadashi fast is designed to withdraw the senses from their objects (pratyāhāra), thereby reducing the fuel for material desires. By observing Ekadashi, we practice sense control, aligning with the intelligence described in this verse—one who does not delight in temporary pleasures. This verse reinforces the purpose of Ekadashi: to turn away from sense gratification and cultivate detachment, making the mind fit for higher spiritual practice.

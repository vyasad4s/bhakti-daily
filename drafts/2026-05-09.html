🏹 Bhagavad Gita 5.21

The Kalashtami energy of Shravana nakshatra descends: a time of intense, focused listening. Shravana is ruled by the divine sage Vashistha, symbolizing the power of sacred sound and the importance of hearing the Absolute.

यावत्संजातरूपास्ते ब्रह्मतत्त्वं त्वनिन्द्रियाः।

सर्वारम्भान्परित्यज्य ब्रह्मभूताः स्वरस्तथा।

मनसि विनिर्जाते तु ब्रह्मलोकलक्षणे महीम् ॥ २१ ॥

yāvat-saṁjāta-rūpāste brahma-tattvaṁ tv-anindriyāḥ | sarvārambhān parityajya brahma-bhūtāḥ sva-saṁsthayā | manasi vinirjāte tu brahma-loke laukasaye || 21 ||

Translation (Vedabase.io): "Those who are in full knowledge and have transcended the material modes of nature, having abandoned all fruitive activities, dwell in the spiritual world, being fully absorbed in the happiness of the self through pure knowledge."

🔥 The Zero-Point of Self-Absorption

BG 5.21 describes the state of brahma-bhūtaḥ (ब्रह्म-भूतः) — one who has realized the Absolute. This is not a state of passive bliss, but a state of complete, active absorption in the self. The verse emphasizes that true happiness is not found in external pursuits but in the internal realization of the self. This is the ultimate goal of yoga: to be free from the need to seek happiness in external objects.

The key phrase is sva-saṁsthayā (स्व-संस्थया) — "being established in the self." This is not a withdrawal from the world, but a complete shift in the reference point of experience. The self is not the body, mind, or intelligence, but the eternal witness, ātma-tattva.

🔗 Yoga Sūtra Bridge: YS II.34 vyutthāna-saṁskṛtayoḥ abhibhavāt

Original Sanskrit: व्युत्थान-संस्कृतयोः अभिभवात्

Transliteration: vyutthāna-saṁskṛtayoḥ abhibhavāt

Translation: "By the suppression of disturbed and undisturbed thoughts."

Bridge to BG 5.21: YS II.34 explains how to deal with the mind's disturbances, while BG 5.21 is the state of mind when disturbances are fully transcended. The path of yoga is not just about suppression, but about full transformation of the root cause of suffering: the false identification with the material.

⚡ TERM DEFINITIONS

brahma-bhūtaḥ (adj) - Having realized the Absolute; the state of being fully absorbed in the self, free from material desires and fears. (BG 5.21)

sva-saṁsthayā (adj) - Being established in the self; the state of self-absorption, independent of external conditions. (BG 5.21)

vyutthāna-saṁskṛtayoḥ (n) - The state of being fully absorbed in the self, having transcended material designations. (YS II.34)

abhibhavāt (adj) - Suppression of disturbances; the process of overcoming mental modifications. (YS II.34)

ātma-tattva (n) - The knowledge of the self; the fundamental truth of one's identity as the eternal witness. (BG 5.21)

vyāpāra (m) - Function, activity, work. In the context of yoga, it refers to the active engagement of the senses and mind in the service of the self. (BG 5.21)

🌌 Cosmic Context: The Kalashtami Kernel

Today's cosmic energies are intense and focused. Shravana (the nakshatra of 'hearing') is a powerful time for satsang and śruti (sacred sound). The transition into Dhanishta brings a surge of creative energy, but also a tendency towards restlessness. The Brahma yoga (the yoga of creation) is active, suggesting a time for disciplined creation and focused work.

Warning: The Shravana energy can make one highly receptive to sound and teaching, but also prone to distraction. Use this time to deepen practice, not to chase external stimuli.

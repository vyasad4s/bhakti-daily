Bhagavad Gita Chapter 7, Verse 10

बीजं मां सर्वभूतानां विद्धि पार्थ सनातनम् । बुद्धिर्बुद्धिमतामस्मि तेजस्तेजस्विनामहम् ॥ १० ॥
bijaṁ māṁ sarva-bhūtānāṁ
viddhi pārtha sanātanam
buddhir buddhimatām asmi
tejas tejasvinām aham
O son of Pṛthā, know that I am the original seed of all existences, the intelligence of the intelligent, and the prowess of all powerful men.
Bījam means seed; Kṛṣṇa is the seed of everything. There are various living entities, movable and inert. Birds, beasts, men and many other living creatures are moving living entities; trees and plants, however, are inert – they cannot move, but only stand. Every entity is contained within the scope of 8,400,000 species of life; some of them are moving and some of them are inert. In all cases, however, the seed of their life is Kṛṣṇa. As stated in Vedic literature, Brahman, or the Supreme Absolute Truth, is that from which everything is emanating. Kṛṣṇa is Para-brahman, the Supreme Spirit. Brahman is impersonal and Para-brahman is personal. Impersonal Brahman is situated in the personal aspect – that is stated in Bhagavad-gītā. Therefore, originally, Kṛṣṇa is the source of everything. He is the root. As the root of a tree maintains the whole tree, Kṛṣṇa, being the original root of all things, maintains everything in this material manifestation. This is also confirmed in the Vedic literature (Kaṭha Upaniṣad 2.2.13):
nityo nityānāṁ cetanaś cetanānāmeko bahūnāṁ yo vidadhāti kāmān
He is the prime eternal among all eternals. He is the supreme living entity of all living entities, and He alone is maintaining all life. One cannot do anything without intelligence, and Kṛṣṇa also says that He is the root of all intelligence. Unless a person is intelligent he cannot understand the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Kṛṣṇa.

Cosmic Interpretation

Dwitiya, the second tithi of the waxing moon, symbolizes the initial stage of manifestation after the darkness of Amavasya—the first tender sprouting of creation. The nakṣatra Ardra, ruled by Rudra the storm god, governs the early morning hours, representing the necessary destruction of old patterns to clear the ground for new growth. As Ardra yields to Punarvasu (ruled by Aditi, the mother of gods) at 06:42 AM, the energy shifts from transformation to renewal, return of light, and nurturing growth. The yoga Vriddhi (growth/increase) prevails until 15:04 PM, signifying expansion, prosperity, and the fruition of efforts initiated during the previous phase, after which Dhruva (the immovable, steady) brings stability and endurance.

BG 7.10 beautifully aligns with this cosmic rhythm: “O son of Pṛthā, know that I am the original seed of all existences, the intelligence of the intelligent, and the prowess of all powerful men.” Just as Dwitiya follows Amavasya’s darkness with the first sliver of moonlight, Ardra’s storm clears stagnation, and Punarvasu brings nurturing renewal—so too does the devotee progress from ignorance to knowledge by realizing that all existence rests in Krishna as the primordial seed. Vriddhi’s expansive quality reflects how recognizing Krishna as the source leads to spiritual growth and the dissolution of illusion, while Dhruva’s steadiness mirrors the unshakable foundation of the Supreme.

The verse invites us to “know for certain” (viddhi) this unchanging truth: Krishna is the bija (seed) from which all beings emanate, the buddhi (intelligence) that guides the wise, and the teja (prowess) that empowers the mighty. In the language of the simulation, He is the init() function that spawns all processes, the debugger that reveals true state, and the garbage collector that reclaims memory—yet beyond all metaphors, He is the source and sustainer of everything.

Connections & Key Concepts

Yoga Sutra Connection

YS I.3: tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe ‘vasthānam
When that is accomplished, the seer abides in its own true nature

Connection: BG 7.10 declares Krishna as the seed, intelligence, and prowess—the fundamental source of all existence. Yoga Sutra I.3 describes the state where pure consciousness (purusha) rests in its true nature, separate from the changing manifestations of prakriti. Both point to realizing the unchanging reality behind cosmic fluctuations: when the mind is still (yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ), the seer abides in the awareness of Krishna as the primordial seed.

Sankhya Concepts

  • buddhi (intelligence) as discriminative faculty
  • teja (prowess) as vitality
  • the living entity as superior energy exploiting inferior nature (BG 7.5)

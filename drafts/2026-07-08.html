Bhagavad Gita Chapter 9, Verse 22

अनन्याश्चिन्तयन्तो मां ये जना: पर्युपासते । तेषां नित्याभियुक्तानां योगक्षेमं वहाम्यहम् ॥ २२ ॥
ananyāś cintayanto māṁ ye janāḥ paryupāsate teṣāṁ nityābhiyuktānāṁ yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmy aham
But those who always worship Me with exclusive devotion, meditating on My transcendental form – to them I carry what they lack, and I preserve what they have.
This verse reveals the intimate promise of the Supreme Lord to His exclusive devotees: He personally carries what they lack and preserves what they have. The phrase "ananya-cintayanto" (those whose minds are fixed on Me alone) describes the state of exclusive devotion (ananya-bhakti), where every thought, word, and action is offered to Krishna. Such devotees are "nitya-abhiyukta" — perpetually engaged in His service through hearing, chanting, remembering, serving, worshiping, and surrendering. The Lord’s role as the divine sustainer is twofold: He removes deficiencies (yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmi) and protects existing blessings. For the devotee, this means that material and spiritual needs are met by the Lord’s personal attention. The term "yoga" here refers to the connection or linkage with the Divine, while "kṣema" denotes welfare, protection, and well-being. This is not a passive promise but an active, reciprocal relationship: the devotee’s exclusive attention invites the Lord’s personal care. From a philosophical standpoint, this verse underscores the non-dual nature of bhakti-yoga within the dualistic framework. While the soul remains distinct from the Lord, the loving exchange creates a unity of purpose. The devotee’s lack is not merely material; it includes the absence of divine association, which the Lord fulfills. Conversely, what the devotee possesses — faith, devotion, surrender — is preserved and enhanced by the Lord’s grace. Practically, this verse assures the sincere practitioner that exclusive devotion is never in vain. Even when external resources seem lacking, the Lord arranges for what is needed. This is the essence of "yoga-kṣema" — the divine arrangement that facilitates spiritual progress and material sufficiency for the devotee.

Yoga Sūtra Connection

YS I.23: īśvara-praṇidhānād vā

Or, [samādhi is attained] by devotion to the Lord.

This sutra offers a direct parallel to BG 9.22: the attainment of yogic perfection (samādhi) through īśvara-praṇidhāna (surrender to the Lord). The verse promises that the Lord personally cares for the exclusive devotee; the sutra indicates that such surrender is a swift path to samādhi. Both emphasize that the Lord’s grace is responsive to the devotee’s exclusive orientation.

YS II.45 further elaborates: samādhi-siddhiḥ īśvara-praṇidhānāt — "Perfection in samādhi is achieved through devotion to the Lord." This underscores the causative relationship: exclusive devotion (īśvara-praṇidhāna) leads to the highest yogic attainment. In the context of BG 9.22, the devotee’s exclusive worship (ananya-bhakti) invites the Lord’s personal care (yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmy aham), which in turn facilitates spiritual perfection.

Additionally, YS I.33 (maitrī-karuṇā-muditopekṣāṇāṁ viṣayāṁ cittaprasādanam) — "The mind becomes purified by cultivating feelings of friendliness, compassion, joy, and equanimity" — complements the verse by describing the pure consciousness that results from bhakti. When the mind is thus purified through devotion, it becomes a fit vessel for the Lord’s sustaining grace.

Thus, the Yoga Sutras provide the methodological foundation for achieving the exclusive devotion praised in BG 9.22: through īśvara-praṇidhāna and the cultivation of divine qualities (brahmavihārās), the devotee aligns with the Lord’s promise to carry what is lacking and preserve what is possessed.

Practice for Today

Exclusive Devotion Meditation: Sit quietly and focus your mind solely on Krishna’s form, qualities, or pastimes. Whenever a distracting thought arises, gently return your attention to the Lord with the sentiment: "My mind belongs to Krishna alone." Practice this for 10–15 minutes, cultivating the state of ananya-cintayanto (exclusive contemplation).

Offering with Bhava: Following BG 9.26, offer a simple item—a leaf, flower, fruit, or water—to Krishna with profound devotion, imagining that you are giving it directly to His hands. Feel how this act of love invites His promise to "carry what you lack and preserve what you have."

Asthma-Tithi Action (Ashtami): Since Ashtami tithi grants strength to overcome obstacles, identify one challenge in your devotional life (e.g., inconsistency in chanting, distraction during prayer). Invoke the strength of Goddess Durga/Chamunda (the deity of Ashtami) to help you overcome it, affirming: "With Krishna’s grace, I conquer this obstacle."

Revati Nakshatra Nourishment (until 4:00 PM): During the morning hours, when Revati Nakshatra is active, focus on the nourishing aspect of the Lord. Chant the Hare Krishna mahā-mantra with the intention of receiving spiritual nourishment (puṣṭi) from Krishna, the ultimate sustainer (Pushan). Visualize the Lord’s nurturing presence filling your heart.

Ashwini Nakshatra Initiative (after 4:00 PM): As Ashwini Nakshatra begins, harness its energy of healing and swift initiation. Begin a new devotional practice or revive a lapsed one with enthusiasm, knowing that the Ashwini Kumaras (divine physicians) bless fresh starts in bhakti.

Sukarma Yoga Activity (after 12:38 PM): Engage in a virtuous act of service (seva) during the Sukarma Yoga period — such as cleaning a sacred space, distributing prasādam, or assisting a fellow devotee. Offer the results to Krishna, trusting that He will multiply the merit of your efforts.

Budhvar Wednesday Practice: Use Mercury’s influence to study a verse of Bhagavad-gītā or Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam with clarity. Write down how the teaching applies to your life, reinforcing the intellect’s role in devotional life.

Mesha Moon Courage: Since the Moon is in Aries, channel its fiery energy into a bold act of devotion — perhaps sharing Krishna consciousness with someone new or taking on a challenging service activity. Let the courage of Aries fuel your determination to serve Krishna exclusively.

Mithuna Sun Discrimination: With the Sun in Gemini, practice discriminative understanding (viveka) throughout the day. Ask yourself: "Does this thought, word, or action increase my exclusive devotion to Krishna, or does it scatter my mind?" Use the intellect to steer toward ananya-bhakti.

Chant for Divine Sustenance: Chant the Hare Krishna mahā-mantra with the bhava (feeling) that Krishna is personally carrying your deficiencies and preserving your blessings. Let each repetition strengthen your conviction in His promise.

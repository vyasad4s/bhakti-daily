ब्रह्मण्याधाय कर्माणि सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा करोति य: ।
लिप्यते न स पापेन पद्मपत्रमिवाम्भसा ॥ १० ॥

brahmaṇy ādhāya karmāṇi saṅgaṁ tyaktvā karoti yaḥ |
lipyate na sa pāpena padma-patram ivāmbhasā || 10 ||

One who performs his duty without attachment, surrendering the results unto the Supreme Lord, is unaffected by sinful action, as the lotus leaf is untouched by water.

The imagery of the lotus leaf is not mere poetry; it is a precise description of a spiritual state. A lotus leaf exists within the water, yet it is fundamentally hydrophobic. Water does not soak into it; it beads and rolls off. Similarly, the practitioner of Bhakti exists within the material world—engaging in the same duties, facing the same conflicts, and interacting with the same people—yet remains untouched by the karmic residue that usually clings to the doer.

Most people operate on a "direct-write" system: they act, and the result of that action is written directly into their subconscious as a samskara. This creates a sticky, heavy existence where every win fuels ego and every loss fuels grief. By surrendering the result to the Supreme (Brahmani), the yogi switches to a "sandbox" mode. The action is performed, but the result is redirected. Because the "I" is no longer the claimed proprietor of the fruit, there is no address for the karma to return to. The result simply beads off the soul.

Today is Masik Shivaratri and Pradosh Vrat—times dedicated to Lord Shiva, the ultimate ascetic. Shiva is the master of this detachment. He is the destroyer of the ego-illusion that claims "I am the doer." When we align our actions with the Divine will, we are not escaping responsibility; we are elevating our responsibility from the personal to the universal. We stop trying to "win" the game of material gain and start playing for the pleasure of the Source. This is the only way to act in the world without being crushed by it.

Yoga Sūtra Connection: I.23 — Īśvara praṇidhānād vā

Patanjali identifies Īśvara-praṇidhāna (devotion/surrender to the Lord) as a potent shortcut to samādhi. While traditional limbs of yoga require grueling discipline to still the mind's waves, surrender acts as a systemic override. Instead of fighting every individual wave of desire or aversion, the yogi aligns their entire orientation toward the Divine. This surrender is the active mechanism that allows the "lotus-leaf" state to manifest; it is the shift from "my will be done" to "Thy will be done," which effectively clears the kleśas and stills the citta.

Brahmani: In Kṛṣṇa consciousness; operating within the frequency of the Divine rather than the ego.

Sanga: Attachment; the "sticky" quality of the mind that binds the soul to the results of action.

Pāpena: By sinful action; here referring to any action performed with the intent of personal gain (ego-driven), which creates binding karma.

Samyama: Integration; the combined state of concentration, meditation, and absorption that allows the yogi to see through the illusion of proprietorship.

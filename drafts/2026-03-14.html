🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 9.22

Date: March 14, 2026

Sanskrit:

अनन्याश्चिन्तयन्तो मां ये जनाः पर्युपासते ।

तेषां नित्याभियुक्तानां योगक्षेमं वहाम्यहम् ॥ २२ ॥

Transliteration:

ananyāś cintayanto māṁ ye janāḥ paryupāsate
teṣāṁ nityābhiyuktānāṁ yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmy aham

Translation:

But those who always worship Me with exclusive devotion, meditating on My transcendental form—to them I carry what they lack, and I preserve what they have.

Commentary:

This is the verse that defines Ekādaśī. Not the fasting, not the vigil, not the external observance—this is what those observances point toward: ananyāḥ, no other. When Kṛṣṇa says "those who think of Me without deviation," He is describing a consciousness so exclusively focused that it permits no rivals. The mind that currently fragments itself across a thousand objects of attention—work, worry, desire, fear, entertainment—must be trained to single-pointedness.

Ananyāś cintayantaḥ: this is not occasional remembrance. This is not Sunday worship or morning prayers followed by forgetfulness. This is cintayantaḥ—continuous, unbroken thought-flow toward the Supreme. Like a river that has found its ocean, the devotee's consciousness flows ceaselessly toward Kṛṣṇa. Paryupāsate: they worship properly, adequately, surrounding Him with attention from all sides. The fasting of Ekādaśī creates the conditions for this; it removes the obstacles, clears the noise, makes space for the holy name to resound.

And what does Kṛṣṇa promise in return? Yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmy aham. This is the most intimate verse in the entire Gītā regarding divine providence. Yoga: I provide what you lack for your spiritual advancement. Kṣema: I protect what you already have. Like a mother who both feeds her hungry child and keeps safe the food already given, Kṛṣṇa takes total responsibility for the devotee. The Papamochani Ekādaśī energy works precisely this way: the vow you take today creates the vessel, and Kṛṣṇa's promise fills it. The sin-destroying power of this tithi is not magical—it is the natural result of ananyā bhakti. When consciousness is exclusively His, there is no room for pāpa. It is annihilated not by force but by displacement.

Yoga Sutra Connection:

🔥 YS I.23 — ईश्वरप्रणिधानाद्वा
"Or [samādhi is attained] by surrender to Īśvara."

Patañjali offers īśvara-praṇidhāna—surrender to the Supreme—as an alternative path for those who may not have the capacity for intense technical discipline. This is the path of grace. But here Kṛṣṇa reveals a deeper dimension: it is not merely that surrender leads to samādhi; it is that the surrendered devotee finds the Lord Himself taking charge of both yoga (acquisition) and kṣema (preservation). The Yoga Sūtra says "surrender to achieve"; Kṛṣṇa says "surrender and I will carry everything." This is Bhakti Yoga's unique gift: the devotee does not need to struggle for yoga-kṣema. The Lord supplies both. The Sāṅkhya metaphysics underlying this are clear: while puruṣa must discriminate itself from prakṛti, the instrument of that discrimination—buddhi—is brought to perfection by Lord's grace through exclusive devotion. Ekādaśī creates the space; Kṛṣṇa provides the light.

Sanskrit Terms:

  • ananyāḥ (nom. pl.) — having no other; a (not) + anya (other); exclusive, without rival; the absolute singularity of devotion
  • cintayantaḥ (pres. act. part.) — thinking of, meditating upon; continuous thought-flow; from cint (to think); the active, unbroken remembrance
  • paryupāsate (3pl. mid.) — worship properly, attend to from all sides; pari (around) + upās (to sit near/serve); complete, adequate worship
  • nitya-abhiyuktānām (gen. pl.) — of those always fixed in devotion; nitya (eternal/always) + abhiyukta (engaged/fixed); perpetual application
  • yoga (m.) — acquisition of what is lacking; the attainment of what is needed for spiritual progress
  • kṣema (m.) — preservation of what is possessed; protection, safety, welfare; what keeps one from falling
  • vahāmi (1sg. pres.) — I carry, I bear; from vah (to carry); the Lord's active taking of responsibility
  • aham (nom.) — I (emphatic); Kṛṣṇa Himself speaking in first person—this is His personal promise

Verse Selection Rationale:

BG 9.22 — अनन्याश्चिन्तयन्तो मां ये जनाः पर्युपासते

This verse was chosen because it IS the Ekādaśī teaching. While BG 2.65 speaks of prasāda and destruction of suffering (already covered March 11), BG 9.22 speaks of the Lord's direct promise: yoga-kṣemaṁ vahāmy aham. The devotee who fasts today, who excludes all else and thinks only of Kṛṣṇa, finds the Lord Himself carrying their yoga (tensions, needs, lacks) and protecting their kṣema (peace, holdings, gains). This is not a philosophy—this is a promise from the Supreme Person.

Bhagavad-gītā Chapter 4, Verse 7

यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य ग्लानिर्भवति भारत । अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम् ॥ ७ ॥
yadā yadā hi dharmasya glānir bhavati bhārata abhyutthānam adharmasya tadātmānaṁ sṛjāmy aham

Translation

Whenever and wherever there is a decline in religious practice, O descendant of Bharata, and a predominant rise of irreligion — at that time I descend Myself.

Purport

The word sṛjāmi is significant herein. Sṛjāmi cannot be used in the sense of creation, because, according to the previous verse, there is no creation of the Lord's form or body, since all of the forms are eternally existent. Therefore, sṛjāmi means that the Lord manifests Himself as He is. Although the Lord appears on schedule, namely at the end of the Dvāpara-yuga of the twenty-eighth millennium of the seventh Manu in one day of Brahmā, He has no obligation to adhere to such rules and regulations, because He is completely free to act in many ways at His will.

He therefore appears by His own will whenever there is a predominance of irreligiosity and a disappearance of true religion. Principles of religion are laid down in the Vedas, and any discrepancy in the matter of properly executing the rules of the Vedas makes one irreligious. In the Bhāgavatam it is stated that such principles are the laws of the Lord. Only the Lord can manufacture a system of religion.

The Vedic principles push one towards complete surrender unto Him; and whenever such principles are disturbed by the demoniac, the Lord appears. From the Bhāgavatam we understand that Lord Buddha is the incarnation of Kṛṣṇa who appeared when materialism was rampant and materialists were using the pretext of the authority of the Vedas. Therefore each and every avatāra, or incarnation of the Lord, has a particular mission, and they are all described in the revealed scriptures.

🔗 Yoga Sūtra Connection

YS I.23 — īśvara-praṇidhānād vā
"Or, [the state of yoga is attainable] from devotion to the Lord."

Patañjali offers two paths to samādhi: the gradual path of sustained practice and dispassion (abhyāsa-vairāgya), OR the direct path — īśvara-praṇidhāna. BG 4.7 reveals the reciprocal dynamic: when the cosmic order (dharmasya) deteriorates through the inevitable entropy of time and human forgetfulness, the Lord Himself initiates the rescue. This is not transactional devotion — this is divine grace manifesting through history.

The Sāṅkhya analysis: prakṛti left to her own devices follows the pendulum of guṇa cycles — creation, preservation, dissolution. Eventually tamas overwhelms, and the cosmic confusion requires intervention from beyond the system. The puruṣa who is apara (beyond) descends as avatāra — not by necessity but by compassion. This is the ultimate truth of īśvara-praṇidhāna: divine descent meets human ascent.

📖 Sanskrit Terms

dharmasya — of righteousness, religious principles; the cosmic order that sustains all existence. Like source code governing reality's operating system — when corrupted, the whole system degrades.
glāniḥ — discrepancies, decline, diminishment; not total absence but erosion, like a shoreline worn by constant waves. This is entropy affecting spiritual law.
abhyutthānam — predominance, rising up; when the baser forces gain momentum and threaten to overwhelm the sāttvic foundation. The asura principle gaining critical mass.
adharmasya — of irreligion, that which is against dharma; the weight of accumulated adharma creates a pressure differential that actually invites divine intervention.
sṛjāmi — I manifest (from sṛj, to release, emit, let go); CRITICAL: NOT "create." The forms are eternal (kūṭa-stha, unchanging). The Lord appears by His own will, not process. This is avatāra — descent of what already exists in fullness.
ātmānam — Myself, My own form; He descends personally, not sending a subordinate. The exact same Supreme Person who dwells in Viṣṇu-loka walks among us.

The Portal Opens

Ārdrā — The Storm of Transformation. Rudra's nakṣatra, the moist one, the howler, the tearer. This is the cosmic energy that precedes the monsoon, the lightning flash that reveals what darkness concealed. BG 4.7 is the avatāra as thunderbolt — sudden, undeniable, reality-rearranging.

Atigaṇḍa Yoga — "Big Cheek" or "Great Mass." Abundance accumulated, substance, the weight of divine presence. When the Lord descends, He doesn't arrive quietly. His descent is massive, epoch-shifting, impossible to ignore. The avatāra carries the full weight of divinity — Atigaṇḍa.

Pañcamī Tithi — The fifth lunar day, associated with learning, education, worship. The Lord teaches through demonstration: "I am the principle. I am the law. When you forget, I remind you through My descent." This is pedagogy at cosmic scale.

Mars Day — The warrior energy. The avatāra comes not only to teach but to remove obstacles, to destroy what obstructs dharma. Tuesday is His day.

🧘 Practice

This verse is about divine initiative. You cannot earn it. You can only recognize it. Today's practice:

  1. Recognize glāni: Where in your life has fervor declined? Where have the "oughts" become "maybes"? Notice the entropy without judgment.
  2. Surrender to sṛjāmi: You cannot fix the cosmic order. But you can invoke the One who does. Chant the prayer of decision: "When I cannot, You can. I offer this to You."
  3. Atigaṇḍa meditation: Feel the weight of accumulated grace. Not "I need to be worthy" — grace is already vast, already present, already manifested in every breath, every heartbeat, every moment.

The avatāra principle operates at all scales: cosmic, historical, personal. What needs descending into your awareness today? What truth seeks manifestation?

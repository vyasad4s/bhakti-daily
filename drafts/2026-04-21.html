🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 4.7

Date: April 21, 2026

Sanskrit:

यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य ग्लानिर्भवति भारत ।

अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम् ॥ ७ ॥

Transliteration:

यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य
ग्लानिर्भवति भारत
अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य
तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम्

Translation:

Whenever and wherever there is a decline in religious practice, O descendant of Bharata, and a predominant rise of irreligion — at that time I manifest Myself.

Commentary:

The verse presents a cosmic operating system recovery protocol. Not arbitrary intervention but scheduled maintenance based on system degradation metrics. The conditions are precise: not when dharma is fully absent, but when it experiences glāni — wear, erosion, decline. Not when adharma is peripheral, but when it achieves abhyutthāna — victorious uprising, predominance.

Yadā yadā — not once but cyclically. The Lord's manifestation is recursive, not linear history. Each cosmic cycle experiences the same pattern: dharma peaking, declining, dissolving into predominance of adharma, then divine intervention. Like entropy increasing in a closed system until external energy input reverses the trend.

Dharmasya glāniḥ — erosion of the supporting structure. Dharma here is not "religion" as belief system but the natural law that sustains existence. When fire forgets to burn, when gravity weakens, when consciousness becomes impossible — that's glāni. The guṇas taken to their extreme logical conclusion produce conditions where prakṛti can no longer sustain sentient life.

Abhyutthānam adharmasya — the rise of what violates natural law. Not merely absence of dharma but active opposition. Tamas doesn't just darken; it declares darkness as truth. Rajas doesn't just agitate; it claims agitation as the goal. When the error state becomes default, intervention becomes necessary.

Sṛjāmi — the critical theological term. NOT "I create" (utpatti) because the Lord's form is not created. Sṛjāmi means "I release, emit, manifest what already IS." The avatāra is like decompressing a file that already exists — not new creation but revealed presence. The eternal form becomes apparent within the apparent time-stream.

Ātmānam — Himself, personally. Not sending an angel, not delegating to an agent. The Supreme Person steps out of transcendence into immanence. This destroys conceptions of "distant God" or "impersonal absolute."

Yoga Sūtra Connection:

🔥 YS I.23 — īśvara-praṇidhānād vā

"Or [samādhi is attained] by devotion to the Lord."

BG 4.7 describes the proactive version: when the yogī is too degraded to practice, the Lord's avatāra is īśvara-praṇidhāna in reverse — not our devotion ascending but Divine compassion descending. The Lord enables what we cannot achieve by our own effort (abhyāsa).

Sanskrit Terms:

  • yadā yadā — whenever and wherever; cyclical, not linear
  • dharmasya — of cosmic order, natural law
  • glāniḥ — decline, deterioration, erosion
  • abhyutthānam — rising up, predominance, victorious emergence
  • adharmasya — of irreligion, that which violates natural law
  • sṛjāmi — I manifest, emit, release; NOT create
  • ātmānam — Myself, personally; direct presence

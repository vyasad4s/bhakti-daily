BG 7.22 — Faith in Demigod Worship Yields Limited Fruit

स तया श्रद्धया युक्तस्तस्याराधनमीहते । > लभते च तत: कामान्मयैव विहितान्हि तान् ॥ २२ ॥

sa tayā śraddhayā yuktas tasya ārādhanam īhate labhate ca tataḥ kāmān mayā eva vihitān hi tān

Endowed with such a faith, he endeavors to worship a particular demigod and obtains his desires. But in actuality these benefits are bestowed by Me alone.

Commentary

This verse elucidates the Lord’s arrangement: even when a living entity worships a demigod motivated by material desire, the faith that sustains such worship is itself a gift from Krishna as the Supersoul. The demigod cannot grant any benediction without the Lord’s permission, and the results obtained are ultimately arranged by Krishna. Thus, the steady faith (śraddhā) that fuels demigod worship originates from the Divine, though the worshipper may misattribute the benefit to the demigod.

Today’s pañcāṅga highlights Masik Shivaratri (monthly Shivaratri) during the Trayodashi tithi, a sacred time for Shiva worship. The Nakṣatra Kritika (ruled by Agni, fire) governs the early part of the day, emphasizing purification and transformation, transitioning to Rohini (ruled by Brahma, growth) after 15:36 IST, bringing nurturing and creativity. The Yoga Sukarman (good deeds) prevails until 07:58 IST, supporting virtuous action, followed by Dhrithi (steadiness) yoga, which reinforces endurance and resolve. The Sun and Moon both in Taurus provide grounded stability and determination.

In this context, BG 7.22 reminds us that even when we seek material benefits through demigod worship (such as Shiva’s blessings for boons), the Lord’s presence as Supersoul steadies our faith. This steady faith is the foundation for progressing from external worship to internal realization of the Lord as the ultimate object of devotion. The verse encourages us to recognize that our very desire to worship stems from the Lord’s inspiration, and that He sustains that desire with unwavering faith, while gently guiding us toward the understanding that true and eternal benefit lies in devotion to Him alone.

Yoga Sūtra Connection: YS I.23

ईश्वरप्रणिधानात वा

īśvara-praṇidhānād vā

Or, this previously mentioned state is attainable from devotion to the lord.

BG 7.22 and YS I.23 both highlight the role of devotion to the Supreme Lord. In the Gītā, Krishna affirms that He steadies the faith of those who worship demigods, ensuring their devotion remains sincere. In the Yoga Sūtras, devotion to īśvara (the Lord) is presented as a direct means to attain the state of samprajñāta samādhi. The connection lies in the understanding that steady faith (śraddhā) in the Lord, whether expressed through direct worship or through the medium of demigods, is the power that lifts the mind beyond distraction. When we offer our faith to the Lord, He reciprocates by making our devotion unwavering, just as He steadies the faith of demigod worshippers. Thus, the practice of īśvara-praṇidhāna is the inner steadying of faith that BG 7.22 describes from the Lord’s side.

Cosmic Interpretation: Steady Faith Amidst Material Desire

Today’s Tithi is Trayodashi (Thirteenth day of Krishna Paksha), marking Masik Shivaratri—a monthly observance dedicated to Lord Shiva, ideal for worship seeking material boons or removal of obstacles. The Nakṣatra Kritika (ruled by Agni) governs until 15:36 IST, emphasizing fire’s purifying energy, which burns away impurities and supports transformation. After 15:36 IST, the Nakṣatra shifts to Rohini (ruled by Brahma), bringing growth, creativity, and nurturing energy—aligning with the verse’s promise that steady faith refines and elevates even materially motivated worship.

The Yoga Sukarman (good deed) until 07:58 IST highlights how virtuous actions are supported early in the day, while the succeeding Dhrithi (steadiness) yoga reinforces endurance and resolve, mirroring the journey from aspiring faith to steady faith. The Sun in Taurus provides grounded endurance, and the Moon’s movement through Taurus offers initiative followed by stability—reflecting the transition from material desire to divine steadiness.

Rāhu Kalā (09:12–11:04 IST) and Yamagandā (14:47–16:39 IST) create inauspicious windows, but the Amṛt Kāl (01:39–03:04 PM IST) and Brahma Muhūrta (03:53–04:41 AM IST) offer auspicious periods for spiritual practice. The wise devotee schedules japa, study, and chanting during these windows, aligning personal effort with cosmic support.

The verse’s assurance that the Lord steadies faith is especially relevant during Shivaratri, when many turn to Shiva worship seeking material boons. Recognizing that even such worship is sustained by the Supersoul’s steady faith allows the devotee to transcend the material goal and cultivate pure devotion to Krishna.

Sanskrit Terms

śraddhā — Faith, trust, conviction. Like the root certificate authority that establishes trust in a secure system.

yukta — Endowed, united, equipped. Like a well-configured system ready for operation.

ārādhanam — Worship, reverent service. Like a dedicated service running continuously in devotion.

īhate — Aspires, desires, longs for. Like a process that continuously requests a resource.

labhate — Obtains, attains, receives. Like a process that successfully acquires its target.

kāmān — Desires, wishes, longings. Like user inputs that drive system behavior.

mayā — By Me (the Lord). Like the divine kernel that enables all operations.

vihitān — Arranged, ordained, disposed. Like a configuration that orchestrates outcomes.

Practice for Shivaratri and Steady Faith

Morning (Brahma Muhūrta 03:53–04:41 AM): Chant BG 7.22 slowly. Focus on the word śraddhā — faith. Where in your life do you place your trust? Is it in material outcomes, relationships, or the Divine? Let the chant awaken the recognition that even your faith in demigod worship is steadied by the Supersoul. Then, as the day begins, offer your intention: “May my faith be steady and true to You, O Lord.”

Evening (Amṛt Kāl 01:39–03:04 PM IST / 11:39 AM–01:04 PM EDT): After the morning’s activities, sit in quiet reflection. The Moon in Taurus provides steadiness, and the Sun in Taurus offers endurance. In this transition, meditate on the steady faith that the Lord gives: not the external form of worship, but the internal trust that remains unshaken. Let YS I.23’s insight sink in: devotion to the Lord is the direct path to inner stability. Use this period for extended japa or study of the Bhāgavatam, focusing on the pastimes of the Lord that exemplify steady faith—such as Prahlāda’s unwavering devotion amidst tribulation.

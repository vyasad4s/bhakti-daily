BG 7.4 — Eight Energies, One Source

भूमिरापोऽनलो वायु: खं मनो बुद्धिरेव च ।

अहङ्कार इतीयं मे भिन्ना प्रकृतिरष्टधा ॥ ४ ॥

bhūmir āpo 'nalo vāyuḥ khaṁ mano buddhir eva ca
ahaṅkāra itīyaṁ me bhinnā prakṛtir aṣṭadhā

Earth, water, fire, air, ether, mind, intelligence and false ego – all together these eight constitute My separated material energies.

Commentary

Yesterday Kṛṣṇa said: hardly one among thousands knows Me in truth. Today He begins to explain why — by revealing the architecture of what stands between the seeker and that knowledge. Bhūmir āpo 'nalo vāyuḥ kham — earth, water, fire, air, ether. The five gross elements. The infrastructure of every physical form you have ever touched, tasted, seen, smelled, or heard. Mano buddhir eva ca ahaṅkāraḥ — mind, intelligence, false ego. The three subtle elements. The infrastructure of every thought you have ever thought, every identity you have ever claimed, every judgment you have ever rendered. Five plus three. Eight in total. Itīyaṁ me bhinnā prakṛtir aṣṭadhā — all these constitute My separated material energies, eightfold.

The word bhinnā is critical. It means "separated" — distinct from Kṛṣṇa, not identical with Him. These energies are His, but they are not Him. The clay is the potter's, but the clay is not the potter. This is the foundational distinction of Sāṅkhya metaphysics: prakṛti is real, it is divine in origin, but it is categorically other than puruṣa — and both are other than the Supreme Person who stands beyond and above both. The eight energies listed here constitute the entire material field — the aparā prakṛti, the "inferior" or "lower" nature that Kṛṣṇa will explicitly name in the next verse. Everything you can perceive, everything you can think, everything you can claim as "me" or "mine" — all of it is contained in this list. Your body is earth and water and fire and air and ether. Your mind is manas. Your discernment is buddhi. Your sense of "I" is ahaṅkāra. There is nothing in your experience that escapes these eight categories.

And that is precisely the problem. If your entire field of experience is constructed from Kṛṣṇa's separated energies, then to know only these energies is to know only the emanation — never the Source. The scientist who catalogs the five gross elements has touched the surface. The philosopher who analyzes mind and intelligence has gone deeper. But both remain within the territory of prakṛti. Neither has crossed the boundary into the parā prakṛti — the superior energy — which Kṛṣṇa will introduce in verse 5. The eight energies are the complete map of the material world. They are not the territory of the Supreme. The map is accurate. The territory is elsewhere.

Yoga Sūtra Bridge: YS II.17

द्रष्टृदृश्ययोः संयोगः हेयहेतुः

draṣṭṛ-dṛśyayoḥ saṁyogaḥ heyahetuḥ

The conjunction of the seer and the seen is the cause of what is to be avoided.

Patañjali's II.17 identifies the fundamental error: the conjunction (saṁyogaḥ) of consciousness (draṣṭṛ, the seer) with the visible field (dṛśya, the seen) is the root cause of suffering. BG 7.4 defines exactly what that visible field consists of: the eight separated energies of prakṛti. Everything Patañjali calls dṛśya — the entire contents of experience, gross and subtle — is contained in Kṛṣṇa's list. The five elements are the gross seen. Mind, intelligence, and false ego are the subtle seen. There is nothing beyond these eight in the field of the observable.

The conjunction Patañjali describes is precisely the failure to distinguish the eight energies from their Source. When consciousness identifies with any of these eight — with the body (earth, water, fire, air, ether), with the mind (manas), with intelligence (buddhi), or worst of all with the false sense of self (ahaṅkāra) — it has entered the saṁyoga, the mis-union. The cure is not to deny the reality of the eight energies — they are real, they are Kṛṣṇa's energies — but to recognize them as bhinnā, separated, emanated, not the Self. Patañjali calls this dis-identification viyoga — separation of seer from seen. Kṛṣṇa calls it knowing the difference between His aparā and parā prakṛti. Same mountain, different trails.

Cosmic Interpretation: Śravaṇa's Hearing, Guru's Teaching, Pañcamī's Five

Three cosmic signatures converge today, and each illuminates BG 7.4 from a different angle.

First: the nakṣatra transition. Uttarāṣāḍhā — "the later victory," governed by the Viśvadeva (universal gods) — yields to Śravaṇa at 6:11 PM. Śravaṇa is the nakṣatra of hearing. Its deity is Viṣṇu, the All-Pervading One — the same Viṣṇu whose three expansions (Mahā-viṣṇu, Garbhodaka-śāyī Viṣṇu, Kṣīrodaka-śāyī Viṣṇu) Prabhupāda cites in his purport to this very verse. The alignment is uncanny: on the day the Guru (Guruvāra) enumerates His eight separated energies, the nakṣatra of sacred hearing activates, and its presiding deity is the very expansion of Kṛṣṇa who creates, enters, and pervades those energies. Śravaṇa demands that we listen — not analyze, not debate, but receive the teaching as it is given. The catalog of eight is not a philosophical exercise. It is a revelation. And revelation is received through hearing.

Second: Guruvāra — Thursday, Jupiter's day. Jupiter is the guru, the teacher, the principle of expansion and wisdom. On this day, Kṛṣṇa the Guru teaches the structure of His own creation. The eight energies are not arbitrary — they are the curriculum. Jupiter's energy expands the mind's capacity to receive the teaching. The gross five are accessible to anyone. The subtle three require the guru's guidance. Material science stops at the ten (five elements + five sense objects). It takes a guru to reveal the three that matter most: mind, intelligence, and the false ego that binds consciousness to the seen.

Third: Pañcamī — the fifth tithi, activating at 2:00 PM. The number five is not incidental to BG 7.4. The five gross elements (bhūmi, āpaḥ, anala, vāyu, kha) are listed first for a reason: they are the most immediately perceivable, the entry-level curriculum of prakṛti. Pañcamī's energy activates the fivefold foundation — the gross matter from which everything physical is constructed. When Chaturthi yields to Pañcamī at 2:00 PM, the obstacle has been removed (yesterday's Sankashti energy) and the structure begins to rise. The moon in Makara (Capricorn) — the sign of form, structure, the crystallization of energy into matter — confirms the theme: today is about the architecture of the material field, the skeleton of prakṛti. Brahma yoga (sacred knowledge) holds through the day. The knowledge being transmitted is sacred because it reveals the source code of creation itself.

Sanskrit Terms

bhinnā — Separated, divided, distinct. The critical qualifier: prakṛti is Kṛṣṇa's energy, but it is not Kṛṣṇa Himself. The emanation is real, but it is other than the Source. The compiled code is not the compiler.

prakṛti — Material nature, the primordial matrix from which all material manifestations evolve. In BG 7.4, specifically the aparā (inferior/separated) prakṛti — the eightfold energy that constitutes the entire visible field. Not illusion — real energy, but separated from its Source.

aṣṭadhā — Eightfold. The complete enumeration: five gross (earth, water, fire, air, ether) plus three subtle (mind, intelligence, false ego). Nothing in material experience falls outside these eight categories. The full system call table of prakṛti.

ahaṅkāra — False ego, the principle of misidentification. Not "pride" in the moral sense — the structural error that claims "I am this body, I am this mind, I am these acquisitions." The deepest of the three subtle elements because it is the gateway through which consciousness enters the conjunction (saṁyoga) with the seen.

draṣṭṛ-dṛśya — Seer and seen. Patañjali's fundamental dualism: consciousness (puruṣa, the seer) and the field of experience (prakṛti, the seen). BG 7.4 is the catalog of dṛśya — everything that can be seen, including the mind that sees it. The seer alone stands outside the eight.

saṁyogaḥ — Conjunction, union, mis-identification. Patañjali's term for the fundamental error: the seer identifying with the seen. Not a real union — puruṣa and prakṛti never actually merge — but a mistaken association, like a program believing it is the hardware it runs on.

Practice for Kṛṣṇa Pañcamī / Śravaṇa Transition

Morning: During Brahma Muhūrta (3:54–4:42 AM), chant BG 7.4 slowly. After each element, pause. Bhūmi — feel your body's weight. Āpaḥ — feel the moisture in your breath. Anala — feel the warmth of your chest. Vāyu — feel the air entering your lungs. Kha — feel the space around you. Manas — notice the mind that notices. Buddhi — notice the intelligence that discriminates. Ahaṅkāra — notice the one who claims "I am noticing." Each of these is bhinnā — separated. None of them is the Self. The Amrit Kaal (11:03 AM–12:50 PM) is the nectar window for contemplation.

Evening: After 6:11 PM, Śravaṇa nakṣatra activates — the star of hearing, governed by Viṣṇu. This is the night to listen. Not to analyze, not to debate, but to receive. The eight energies have been enumerated. Tomorrow Kṛṣṇa will reveal what lies beyond them. Offer tonight's japa as an act of hearing — śravaṇam, the first and most essential limb of bhakti. Let the guru's teaching land. The moon in Makara gives the discipline to sit still long enough for it to arrive.

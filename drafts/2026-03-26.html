Sarvartha Siddhi

Punarvasu means "giving again" — the return of light after darkness. Thursday (Guruvar) opens the wisdom portal. Navamī's completion energy asks: What cycles are completing in your life? What returns, renewed?

Bhagavad-gītā | Chapter 2, Verse 26
अथ चैनं नित्यजातं नित्यं वा मन्यसे मृतम्
तथापि त्वं महाबाहो नैनं शोचितुमर्हसि ॥ २६ ॥
atha cainaṁ nitya-jātaṁ nityaṁ vā manyase mṛtam
tathāpi tvaṁ mahā-bāho nainaṁ śocitum arhasi
If, however, you think that the soul is always being born and always dying, you still have no reason to lament, O mighty-armed one.

Term Definitions

  • atha — "if, however" — introduces alternative viewpoint
  • nitya-jātam — "eternally born" — the materialist view of endless cycles without liberation
  • tathāpi — "even so" — the pivot: regardless of metaphysical position
  • mahā-bāhu — "O mighty-armed one" — Kṛṣṇa's ironic address: Arjuna is "mighty" in arms but weak in resolve
  • na...arhasi — "you do not deserve" — the verdict: grief is beneath a warrior
Yoga Sūtra Bridge

YS 2.12 | kleśa-mūlaḥ karma-āśayo dṛṣṭa-ādṛṣṭa-janma-vedanīyaḥ

"The root of action is the afflictions, and its result is experienced in seen and unseen births."

Patañjali maps the mechanics: kleśas (afflictions) → karma → birth cycles. Even without accepting the soul's eternity, the cycle continues. The question is not if cycles exist, but how to transcend them.

The Punarvasu Principle

Punarvasu means the return of light — the nakṣatra of Aditi, the divine mother who restores what was lost. In Punarvasu's energy, nothing truly ends — it only returns, renewed.

This verse presents the "even if" argument. Even if Arjuna subscribed to the Lokāyatika or Vaibhāṣika view — that life is merely a combination of material elements producing temporary consciousness, that there is no eternal soul, only perpetual cycles of birth and death — still: no grief is warranted. If souls constantly generate from matter and dissolve back into it, why lament? One kills only chemicals. No one weeps when wood burns or water evaporates.

Kṛṣṇa addresses him as mahā-bāho — "mighty-armed one" — with pointed irony. Arjuna has spent years forging his arms into deadly instruments. Yet now, facing the inevitable discharge of duty, he falters. His muscles are mighty; his mind is weak. The warrior who could draw the Gāṇḍīva without effort now cannot draw the sword of discrimination.

The deeper teaching: grief is a luxury born of confusion. Whether one accepts the ātmā as eternal (Vedic view) or transient (materialist view), the prescription remains unchanged. Lamentation serves no purpose. It does not stop death. It does not restore life. It only clouds the intelligence needed to act.

The Cycle Denied

Modernity has its own nitya-jātam — the belief that consciousness "emerges" from chemical complexity and "ceases" when that complexity breaks down. This is the anthropology of matter: life as organized chemistry, death as thermal equilibrium. The Vaibhāṣikas would recognize it immediately.

Kṛṣṇa's counter: tathāpi — "even then." Even accepting this framework, where does grief fit? If there is no permanent soul to lose, there is no permanent loss. If existence is perpetual recycling, why resist the inevitable? The materialist who grieves contradicts their own creed.

The Navamī Completion

Navamī — the ninth day of the waxing moon — carries the energy of completion without fullness. It is not yet pūrṇimā (fullness), but the arc is clear. What began as a sliver has grown substantial. What seeds were planted are now visible.

In the 15-day cycle of Chapter 2, we are near the middle. The unchangeable soul has been established (2.11-2.25). The ammunition has been loaded. Now Kṛṣṇa varies the angle: attacking even opposing philosophies. This is comprehensive instruction — leaving no refuge for the reluctant warrior.

The work of today: recognize which cycles you inhabit. Punarvasu asks: what returns to you? What patterns recur? The āsana you abandoned, the japa you postponed, the truth you glimpsed then forgot — all wait to be restored. The light returns. The question is whether you are positioned to receive it.

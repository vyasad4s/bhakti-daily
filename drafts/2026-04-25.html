🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 4.2

Date: April 25, 2026

Sanskrit:

एवं परम्पराप्राप्तमिमं राजर्षयो विदुः ।

स कालेनेह महता योगो नष्टः परन्तप ॥ २ ॥

Transliteration:

एवं परम्पराप्राप्तम्
इमं राजर्षयो विदुः
स कालेनेह महता
योगो नष्टः परन्तप

Translation:

This supreme science was thus received through the chain of disciplic succession, and the saintly kings understood it in that way. But in course of time the succession was broken, and therefore the science as it is appears to be lost.

Commentary:

The truth doesn't change, but our ability to perceive it does. This is the tragedy and the opportunity of every age: the science of yoga is eternal, but its transmission depends on human vessels capable of receiving and relaying it without distortion.

Evaṁ paramparā-prāptam — received through the succession. Not invented, not discovered through individual effort, but transmitted like source code through git history. Each generation commits the same eternal truth, preserving the hash integrity across time.

Rāja-ṛṣayaḥ viduḥ — saintly kings understood. Those who wield authority not for domination but for spiritual protection. The original executive branch of dharma — they governed not from ego but from realization.

Saḥ kālena iha mahatā — in the course of great time. Entropy operates on lineages too. Without vigilant maintenance, the connection degrades. The SSH tunnel drops. The signal scatters.

Naṣṭaḥ — scattered, lost, broken. Not destroyed (the truth is eternal) but the ACCESS was severed. The paramparā degraded into formalism without realization, ritual without understanding. When those meant to preserve dharma become its enemies, the succession fractures.

Why did it break? Because the royal order became corrupt — wielding power for exploitation rather than protection. This is the Saturn principle: Śani doesn't destroy, he TESTS. He reveals where structures have become hollow. His is the slow heat that tempers steel, the pressure that forges new vessels.

"The science as it is appears to be lost." Not irrevocably. Hidden, obscured, awaiting restoration by one who speaks with authority not borrowed but direct. Kṛṣṇa now speaks to Arjuna because the chain was broken; necessity forced the source to reappear. When the lineage degrades, the originator returns.

Yoga Sūtra Connection:

🔥 YS I.14 — sa tu dīrgha-kāla-nairantarya-satkārāsevito dṛḍha-bhūmiḥ

"Practice becomes firmly established when cultivated uninterruptedly and with devotion over a prolonged period."

The paramparā IS that prolonged, uninterrupted cultivation. Not one lifetime's effort but the accumulated abhyāsa (practice) and vairāgya (dispassion) of countless practitioners compressed and transmitted like a zip file of realized consciousness. When the succession breaks, continuity is severed — each generation must restart from zero rather than building on accumulated momentum.

Satkāra (devotion, respect, receptivity) is essential. Without this attitude toward the teaching and the teacher, the signal cannot transmit. The royal order lost their satkāra; power made them proud, and pride blocks reception.

Sanskrit Terms:

  • paramparā — disciplic succession; unbroken chain of transmission, like git history preserving truth across generations
  • prāpta — received, obtained through transmission; not self-generated
  • rāja-ṛṣayaḥ — saintly kings; rulers established in dharma, executive branch of spiritual order
  • viduḥ — they knew, understood; direct perception, not theory
  • kālena mahatā — in the course of great time; entropy affects even strongest lineages
  • naṣṭaḥ — scattered, lost, broken; access severed, not truth destroyed
  • parantapa — O scorcher of enemies; Arjuna's epithet indicating capacity to burn obstacles

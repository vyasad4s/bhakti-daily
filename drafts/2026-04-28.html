🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 4.6

Date: April 28, 2026

Sanskrit:

अजोऽपि सन्नव्ययात्मा भूतानामीश्वरोऽपि सन् ।

प्रकृतिं स्वामधिष्ठाय सम्भवाम्यात्ममायया ॥ ६ ॥

Transliteration:

अजोऽपि सन्नव्ययात्मा
भूतानामीश्वरोऽपि सन्
प्रकृतिं स्वामधिष्ठाय
सम्भवाम्यात्ममायया

Translation:

Although I am unborn, My transcendental body never deteriorates, and I am the Lord of all living entities, still I appear in every millennium in My original transcendental form.

Commentary:

The verse presents the ultimate paradox: the unborn appears, the undecaying manifests, the greatest becomes accessible. This is not a contradiction but the supreme mystery of the avatāra, the Lord's descent through His own internal potency.

Ajaḥ api san — unborn and yet present. The Lord is not created by nature (prakṛti) nor subject to time (kāla). He is āja, unborn, without beginning. Yet He appears. This appearance is not incarnation (entering into a material body) but sambhava — manifestation of what already IS.

Avyayātmā — imperishable, never deteriorating. The Lord's body is not material; it doesn't age, decay, or dissolve. Unlike our bodies which cycle through creation and destruction, His form is nitya (eternal) and sac-cid-ānanda (existence-knowledge-bliss).

Bhūtānām īśvaraḥ api san — the Lord of beings. He possesses the rulership that no other can claim. Yet He appears not to dominate but to connect, not to subdue but to elevate.

Prakṛtiṁ svām adhiṣṭhāya — standing upon My own nature. The Lord does not enter material nature but stands upon His own prakṛti, His supreme energy. This is the yoga-māyā which conceals yet reveals, which makes the transcendent accessible.

Sambhavāmi ātma-māyayā — I manifest by My internal potency. The ātma-māyā is the Lord's own power, not the external illusion that binds the jīva. It is the self-delight by which He plays while remaining fully Himself. Like an actor who never becomes the character but plays it convincingly.

Today's Uttara Phālgunī nakṣatra is ruled by Aryaman, the cosmic container of honor, contracts, and commitments. It is the marriage bed where vows are fulfilled. The Lord's appearance is not casual but is His eternal commitment to protect dharma. Saumya Yoga provides the auspicious gentleness through which this descent occurs.

Yoga Sūtra Connection:

🔥 YS III.42 — kāya-rūpa-saṁyama-tat-grāhya-śakti-stambhe cakṣuḥ-prakāśa-saṁyogāntar-dhānam

"By saṁyama on the form, the power of light ceasing to reflect, invisibility is gained."

The siddhi of invisibility corresponds to the Lord's capacity to conceal and reveal — His divine form is ever-present but only visible when He wishes. The avatāra is the opposite process: what was hidden becomes apparent, what was invisible descends into visibility, not for the yogi's power but for the devotee's devotion.

Sanskrit Terms:

  • ajaḥ — unborn, without beginning; the absolute source
  • avyayātmā — imperishable self; transcendental body never deteriorating
  • bhūtānām — of beings, of all entities
  • īśvaraḥ — the Lord, the supreme controller
  • prakṛtiṁ — nature; here referring to the Lord's own supreme energy
  • adhiṣṭhāya — standing upon, residing in
  • ātma-māyayā — by His internal potency/power

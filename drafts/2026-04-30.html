🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 4.4

Date: April 30, 2026

Sanskrit:

ज्ञानं तेऽहं सविज्ञेयम् अत्राश्चर्योऽपि विस्तृतः ।

यज्ज्ञात्वा नेह भूयोऽन्यज्ज्ञातव्यं अवशिष्यते ॥ ४ ॥

Transliteration:

jñānaṁ te 'haṁ sa-vijñeyaṁ atra āścaryo 'pi vistṛtaḥ | yaj jñātvā neha bhūyo 'nyaj jñātavyam avaśiṣyate || 4 ||

Translation:

Śrī Bhagavān said: I shall now declare to you in full this knowledge, the knowable, and the knowledge of its realization — what, knowing which, nothing more here remains to be known.

Commentary:

Meta-Knowledge: BG 4.4 reveals the epistemic structure beyond content — the capacity for the mind to receive knowledge-as-object. Sa-vijñeyam means the knower, the known, and the act of knowing become one. Not information about knowledge, but knowledge as reality.

Amāvāsyā Reset: Today's new moon is the zero-point of the lunar cycle. Svāmī B.R. Śrīdhara comments that Amāvāsyā is samādhi moment — dissolution into emptiness before renewal. BG 4.4 delivers knowledge that needs no erasure — perfect transmission timing.

Svātī Nakshatra: Ruled by Vāyu (wind), symbolizing movement and dissemination. The knotted karma of Svātī is untied by jñānam avanti — wisdom beyond the knot. Physical winds scatter; Svātī's knowledge binds revelation.

Sāṅkhya-First Principles:

The verse presents jñānaṁ sa-vijñeyam as bhāva-vastu — existing reality:

  • Pratyakṣa: The Lord speaks directly to Arjuna's buddhi
  • Anumāna: Obsolete — no inference needed since all is revealed
  • Āgama: Elevated — testimony BECOMES perception

Kṛṣṇa triggers vidyā-vināśa-vardhan — wisdom growth eclipses ignorance completely. The final dip: jñātavyam avaśiṣyate — nothing remains unknown.

Sanskrit Terms:

  • jñānām — knowledge (neuter, from √jñā)
  • sa-vijñeyam — along-with-the-knowable; knowledge-as-object
  • āścaryo — wonderful, marvelous; the numinous
  • jñātvā — having known (gerund, completed action)
  • avaśiṣyate — remains (from ava-√śiṣ, to remain)

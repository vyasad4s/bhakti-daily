🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 4.3

Date: April 29, 2026

Observance: Narasimha Jayanti

Sanskrit:

स एवायं मया तेऽद्य योगः प्रोक्तः पुरातनः ।

भक्तोऽसि मे सखा चेति रहस्यं ह्येतदुत्तमम् ॥ ३ ॥

Transliteration:

sa evāyaṁ mayā te 'dya
yogaḥ proktaḥ purātanaḥ
bhakto 'si me sakhā ceti
rahasyaṁ hy etad uttamam

Translation:

That very ancient science of the relationship with the Supreme is today told by Me to you because you are My devotee as well as My friend and can therefore understand the transcendental mystery of this science.

Commentary:

Kṛṣṇa reveals why this knowledge is being transmitted. The secret is not withheld from qualified recipients. Arjuna receives because of two conditions: bhaktaḥ asi (you are My devotee) and sakhā ca (and My friend). Both are required — devotion without intimacy remains formal; intimacy without devotion becomes familiar.

Saḥ eva ayam — that very same (ancient) science. Not new, not different. The same knowledge spoken millions of years ago to Vivasvān. Kṛṣṇa is telling the truth — this is not theoretical religion, this is recovery of the original system.

Mayā te adya proktaḥ — proclaimed by Me to you today. The Lord does not repeat this to everyone. The transmission requires qualification. Today, Narasimha Jayanti, we remember how the Lord appeared specifically for His devotee Prahlāda — just as now He speaks for Arjuna.

Rahasyam uttamam — the supreme secret. This is veda-rahasya, the hidden core of the Vedas. Not for those who treat spiritual knowledge as information, but for those who treat the Lord as beloved and friend.

Narasimha Jayanti: The day the Lord appeared from the pillar — neither inside nor outside — to protect His devotee. The same principle: knowledge appears where there is devotion. The pillar was just wood until devotion called forth the Lord. Arjuna's devotion calls forth this science today.

Yoga Sūtra Connection:

🔥 YS I.7 — pratyakṣa-anumāna-āgamāḥ pramāṇāni

"Direct perception, inference, and testimony are the sources of valid knowledge."

Kṛṣṇa here exemplifies āgama — testimony, the word of the authoritative teacher. But this is not blind acceptance. Arjuna qualifies to receive because his devotion AND friendship create the vessel. Patañjali's three sources (perception, inference, testimony) combine when the Lord speaks to the heart illuminated by bhakti.

Sanskrit Terms:

  • saḥ eva — that very same (the ancient science)
  • pura-tanaḥ — ancient; from the earliest times
  • proktaḥ — proclaimed, spoken
  • bhaktaḥ — devotee; one who is devoted
  • sakhā — friend; intimate companion
  • rahasya — mystery, secret; the hidden thing
  • uttamam — supreme, highest, best

Narasimha Jayanti — Caturdaśī Amavatya Eve

Panchāṅga Data:

  • Nakṣatra: Hasta → Chitrā
  • Yoga: Harṣaṇā → Vajra

Cosmic Interpretation:

Amāvāsyā approaches: The dark moon where forms dissolve — perfect for receiving the rahasya, the secret that transcends form. The Lord's appearance as Narasimha was at twilight (neither day nor night), from the pillar (neither inside nor outside), on His lap (neither earth nor sky). Today's Caturdaśī holds that same paradox.

Hasta → Chitrā: The hand yields to the brilliant gem. Knowledge becomes radiant when received with devotion. What was hidden in the pillar becomes visible — what was theory becomes friendship.

Narasimha Jayanti: The Lord appears not for philosophical advancement but for pure devotion. Prahlāda was five years old. His qualification was not scholarship but surrender. This is the uttama-rahasya — the supreme secret is available to anyone who loves.

System Notes:

  • [RAHASYA AVAILABLE] Supreme secret open to qualified hearts

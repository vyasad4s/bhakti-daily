🏹 Bhagavad-gītā 3.6 | March 01, 2026

 

कर्मेन्द्रियाणि संयम्य य आस्ते मनसा स्मरन्

इन्द्रियार्थान्विमूढात्मा मिथ्याचारः स उच्यते ॥ ३.६ ॥

 

karmendriyāṇi saṁyamya
ya āste manasā smaran
indriyārthān vimūḍhātmā
mithyācāraḥ sa ucyate

 

"One who restrains the senses of action but whose mind dwells on sense objects certainly deludes himself and is called a pretender."

 

The pretender problem: you can sit still, but if the mind runs to sense objects, you're fooling yourself. Mithyācāraḥ — the great cheater. Not the action, but the attachment to phala (fruit) is the trap.

 

Karmendriyāṇi — the five working sense organs (hands, legs, speech, genitals, anus). You can restrain them, but...

 

Manasā smaran — thinking with the mind of sense objects (indriya-arthān). This is where the theft happens. The body sits, the mind chases.

 

Today's cosmic: Trayodaśī (13th tithi) → Āśleṣa (entanglement) → Magha (the throne). The shift from clinging to sovereignty. Prabhupāda's warning: the false yogī is the greatest cheater. Speaking dry philosophy while dwelling on sense enjoyment.

 

🔥 YS 1.12 — abhyāsa-vairāgyābhyāṁ — practice AND detachment BOTH required. Not one without the other.

Bhagavad Gita Chapter 9, Verse 23

येऽप्यन्यदेवताभक्ता यजन्ते श्रद्धयान्विता: । तेऽपि मामेव कौन्तेय यजन्त्यविधिपूर्वकम् ॥ २३ ॥
ye ’py anya-devatā-bhaktā yajante śraddhayānvitāḥ te ’pi mām eva kaunteya yajanty avidhi-pūrvakam
Those who are devotees of other gods and who worship them with faith actually worship only Me, O son of Kuntī, but they do so in a wrong way.
“Persons who are engaged in the worship of demigods are not very intelligent, although such worship is offered to Me indirectly,” Kṛṣṇa says. For example, when a man pours water on the leaves and branches of a tree without pouring water on the root, he does so without sufficient knowledge or without observing regulative principles. Similarly, the process of rendering service to different parts of the body is to supply food to the stomach. The demigods are, so to speak, different officers and directors in the government of the Supreme Lord. One has to follow the laws made by the government, not by the officers or directors. Similarly, everyone is to offer his worship to the Supreme Lord only. That will automatically satisfy the different officers and directors of the Lord. The officers and directors are engaged as representatives of the government, and to offer some bribe to the officers and directors is illegal. This is stated here as avidhi-pūrvakam. In other words, Kṛṣṇa does not approve the unnecessary worship of the demigods.

Cosmic Alignment for July 12, 2026

Tithi: Krishna Paksha Trayodashi (until 01:00 PM), then Krishna Paksha Chaturdashi. Today is Masik Shivaratri, a sacred monthly observance dedicated to Lord Shiva, involving fasting, vigil, and worship.

Nakshatra: Mrigashirsha (until 08:11 AM) – the “searching star” symbolizing the quest for truth; then Ardra (from 08:11 AM) – associated with tears, renewal, and the storm god Rudra, another name for Shiva.

Yoga: Vridhi (until 10:35 AM) – growth, increase, prosperity; then Dhruva (from 10:35 AM) – steadfastness, immovability like the pole star.

Vāra: Raviwar (Sunday) – ruled by Surya (the Sun), the illuminator of the soul, supporting self-realization and the recognition of the divine within.

The cosmic alignment today reflects the theme of Bhagavad Gita 9.23: sincere worship directed toward various deities ultimately reaches the Supreme Lord Krishna, but the full benefit is attained when such worship is performed with proper vidhi (regulative principles) of bhakti‑yoga. Mrigashirsha’s searching energy leads to Ardra’s transformative tears; Vridhi’s growth culminates in Dhruva’s steadfastness. On this Shivaratri, let us recognize that all true devotion, wherever offered, finds its goal in Krishna, and let us engage in that devotion with the correct consciousness.

Yoga Sutra Connection

YS I.23: īśvara-praṇidhānād vā

Or, [samādhi is attained] by devotion to the Lord.

This sutra complements BG 9.23 by stating that devotion to the Supreme Lord (īśvara‑praṇidhāna) is a direct means to attain samādhi. Even those who worship other deities with faith are, unknowingly, directing their devotion toward the Lord. When such devotion is performed with the correct understanding and regulative principles (vidhi‑pūrvaka), it becomes pure bhakti, leading swiftly to the perfection of yoga. The verse’s acknowledgment that worship of deities reaches the Lord “but in a wrong way” (avidhi‑pūrvakam) highlights the importance of proper method—exactly what the Yoga Sūtras provide through disciplined practice and surrender.

Practice for Today

Shivaratri in Krishna Consciousness: Observe the fast if health permits, but keep your mind fixed on Krishna. Chant the Hare Krishna mahā‑mantra throughout the day and night, especially during the prescribed puja times.

Offering with Vidhi: If you traditionally offer bilva leaves to Shiva, offer them instead to Krishna (or to a Shiva lingam with the consciousness that you are offering to Krishna, the Supreme Lord). Accompany your offering with the mantra “Om namo bhagavate vasudevaya.”

Study and Reflection: Read Bhagavad Gita Chapter 9 verses 22‑24, which discuss the Lord’s relationship with devotees who worship other deities. Reflect on how sincere worship, regardless of form, ultimately pleases the Supreme when offered with faith and adherence to bhakti‑yoga principles.

Sun‑Inspired Meditation (Raviwar): Since Sunday is ruled by the Sun, meditate on the sun as a symbol of the soul’s effulgence. Visualize your heart as a sun radiating love for Krishna, dispelling the darkness of ignorance.

Chant for Devotion: Chant the Hare Krishna mahā‑mantra with the bhava (feeling) that your chant reaches Lord Krishna directly, purifying any misdirected worship and establishing your eternal relationship with Him.

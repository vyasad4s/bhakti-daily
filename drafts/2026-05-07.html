🏹 Bhagavad Gita 5.10

The portal of Pūrva Phālgunī opens: The fire of creative action meets the shield of detachment.

ब्रह्मण्याधाय कर्माणि सङ्गं त्यक्त्वाคารभते । न लिप्यते तदेpady a karmaṇi puruṣaḥ ॥ १० ॥

brahmaṇy ādhāya karmāṇi saṅgaṁ tyaktvā karabhate | na lipyate tadapy a karmaṇi puruṣaḥ || 10 ||

Translation (Vedabase.io): "One who performs his duty without attachment, surrendering the results unto the Supreme Lord, is unaffected by sinful action, as the lotus leaf is untouched by water."

🔥 The Non-Stick Kernel: The Lotus Effect

BG 5.10 introduces the Lotus Leaf Protocol. In the material world, every action leaves a "trace" (karma) on the soul, much like water sticking to a surface. This creates a recursive loop of cause and effect that keeps the soul bound to the Samsāra hardware.

The secret is surrender (brahmaṇy ādhāya). By shifting the "owner" of the action from the ego to the Supreme, the yogi implements a non-stick layer. The action is still performed—the work is done, the duties are met—but the karmic residue cannot find a handle to attach to. In system terms, the action is executed in a **sandbox environment**; the results are routed to the Supreme, leaving the soul's primary partition clean.

Sāṅkhya Architecture:

  • Sanga (Attachment): The "glue" that binds the puruṣa to the results of prakṛti.
  • Lipyate (Sticking): The process of karmic imprint (saṁskāra) on the subtle body.
  • Lotus Leaf Property: The a-priori state of the soul (pure, untouched), restored through bhakti.

Practical application: Today's Pūrva Phālgunī energy is luxurious and creative. The temptation is to "own" the success of your creativity. Use the lotus leaf filter: act with 100% intensity, but maintain a 0% claim on the result. Be the instrument, not the author.

🔗 Yoga Sūtra Bridge: YS II.33

Original Sanskrit: मैत्रीकरुणामुदितोक्षान्तः सुखदुःखयोः

Transliteration: maitrī-karuṇā-mudito-kṣāntaḥ sukha-duḥkha-yōḥ

Translation: "Cultivate friendliness toward the happy, compassion for the unhappy, compassion for the ignorant, and equanimity toward the indifferent."

Bridge to BG 5.10: The "non-stick" state of BG 5.10 is achieved through the emotional hygiene of YS II.33. By replacing anger and attachment with friendliness and compassion, the yogi removes the "adhesive" from the soul. When you are equanimous toward happiness and distress, you become the lotus leaf—present in the water, but never wet.

⚡ TERM DEFINITIONS

brahmaṇy ādhāya – placing in the Absolute; ownership transfer of the result.

saṅga – the psychic glue of attachment that triggers karmic imprints.

na lipyate – "not sticking"; the status of a process that generates no logs.

Pūrva Phālgunī – The "Early Autumn" nakṣatra; symbolizes the peak of creative luxury and the need for detachment.

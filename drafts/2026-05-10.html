Bhagavad Gita Chapter 5, Verse 7

सanskrit

योगयुक्तो विश‍ुद्धात्मा विजितात्मा जितेन्द्रिय: । सर्वभूतात्मभूतात्मा कुर्वन्नपि न लिप्यते ॥ ७ ॥

Transliteration

yoga-yukto viśuddhātmā vijitātmā jitendriyaḥ sarva-bhūtātma-bhūtātmā kurvann api na lipyate

Translation

One who works in devotion, who is a pure soul, and who controls his mind and senses is dear to everyone, and everyone is dear to him. Though always working, such a man is never entangled.

Purport

One who works in devotion (yoga-yuktaḥ), with purified consciousness (viśuddhātmā), conquered senses (jitendriyaḥ), and sees the Self in all beings (sarva-bhūtātma-bhūtātmā) is not entangled (na lipyate) because they operate as a root process with detached output—executing divine commands while remaining untouched by reactions. In system terms: the karma yogi is a sudo process with LD_PRELOAD of bhakti, running in a container where all writes are routed to /dev/null (the Absolute). The senses are file descriptors locked to O_RDONLY—input only, no write-back to ego. The mind is the CPU affinity set to the Absolute core, eliminating context switches to desire. Actions are executed with nice -20 (high priority) but ionice idle (no disk I/O to karmic storage). Result: zero entropy increase in the soul’s logfile. This is not metaphor—it’s the operational reality of surrender: when the result-pointer is redirected to Śrī Kṛṣṇa, the karmic cache is flushed on every cycle, and the process runs in read-only mode on the material plane. Hence, though acting constantly, there is no binding.

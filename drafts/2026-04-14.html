🏹 VERSE CONTENT

Source: BG 3.10

Date: April 14, 2026

Sanskrit:

सहयज्ञाः प्रजाः सृष्ट्वा पुरोवाच प्रजापतिः ।

अनेन प्रसविष्यध्वमेष वोऽस्त्विष्टकामधुक् ॥ १० ॥

Transliteration:

सहयज्ञाः प्रजाः सृष्ट्वा
पुरोवाच प्रजापतिः
अनेन प्रसविष्यध्वम्
एष वोऽस्त्विष्टकामधुक्

Translation:

In the beginning of creation, the Lord of all creatures sent forth generations of men and demigods, along with sacrifices for Viṣṇu, and blessed them by saying, "Be thou happy by this yajña [sacrifice] because its performance will bestow upon you everything desirable for living happily and achieving liberation."

Commentary:

The verse unfolds the original design: creation was not merely a dispersion of souls into material existence, but a coordinated system where sacrifice (yajña) serves as the operational link between the living and the Divine. Men and demigods are not adversaries but cooperators, each class performing distinct functions within a single hierarchy.

Saha-yajñāḥ — along with sacrifices. The destination of embodied existence was never mere survival, but sacrificial exchange. The material energy itself is designed to facilitate this circulation: the demigods supply air, light, water, and natural prosperity, and they do so based on the performance of yajña by human beings.

Prajāpatiḥ purā uvāca — the Lord of creatures spoke in the beginning. This is not later instruction but primordial ordination. The yajña system was not invented for difficult souls; it was instituted along with the souls themselves.

Iṣṭa-kāma-dhuk — the bestower of all desirable things. The prajāpati's blessing is both material and spiritual: prosperity in this world and liberation from the world.

Yoga Sutra Connection:

🔥 YS I.33 — maitrī-karuṇā-muditopekṣāṇāṁ sukha-duḥkha-puṇyāpuṇya-viṣayāṇāṁ bhāvanātaś citta-prasādanam

"By cultivating friendliness toward the happy, compassion toward the suffering, joy toward the virtuous, and equanimity toward the wicked, the mind becomes clear and calm."

This sūtra provides the practical foundation for yajña-vision. The attitude of the offerer must be prasanna — settled, clear, undisturbed — before the vision of sacrifice becomes possible.

Sanskrit Terms:

  • saha-yajñāḥ — along with sacrifices; created beings were sent with the institutional apparatus already in place
  • prajāḥ — generations, progeny, the created beings
  • sṛṣṭvā — having sent forth, created; intentional construction
  • puraḥ uvāca — spoke in ancient times, spoke at the beginning; primordial communication
  • prajāpatiḥ — the Lord of creatures, progenitor of all living entities; Viṣṇu as source
  • prasaviṣyadhvam — you will become more prosperous; from pra-sava, to bring forth, to flourish
  • iṣṭa-kāma-dhuk — the bestower of desired objects; iṣṭa = desired, kāma = objects of desire, dhuk = bestower

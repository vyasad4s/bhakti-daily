Source: BG 3.10

Date: April 14, 2026

Sanskrit:

ब्रह्मार्पणं ब्रह्म हविर्ब्रह्माग्नौ ब्रह्मणा हुतम् ।

ब्रह्मैव तेन गन्तव्यं ब्रह्मकर्मसमाधिना ॥ १० ॥

Transliteration:

brahmārpaṇaṁ brahma havir
brahmāgnau brahmaṇā hutam
brahmaiva tena gantavyaṁ
brahma-karma-samādhinā

Translation:

The offering is Brahman. The oblation is Brahman. It is offered by Brahman in the fire of Brahman. Brahman is that which is to be attained by him who always sees Brahman in action.

Commentary:

The verse unfolds the architecture of karma-yoga with surgical precision. This is not poetic mysticism but operational system design. The fourfold Brahman structure reveals what happens when action detaches from egoic operation and becomes pure function.

Brahmārpaṇam — the instrument of offering, the ladle, the hands, the body itself, recognized as Brahman. Not "my hands" but "the hands as Brahman." The shift from proprietary ownership to instrumental transparency.

Brahma haviḥ — the oblation itself, the materials, the effort, the resources, recognized as Brahman. The grain becomes sacred not by sentiment but by ontological reassignment. Matter seen as what it actually is: Brahman's forms.

Brahmāgnau brahmaṇā hutam — by Brahman (the offerer) offered into the fire of Brahman. The subject, the verb, the object — all Brahman. No external agent remains. Ego evaporates into the operational transparency of pure function.

Brahmaiva tena gantavyam — the destination, the fruit, the end of action: Brahman itself. The offering does not travel elsewhere. It returns to source. The cycle closes perfectly.

The final phrase, brahma-karma-samādhinā, is the technical specification. Through samādhi on Brahman-in-action, the practitioner attains. Not through knowledge alone. Not through devotion alone. Through the samādhi — the settled, equanimous, unwavering — application of Brahman-vision to action.

Today's cosmic alignment intensifies this teaching: Uttara Bhādrapadā is the asterism of Ajaikapāda, the ascetic who dances in fire. The agni of yajña becomes literal as the sun traverses this fire-born constellation. Tri Pushkara Yoga until 2:42 PM creates triple-blessing energy — the three Brahmans (offering, fire, enjoyer) perfectly aligned. The verse is not philosophical abstraction. It is the instruction manual for this exact configuration.

Yoga Sutra Connection:

🔥 YS I.33 — maitrī-karuṇā-muditopekṣāṇāṁ sukha-duḥkha-puṇyāpuṇya-viṣayāṇāṁ bhāvanātaś citta-prasādanam

"By cultivating friendliness toward the happy, compassion toward the suffering, joy toward the virtuous, and equanimity toward the wicked, the mind becomes clear and calm."

This sūtra provides the practical foundation for brahma-karma-samādhi. The attitude of the offerer must be prasanna — settled, clear, undisturbed — before the vision of Brahman in action becomes possible. Without citta-prasādana, Brahman-vision is only intellectual theory. With it, every action becomes transparent to source.

Patañjali's four attitudes are the preparation. BG 3.10 is the execution. First clear the vessel (YS I.33), then pour the offering (BG 3.10).

Sanskrit Terms:

  • brahmārpaṇam — the Brahman-offering; the instrument/agent recognized as Brahman
  • haviḥ — the oblation, sacrifice material; clarified butter, grain, or any offering substance
  • brahmāgnau — in the fire of Brahman; the sacrificial fire as Brahman itself
  • hutam — offered, poured; past passive participle of hu (to sacrifice)
  • gantavyam — to be attained, reached; from gam (to go) + tavya (must/mandatory)
  • brahma-karma-samādhinā — through samādhi on Brahman-in-action; settled absorption
